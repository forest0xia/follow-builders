### Training Data — Google DeepMind's Logan Kilpatrick: Why the Model Eats the Harness

**The Takeaway:** The agent harness — that layer of scaffolding everyone is racing to build around LLMs — is temporary. Within 12 months, the models will have absorbed most of it.

Logan Kilpatrick runs Google AI Studio and the Gemini API, sitting at the intersection of developer experience and frontier model research. His central thesis is that what we call "the model" today is no longer just a set of weights: it's an expanding system — tool calling, code execution, hosted search, containers, agent harnesses — all wrapped around those weights. The pattern is consistent: scaffolding gets built externally first, then the model eats it. "The alpha will be somewhere else now. It won't be in trying to spin your own harness because the model just does it natively."

He's bullish on Antigravity (Google's agent harness and coding ecosystem) as the new through line across Google products, much like Gemini became the model through line before it. At Google IO, Sundar showed internal token consumption growing rapidly — proof the engine is spinning.

On Google's position in coding: Kilpatrick is candid that the narrative has shifted fast ("December, the narrative was that Google had won"), but argues Gemini 3.5 Flash — a flash model that surpassed every prior pro model at coding using post-training gains alone — signals real progress. He credits the Windsurf acquisition and the team behind Antigravity as the internal "Avengers of AI."

He also coins "jagged superintelligence" as his framework for what's coming: not AGI everywhere at once, but domain-by-domain superintelligence — first coding, then perhaps math, science, and finance — wherever verifiability is strong. The broader point lands with quiet force: "It's so easy to get lost in the competitive race of who's pushing a number higher on SWE-bench... It's very easy to lose sight of the reason we're doing that, which is so that we can solve problems that humans actually have."

**Training Data — Google DeepMind 的 Logan Kilpatrick：为何模型会"吞噬"agent harness**

**核心洞见：** 所有人都在争先建设的 agent harness——那层围绕 LLM 的脚手架——是暂时的。12 个月之内，模型将消化其中的大部分。

Logan Kilpatrick 负责 Google AI Studio 和 Gemini API，身处开发者体验与前沿模型研究的交汇点。他的核心论点是：我们今天所说的"模型"已不再只是一组权重，而是一个不断扩张的系统——工具调用、代码执行、托管搜索、容器、agent harness——都是围绕权重构建的外壳。这一模式始终如一：先在外部构建脚手架，然后模型将其内化。"alpha 会转移到别处。靠自建 harness 已无法构建竞争优势，因为模型会直接原生支持。"

他对 Antigravity（Google 的 agent harness / 编程生态系统）持乐观态度，认为它将成为串联 Google 各产品的新主线，就像 Gemini 此前成为模型层主线一样。在 Google IO 上，Sundar 展示了 Google 内部 token 消耗量的快速增长——引擎已全速转动。

关于 Google 在编程领域的位置：Kilpatrick 坦承叙事转变之快令人惊讶（"去年 12 月，外界普遍认为 Google 已经赢了"），但他指出，Gemini 3.5 Flash——一个仅靠 post-training 就超越此前所有 pro 模型的 flash 模型——是真实进步的信号。他将 Windsurf 收购和 Antigravity 背后的团队称为 Google 内部的"复仇者联盟"。

他还提出了"锯齿形超级智能"（jagged superintelligence）这一框架：并非 AGI 同时降临各处，而是逐领域出现超级智能——先是编程，然后可能是数学、科学、金融——凡是可以被清晰验证的领域。更深层的洞见平静而有力："我们太容易迷失在谁的 SWE-bench 分数更高这种竞赛里……很容易忘记我们这么做的原因，其实是为了解决人类真正面临的问题。"
