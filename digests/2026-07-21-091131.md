**Aaron Levie** (CEO of Box)

Levie gave the most substantive thread of the day, spotlighting Cursor's new research on multi-model agentic systems. Key finding: using a frontier model as the planner/orchestrator with a cheaper workhorse model for execution can achieve a 15X cost improvement on large projects. The underlying logic: "Few moments in a large task genuinely require frontier intelligence, such as the original decomposition, the design decisions, and certain trade-offs. Once a frontier planner has collapsed the ambiguity into a detailed, explicit instruction, less expensive models simply have to follow it." Levie argues this will become the core design pattern of complex agents, and that companies who master domain-specific model routing — across coding, finance, legal, healthcare, and life sciences — will be positioned to take on much larger workloads that would otherwise be too expensive.
https://x.com/levie/status/2079402164988895293

Levie 发出了当天最有实质内容的长文，重点介绍了 Cursor 关于多模型 agentic 系统的最新研究。核心发现：以前沿模型作为规划者/协调者，以更廉价的模型负责执行，可以在大型项目中实现 15 倍的成本优化。背后的逻辑是："在一个大型任务中，真正需要前沿智能的时刻其实寥寥无几，比如最初的任务分解、设计决策和某些权衡。一旦前沿规划者将模糊性转化为详尽、明确的指令，成本更低的模型只需照单执行即可。"Levie 认为这将成为复杂 agent 的核心设计模式，而那些掌握领域特定模型路由的公司——横跨编程、金融、法律、医疗和生命科学——将有能力承接原本成本过高、客户无力部署的大规模工作负载。

**No Priors — Travel Through the Lens of AI with Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8nj_0wZkbtA

**The Takeaway:** There is no such thing as a moat — not even Booking.com's $186 billion in annual travel volume will protect you if you stop innovating.

Glenn Fogel joined Priceline in February 2000, the exact week the Nasdaq peaked, and has spent 27 years watching the stock go from $6 to nearly $6,000 — a thousandfold gain over a quarter-century. As CEO of Booking Holdings, which encompasses Booking.com, Priceline, and several other global travel brands, Fogel brings perspective on what actually survives technological disruption.

On AI and travel: Fogel rejects the early narrative that AI would simply replace OTAs. The complexity of travel — regulatory frameworks across dozens of countries, partner relationships spanning 8.6 million alternative accommodation listings, the "dominoes" nature of trip disruptions — creates real barriers that newcomers consistently underestimate. "If you think you're just gonna come in and do this business and knock away these very big players, I'd say you should really understand what the business is before you decide to commit your capital."

That said, Fogel is not complacent. Booking Holdings is investing approximately $700 million this year in AI and platform capabilities. Their agentic travel assistant "Penny" on Priceline has been doubling in adoption monthly, delivering faster search, shorter paths to booking, lower cancellation rates, and higher customer satisfaction. Fogel tested it himself planning a complex family Europe trip — multiple cabin classes, different departure cities, frequent flyer mile optimization across airlines — and called the result "incredible." Customer service cost per contact is down roughly 10%, while satisfaction is up.

On job displacement: Fogel is sober. The translation roles that existed at Booking.com in 2005 — 40 languages, all human — are simply gone now. His concern is not whether AI will displace workers (history says it will) but whether job creation can keep pace with displacement, and whether companies and governments are doing enough to upskill workers who cannot easily retrain. He singles out the 50-year-old truck driver as the paradigm case nobody has solved.

On the AI bubble: Fogel draws direct parallels to the dot-com era but believes the stakes are bigger in every direction. His advice to builders is consistent with his philosophy: "There is no such thing as a moat. The only way to win long term is to continue to develop new services, new ways to do things."

**要点：** 没有什么是真正的护城河——哪怕是 Booking.com 每年 1860 亿美元的旅行交易量，一旦你停止创新，也无法保护你。

**No Priors — 用 AI 的视角看旅游：Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8nj_0wZkbtA

Glenn Fogel 于 2000 年 2 月——也就是纳斯达克触顶的那一周——加入 Priceline，此后用了 27 年时间见证公司股价从 6 美元涨至近 6000 美元，四分之一个世纪实现千倍增长。作为 Booking Holdings 的 CEO，他麾下涵盖 Booking.com、Priceline 及多个全球旅游品牌，对技术颠覆下"什么能存活"有着深刻洞见。

关于 AI 与旅游：Fogel 否定了"AI 将直接取代在线旅游平台"的早期叙事。旅游业的复杂性——跨越数十个国家的监管框架、覆盖 860 万套民宿和另类住宿的合作伙伴关系、行程变故的"多米诺效应"——构成了新入局者持续低估的真实壁垒。"如果你以为自己能轻松杀进来、打倒这些巨头，我建议你在投入资本之前，先真正搞清楚这门生意是怎么回事。"

尽管如此，Fogel 并不自满。Booking Holdings 今年在 AI 和平台能力上投入约 7 亿美元。Priceline 的 agentic 旅行助理"Penny"每月用户增长翻倍，带来了更快的搜索速度、更短的预订路径、更低的取消率和更高的用户满意度。Fogel 亲自测试它来规划一次复杂的欧洲家庭旅行——不同舱位、不同出发城市、跨航空公司里程优化——并称结果"令人惊叹"。每次客服联系的成本已下降约 10%，而满意度同步提升。

关于就业替代：Fogel 态度清醒。Booking.com 在 2005 年拥有的翻译岗位——40 种语言、全部由人工完成——如今早已消失。他的担忧不在于 AI 会不会替代工人（历史已经给出答案），而在于新岗位的创造速度能否跟上替代速度，以及企业和政府是否为难以再培训的工人做了足够的能力提升。他以"50 岁的卡车司机"为典型案例——这是至今无人真正解决的难题。

关于 AI 泡沫：Fogel 认为当前与互联网泡沫时代有直接的相似之处，但认为这次的风险在每个维度上都更大。他对创业者的建议与他的核心哲学一脉相承："没有什么是真正的护城河。长期取胜的唯一方法，是持续开发新服务、找到新的做事方式。"
