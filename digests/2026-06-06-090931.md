**Swyx** — AI Engineer community organizer / Latent Space podcast

Instead of defaulting to "plan mode," Swyx proposes a simpler behavioral hack: always frame your task as a question. When you phrase something as "How do I do X?" rather than "Do X," the model is invited to push back, evaluate the idea, and suggest alternatives — rather than blindly executing what you said versus what you actually meant. His tip: literally just append "?" to the end of your prompt.
https://x.com/swyx/status/2063082950317486133

与其默认使用"计划模式"，Swyx 提出了一个更简单的行为技巧：始终把任务表述为一个问题。当你将"做X"改为"如何做X？"时，模型会被引导去质疑、评估想法并提出替代方案——而不是盲目执行你说的话，而非你真正的意图。他的小窍门：在 prompt 末尾直接加一个"？"就行了。
https://x.com/swyx/status/2063082950317486133

**Peter Yang** — Product @Roblox / AI tutorials for 140K+ readers

Peter Yang shared a 5-step framework for building AI skills that self-improve over time: (1) give the skill context with examples of good output, (2) write a clear trigger description, (3) add evals with pass/fail checks for common errors, (4) add a memory file to capture learnings from past uses, and (5) build a "skill that edits skills" to remove stale instructions and AI slop. He also teased an interview with a self-taught engineer who contributed to Python and Go repos without a CS degree — crediting AI-assisted planning for his productivity.
https://x.com/petergyang/status/2062899832965255443

**Madhu Guru** — ex-Product Lead @Google (Gemini, Veo)

Former Google product leader Madhu Guru offered sharp strategic advice for enterprise AI teams: stop building for today's model capabilities and price points. The right approach is to "build for the slope" — scaffold around current model weaknesses, bet the next generation will solve them natively, then push the frontier again. Over time, the ability to repeatedly identify and bridge model gaps becomes a durable competitive moat.
https://x.com/realmadhuguru/status/2063024953721827329

前谷歌产品负责人 Madhu Guru 为企业AI团队提供了深刻的战略建议：不要基于当前模型的能力和成本构建产品。正确的方法是"为斜率而建"——围绕当前模型的弱点搭建架构，押注下一代模型会原生解决这些问题，然后再次推动前沿。随着时间积累，反复识别并弥补模型差距的能力本身就会成为一种持久的竞争护城河。

**Aaron Levie** — CEO @Box

Box CEO Aaron Levie made a counterintuitive argument about AI and engineering jobs. Coding is the domain where AI has every advantage: massive training data, technically sophisticated users who adopt new tools fast, verifiable outputs (you can test an app), and context that's already digitized in the codebase. And yet, engineers remain in extremely high demand. His conclusion: if AI can't fully replace engineers even under these near-ideal conditions, the displacement risk for other knowledge workers — where most of those advantages disappear — is far lower than the headlines suggest. Agents will multiply what people can do, but they won't make people go away.
https://x.com/levie/status/2063055332545540096

Box CEO Aaron Levie 对AI与工程师岗位提出了一个反直觉的论断。编程是AI具备所有优势的领域：海量训练数据、快速采用新工具的高技术用户、可验证的输出（可以测试应用），以及已数字化存储在代码库中的上下文。然而，工程师的需求依然极为旺盛。他的结论：如果AI在近乎理想的条件下都无法完全取代工程师，那么对其他知识工作者——这些优势大多不存在的领域——的替代风险远比媒体所渲染的要低。Agent 会倍增人们的能力，但不会让人消失。

## PODCASTS

**AI & I by Every — "The SaaS Apocalypse Is a Goldmine With Figma's Matt Colyer"**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYKebKB3-d0

**The Takeaway:** The "SaaS apocalypse" narrative gets the direction exactly backwards — a world with dramatically more software being built is a gold mine for tools, not a death knell.

Every CEO Dan Shipper sits down with Matt Colyer, Director of Product Management for Developers at Figma, to pull apart the SaaSpocalypse thesis. Matt's counterargument is structural: five years ago, there were maybe 25–40 million developers worldwide. He believes that number is heading toward a billion as AI democratizes software creation. More builders means more software, which means more demand for the tools that support them — not less. Figma has leaned into this by shipping both its own agent and a Figma MCP integration, rather than retreating.

The conversation gets more personal from there. Matt built his own email agent two years ago, starting with a rickety Python script that would grab an inbox, paste emails to an LLM, and extract three bullet points from a multipage message. What surprised him: now he buys *more* software than ever, because living through the pain of maintaining your own agents teaches you exactly why managed products are worth paying for. His latest evolution — a daily summary agent that proactively emails him — underscores what he sees as the key unlock for agents: not just answering when asked, but showing up on your behalf.

The unsolved problem both hosts circle around is context-aware filtering. Agents can be told what to surface, but they tend to either under-deliver or overcorrect into firehose mode. Neither host has cracked it. Matt also shares an unexpected productivity trick: using Loom-style voice recordings to talk to AI tools, because narrating to a pretend screen-share audience feels less awkward than talking to your computer.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYKebKB3-d0

**《AI & I》by Every — "SaaS 末日是一座金矿：对话 Figma 的 Matt Colyer"**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYKebKB3-d0

**核心洞察：** "SaaS 末日"的叙事方向完全搞反了——一个有大量软件正在被构建的世界，对工具来说是一座金矿，而非一记丧钟。

Every CEO Dan Shipper 与 Figma 开发者产品管理总监 Matt Colyer 深入拆解了"SaaSpocalypse"论题。Matt 的反驳是结构性的：五年前，全球大约有 2500 万到 4000 万开发者。他认为随着 AI 使软件创作民主化，这个数字将迈向十亿。更多的构建者意味着更多的软件，也就意味着对工具的需求更多，而非更少。Figma 的应对方式是同时推出自研 agent 和 Figma MCP 集成，而不是退缩观望。

对话随后变得更加个人化。Matt 两年前就构建了自己的邮件 agent，起初只是一个摇摇欲坠的 Python 脚本——抓取收件箱、粘贴邮件内容到 LLM、从多页邮件中提炼三条要点。出乎他意料的是：现在他购买的软件比以往任何时候都多，因为亲历维护自己 agent 的痛苦，让他深刻理解了为什么托管产品值得付费。他最新的实践——一个每天主动向他发送摘要邮件的 agent——印证了他对 agent 关键突破口的判断：不只是被动等待提问，而是主动出现在你身边。

两位嘉宾都绕不开一个尚未解决的问题：具备上下文感知的内容过滤。告诉 agent 该呈现什么内容，它往往要么信息不足，要么矫枉过正变成信息轰炸，两人都还没找到答案。Matt 还分享了一个意想不到的生产力技巧：用 Loom 风格的语音录制来与 AI 工具对话——假装在向某人分享屏幕时开口，比直接对着电脑说话感觉自然得多。
