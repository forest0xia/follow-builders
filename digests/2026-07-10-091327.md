**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 90: AI Pioneer Jürgen Schmidhuber on the State of AI Today**

**The Takeaway:** The father of LSTM and meta-learning argues that today's AI is fundamentally capped by its dependence on human-generated data — the real breakthrough will come from systems that generate their own training data through curiosity, not content scraped from the web.

Jürgen Schmidhuber, sometimes called the father of modern AI, has been working toward a single goal since the 1970s: build an AI smarter than himself so he can retire. He's behind LSTM networks, the meta-learning frameworks underpinning today's self-improving systems, and the 2003 Gödel Machine — a mathematically optimal self-modifying AI. He now runs research at KAUST in Saudi Arabia.

His most counterintuitive claim: everything on the World Wide Web — the training data for every large language model — was put there because at least one human found it interesting. That makes today's LLMs "super biased towards humans" in a way that fundamentally limits their potential. The fix is artificial curiosity: "The future of AI is going to lie in systems that, through their own actions, through artificial curiosity, collect all the data that is used to train the world models." Like a baby learning physics by moving its fingers and watching what changes, not by downloading Wikipedia.

On recursive self-improvement as a moat for big AI companies: Schmidhuber is deeply skeptical. "Everybody's cooking with the same water." The key RSI ideas — from his own meta-evolution work in 1987 to the Gödel Machine in 2003 — came from academic labs and grad students, not companies. Any incremental advantage gets commoditized fast.

On the AI investment boom, he does the math and doesn't like it: compute costs drop roughly 10x every five years. Companies investing hundreds of billions in GPUs today are buying assets that will be worth 90% less in 2031. "There's no business model that can recuperate that loss." His prediction: a stock market correction in AI — not civilization-ending, but a "renormalization" of overvalued companies that don't yet have profitable business models.

On AGI: "You can't have AGI just behind the screen." Real general intelligence requires mastering the physical world, and robot hardware still can't touch what evolution built into a human hand. That piece — not the algorithms — is the long pole in the tent.

On AI safety and alignment: he never signed any of the warning letters. The premise that you can give an AI a fixed objective function and call it "aligned" was always naive — genuinely intelligent systems must be able to set their own objectives, like scientists do. The answer is closer to parenting than alignment research.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKjR8DQ40po

**要点：** LSTM 和元学习之父认为，当今 AI 从根本上受限于对人类生成数据的依赖——真正的突破将来自能够通过好奇心自主生成训练数据的系统，而非依赖从网络抓取的内容。

被誉为现代 AI 之父的 Jürgen Schmidhuber 自 20 世纪 70 年代起就在追求同一个目标：构建一个比自己更聪明的 AI，这样他就可以退休了。他是 LSTM 网络、当今自我改进系统所依赖的元学习框架，以及 2003 年哥德尔机（数学上最优的自修改 AI）的发明者，目前在沙特阿拉伯阿卜杜拉国王科技大学（KAUST）主持研究工作。

他最反直觉的观点是：互联网上的所有内容——即每个大型语言模型的训练数据——之所以存在，是因为至少有一个人觉得它有趣。这使得今天的 LLM "严重偏向人类"，从根本上限制了其潜力。解决方案是人工好奇心："AI 的未来在于这样的系统——通过自身的行动、通过人工好奇心，收集用于训练世界模型的所有数据。"就像婴儿通过移动手指、观察变化来学习物理，而不是下载维基百科。

关于递归自我改进对大型 AI 公司的护城河价值：Schmidhuber 深感怀疑。"大家用的都是同一锅水。"关键的 RSI 想法——从他 1987 年的元进化工作到 2003 年的哥德尔机——都来自学术实验室和研究生，而非大公司。任何微小的先发优势都会迅速被商品化。

关于 AI 投资热潮，他算了一笔账，结论不乐观：计算成本大约每五年下降 10 倍。今天投入数千亿购买 GPU 的公司，买的是五年后价值缩水 90% 的资产。"没有任何商业模式能弥补这一损失。"他的预测：AI 领域将出现股市修正——不是文明终结，而是对那些尚无盈利商业模式的过度估值公司的"回归正常"。

关于 AGI：他认为"不能在屏幕背后拥有 AGI"。真正的通用智能需要掌握物理世界，而机器人硬件仍然远不及进化赋予人类手部的能力。这一点——而非算法——才是真正的瓶颈。

关于 AI 安全与对齐：他从未签署过任何警告信。认为给 AI 一个固定目标函数就叫"对齐"的前提从一开始就很幼稚——真正智能的系统必须能够设定自己的目标，就像科学家那样。答案更接近于养育，而非对齐研究。
