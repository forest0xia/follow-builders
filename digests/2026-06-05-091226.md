### Anthropic Engineering

**An update on recent Claude Code quality reports**

Anthropic published a detailed postmortem on three separate bugs that caused Claude Code quality degradation since March, affecting Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.6, and Opus 4.7:

1. On March 4, default reasoning effort was quietly lowered from high to medium to reduce latency — users noticed Claude felt less intelligent. Reverted April 7.
2. On March 26, a caching optimization had a bug that continuously dropped prior reasoning on every subsequent turn (not just once after an idle session), causing forgetfulness, repetition, and strange tool choices. Also drove faster-than-expected usage limit drain due to cache misses. Fixed April 10.
3. On April 16, a system prompt change added extreme verbosity limits (≤25 words between tool calls, ≤100 words for final responses) that hurt coding quality. Reverted April 20.

Because each bug hit a different slice of traffic on a different schedule, the aggregate effect looked like broad, inconsistent degradation — making it hard to diagnose. Going forward: broader ablation evals for every prompt change, stricter controls on system prompt modifications, gradual rollouts, and longer soak periods for intelligence-affecting changes. Usage limits are being reset for all subscribers.

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem

Anthropic 发布了一篇详细的事故复盘，分析了自三月以来导致 Claude Code 质量下降的三个独立 bug，影响范围涵盖 Sonnet 4.6、Opus 4.6 和 Opus 4.7：

1. 3 月 4 日，默认推理强度从 high 悄悄降至 medium 以降低延迟，用户感觉 Claude 变笨了，4 月 7 日回滚。
2. 3 月 26 日，一个 cache 优化存在 bug，导致每次对话轮次都会丢弃历史推理（而非只在 session 超时后执行一次），引发遗忘、重复和奇怪的工具调用。由于 cache miss 频发，也导致用量额度消耗加速。4 月 10 日修复。
3. 4 月 16 日，一处 system prompt 更改加入了极端的字数限制（工具调用间不超过 25 词，最终回复不超过 100 词），损害了编码质量。4 月 20 日回滚。

由于三个 bug 各自影响不同流量、发生在不同时间，叠加效果看起来像大面积的不稳定退化，诊断难度极高。后续改进措施包括：每次 prompt 变更都进行更广泛的消融测试、更严格的 system prompt 修改管控、渐进式发布，以及影响智能水平的变更须有更长的观察期。所有订阅用户的用量额度将全部重置。

**Scaling Managed Agents: Decoupling the brain from the hands**

Anthropic published the technical architecture behind Managed Agents, their hosted long-horizon agent service. The core insight: by decoupling "the brain" (Claude and its harness) from "the hands" (sandboxes and tools) and the session log, each component can fail or be replaced independently — turning everything from pets into cattle.

The architectural shift yielded dramatic performance gains: p50 time-to-first-token dropped ~60% and p95 dropped over 90%, since containers are now provisioned only when needed. Security improved because credentials never enter the sandbox where generated code runs. The session log lives outside the context window, enabling recoverable long-horizon state without irreversible compaction decisions. The design philosophy mirrors operating systems: build abstractions general enough for "programs as yet unthought of."

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/managed-agents

Anthropic 发布了 Managed Agents（其托管的长周期 agent 服务）背后的技术架构。核心洞察：将"大脑"（Claude 及其 harness）与"双手"（沙箱和工具）以及 session 日志解耦，使每个组件都可以独立失败或替换，从而将所有组件从"宠物"变成"牲口"。

这一架构转变带来了显著的性能提升：p50 首 token 时延下降约 60%，p95 下降超过 90%，因为容器现在只在需要时才按需创建。安全性也得到改善，因为 credentials 永远不会进入运行生成代码的沙箱。session 日志存储在 context window 之外，支持可恢复的长周期状态，无需做出不可逆的压缩决策。设计哲学与操作系统一脉相承：构建足够通用的抽象，以应对"尚未出现的程序"。

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/managed-agents

### The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck

**OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning is no longer the cherry on top of the AI cake — it's the cake itself. The best proof is an AI that spent hours being deliberately, stubbornly wrong before finding a breakthrough result no human would have thought to look for.

Dan Roberts leads the Foundations of Reinforcement Learning team at OpenAI and came to AI from a PhD in theoretical physics at MIT, where he studied quantum gravity and quantum chaos. He argues that AI's recent breakthrough on the Erdős unit distance conjecture wasn't just a computational victory — it was a genuine act of contrarian scientific thinking. The model assumed the conjecture was *false* when virtually everyone believed it was true, then persisted through hours of reasoning across an obscure branch of algebraic number theory to find the counterexample. "When you go against the grain and do something contrarian like that, you really have to have strong conviction in what you're doing in order to persevere down a really long calculation path."

Roberts draws a sharp technical contrast between OpenAI's approach (informal reasoning in natural language, check it yourself afterward) and DeepMind's approach (formal proofs in the Lean theorem prover, automatically verified). He argues that language itself is the essential grounding layer for intelligence — the entire sum of human knowledge lives on the internet in language, so a model pre-trained on language has a uniquely rich prior to reason from. RL then teaches that model to use test-time compute: generate tokens as a running scratchpad, reuse weights over arbitrarily long thought chains, and turn raw compute into reasoning.

On scaling, Roberts pushes back against "scale is all you need." Good ideas are essential too — training RL on top of pre-training was itself one such idea, and it's why models today are dramatically more capable. He's also skeptical of "emergence": when something surprising happens at scale that didn't happen before, the right move is to build a simpler model that reproduces it — then you understand it. That's the physics mindset applied to AI.
