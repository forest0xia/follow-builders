**AI & I by Every — "How a Writer Uses AI Without Losing His Voice"**

**The Takeaway:** Protecting your creative depth in the AI era isn't about avoiding the technology — it's about building deliberate barriers between yourself and the network.

Greg, a writer and technologist with a membership community, has thought harder than most about what it means to use AI without losing what makes your work distinctly yours. His morning ritual is strict: he doesn't touch his phone or go online until well after lunch. "As soon as I touch my phone, I feel the chemical shift and I can't go into any kind of deep thinking place." He keeps a dedicated MacBook with internet access blocked — not because he's anti-technology (he rebuilt his own newsletter software, membership platform, and an ephemeral social network for his community), but because the enemy isn't the tool, it's the network.

On AI as a building platform: Greg dropped his $67,000/year Campaign Monitor bill down to roughly $150 by rebuilding his newsletter infrastructure with Claude and Amazon SES. He built "The Good Place" — an ephemeral social platform for members where posts disappear after a week, there's no algorithm, you can only post twice a day, and photos start in one-bit black and white until clicked. His take on the broader moment: we're entering a golden age of n-of-one software, where individuals can build tools precisely tailored to how they think and work, and the competitive pressure on bloated SaaS incumbents will finally force innovation.

His contrarian heresy: the entire internet should be deleted every two weeks. Ephemerality, he argues, has value in the digital world that we've systematically ignored.

He's enthusiastic about AI coding tools but deliberately unplugged when it comes to creative work — not because one contaminates the other, but because the dopamine dynamics of AI-assisted building and the deep attention required for serious writing pull in opposite directions, and protecting the latter requires active infrastructure, not just willpower.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7ND0lQmLJlA

**AI & I by Every — "How a Writer Uses AI Without Losing His Voice"**

**核心洞察：** 在 AI 时代保护创意深度，不是回避技术本身，而是在自己与"网络"之间建立刻意的屏障。

Greg 是一位拥有会员社区的作家和技术人，他对"如何使用 AI 而不失去作品独特性"这个问题的思考，比大多数人都深入。他的晨间仪式极为严格：午饭后很久才触碰手机和网络。"一旦拿起手机，我就能感受到那种化学变化，我再也无法进入任何深度思考或深度专注的状态。"他特意准备了一台屏蔽了互联网的专用 MacBook——不是因为他反对技术（他用 Claude 重建了自己的 newsletter 系统、会员平台和一个为会员打造的"消逝型"社交网络），而是因为真正的敌人不是工具，是网络。

关于用 AI 构建工具：Greg 把每年 6.7 万美元的 Campaign Monitor 账单，用 Claude 和 Amazon SES 重建后压缩到了大约 150 美元。他还打造了"The Good Place"——一个为会员设计的消逝型社交平台：帖子一周后自动消失，没有算法，每天只能发两条内容，照片默认黑白单色，点击后才渐变为彩色。关于这个时代的更大判断：我们正在进入 n of one 软件的黄金时代，个体可以构建完全适配自己思维方式的工具，而这种竞争压力也终将倒逼那些臃肿的 SaaS 巨头创新。

他的反直觉"异端"观点：整个互联网应该每两周删除一次。他认为，数字世界的短暂性拥有一种被我们系统性忽视的价值。

他对 AI 编程工具充满热情，但在创意写作时选择主动断网——不是因为两者互相污染，而是因为 AI 辅助构建带来的多巴胺驱动模式，与严肃写作所需的深度专注，天然地拉向两个方向。保护后者需要主动构建基础设施，光靠意志力远远不够。
