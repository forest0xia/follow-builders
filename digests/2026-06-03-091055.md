**Training Data — "Knowing What Your Customers Want, All the Time: Listen Labs' Alfred Wahlforss"**

**The Takeaway:** As AI makes building faster than ever, the real bottleneck shifts to knowing *what* to build — and Listen Labs is betting that AI-powered voice interviews at scale are the answer.

Alfred Wahlforss, founder and CEO of Listen Labs, joined the Training Data podcast to explain why the hardest part of the AI-accelerated future isn't writing code, it's understanding customers. Listen Labs runs an AI agent that conducts thousands of simultaneous voice interviews via what feels like a Zoom call — complete with emotion detection — drawing from a panel of 30 million participants. The platform already serves 20% of the Fortune 500, including Microsoft, Anthropic, Sweetgreen, NBC, and others.

What makes this more than a survey tool? A few counterintuitive insights. First, people are actually *more honest* talking to an AI than a human interviewer — partly because there's no social pressure, and partly because it's asynchronous, so participants can engage on their own time. Second, the platform builds persistent profiles across interviews, meaning when Nike wants sneakerheads for a launch study, Listen can surface exactly the right 1,000 people rather than blasting a list with a 10% incidence rate. Third, emotion detection during video interviews adds a behavioral signal that traditional surveys can't capture — and correlates directly with ad performance on Meta and LinkedIn.

The most ambitious move coming next: simulation. After running tens of thousands of interviews, Listen can now model individual users well enough to predict how they'd respond to new questions. In a live test, the simulation correctly picked the better-performing conference talk title while ChatGPT picked the wrong one — because, as Wahlforss explains, "the models are trained on the average person. You want to build for a very specific niche." The end vision is a "human API" — a layer that coding agents can call to understand user preferences in real time, enabling what he calls the one-person billion-dollar company.

**Training Data — "Knowing What Your Customers Want, All the Time: Listen Labs' Alfred Wahlforss"**

**核心洞见：** 当AI让构建速度前所未有地快，真正的瓶颈转向了"知道构建什么"——而Listen Labs正押注于大规模AI语音访谈就是答案。

Listen Labs创始人兼CEO Alfred Wahlforss 登上Training Data播客，阐释了为什么在AI加速的未来，最难的不是写代码，而是理解用户。Listen Labs的AI agent通过类似Zoom通话的形式（内置情绪检测）同时开展数千场语音访谈，受访者来自其拥有的3000万人受访者池。该平台已服务于20%的财富500强，包括Microsoft、Anthropic、Sweetgreen、NBC等知名品牌。

为什么不只是一个调查工具？几个反直觉的洞见：第一，人们对AI面试官实际上更坦诚——部分原因是没有社交压力，部分原因是异步方式让受访者可以按自己的节奏参与。第二，平台会跨访谈建立持久的用户画像——当Nike需要在新品发布研究中找到球鞋文化爱好者时，Listen可以精准推送最合适的1000人，而不是对着一份名单广撒网（且成功率仅10%）。第三，视频访谈中的情绪检测提供了传统调查无法捕捉的行为信号，且与Meta和LinkedIn上的广告效果直接相关。

最令人期待的下一步：模拟预测。经过数万次访谈积累后，Listen已能对单个用户建模，预测他们对新问题的回答。在一次实测中，Listen的模拟成功选出了表现更好的演讲题目，而ChatGPT选错了——因为正如Wahlforss所说："通用模型是用'普通人'训练的，但你要构建的是针对特定细分人群的产品。"他的终极愿景是打造"人类API"——一个coding agent可以实时调用的层，用来了解用户偏好，从而实现他所说的"一人十亿美元公司"。
