**Former Google Gemini PM Madhu Guru** maps out how enterprise AI model routing has evolved through three distinct phases. Phase 1 (2024): everyone defaulted to the "it" model — GPT regardless of task, because it was the shiny new thing. Phase 2 (early 2025): teams over-corrected, hunting for the smallest/cheapest model but lacking evals sophisticated enough to map tasks to models — they burned cycles and shipped slower. Phase 3 (now): the most sophisticated AI-native startups have succeeded in breaking their product into sub-agents and routing each task to the right model — hardest reasoning to Claude, simplest to Gemini Flash-Lite or open-weight models. Enterprises typically follow AI-native builders 6–9 months later.
https://x.com/realmadhuguru/status/2063342268472574268

前 Google Gemini 产品负责人 Madhu Guru 总结了企业 AI model routing 演进的三个阶段。第一阶段（2024年）：无论任务如何，所有人默认使用"当红"模型 GPT，因为它是最闪亮的新玩具。第二阶段（2025年初）：团队矫枉过正，拼命追求最小最便宜的模型，但 eval 体系不够成熟，无法准确匹配任务与模型——反而烧掉了大量时间，拖慢了交付。第三阶段（当下）：最顶尖的 AI-native 创业公司已成功将产品拆分为多个 sub-agent，并将每项任务路由到最合适的模型——最难的推理交给 Claude，最简单的交给 Gemini Flash-Lite 或开源模型。企业客户通常在 AI-native 建设者之后 6–9 个月跟进。

**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 89: AI Research Legend's Honest Assessment of Where We Are**

**The Takeaway:** Transformers with RL and agents have delivered jaw-dropping productivity gains — but there's a persistent, well-founded feeling among top researchers that the current approach isn't truly generalizing the way human intelligence does, and that something else may be needed to unlock the next level.

Lukasz Kaiser — co-author of the seminal "Attention Is All You Need" transformer paper, formerly of Google Brain and OpenAI — gives a candid and specific assessment of where AI actually stands. On the fundamental question of whether transformers can fully generalize: "LLMs — they will learn the concept. They will learn it. But after exhausting all other options. You need this trillion tokens." Humans acquire concepts from vastly less data; something else seems to be happening in human learning that current architectures don't capture.

On coding agents as a productivity revolution: Kaiser estimates 5–10x gains on research tasks, now runs experiments in parallel he'd previously do sequentially, and has essentially stopped reading code at the function level — trusting agents to implement while maintaining full ML-level control of what's actually running. His diagnosis: agents don't make you less sharp — they force you to stay sharper at the architectural level, precisely because you can't afford to miss when the agent adds a spurious auxiliary loss.

On why Anthropic nailed coding first: "Anthropic made this very good decision to focus on coding. OpenAI was like, we're doing ChatGPT... partway Anthropic made this decision was that they just could not compete." The lesson: committing to the right non-dominant bet compounds faster than defending the current market position.

On post-transformer architectures: Kaiser sees genuine early results from small models that incorporate recurrence (TRM, HRM) performing well on tasks like Sudoku and ARC. The open question is whether these ideas will scale to LLM-level applications. He frames the current moment as an ecosystem with enough labs trying wild bets that some will hit — a healthy sign.

On the open vs. closed source gap: distillation limits it, but big models distill imperfectly. Sovereign model demand from governments and enterprises will sustain a robust open ecosystem. Neither side wins cleanly.

**结语 / Podcast Summary**

Unsupervised Learning 第 89 集：一位 AI 研究传奇的坦诚评估

**核心结论：** transformer + RL + agent 的组合已经带来了令人瞠目的生产力飞跃——但顶尖研究者中存在一种持续且有据可查的感受：当前方法的泛化方式并不像人类智能那样，还需要某些不同的东西才能解锁下一个层级。

Lukasz Kaiser——开创性论文"Attention Is All You Need"的联合作者，曾就职于 Google Brain 和 OpenAI——对 AI 的真实现状给出了坦诚而具体的评估。关于 transformer 是否能真正泛化这一根本问题，他说："LLM 会学会这个概念，它一定会学会。但需要穷尽所有其他选项之后。你需要万亿个 token。"人类从少得多的数据中就能习得概念；人类学习中似乎存在着当前架构尚未捕捉到的某种机制。

关于 coding agent 带来的生产力革命：Kaiser 估计研究任务效率提升了 5–10 倍，现在能并行运行以前只能串行完成的多个实验，基本上已经不再阅读函数级别的代码——信任 agent 来实现，同时在 ML 层面保持对实际运行内容的完整掌控。他的判断是：agent 不会让你变迟钝——恰恰相反，它迫使你在架构层面保持更高的清醒度，因为你一旦没发现 agent 随意添加了一个多余的辅助 loss，后果不堪设想。

关于 Anthropic 为何率先在 coding 上取得突破："Anthropic 做了一个非常正确的决定，专注于 coding。OpenAI 在做 ChatGPT……而 Anthropic 做出这个决定，部分原因是他们意识到自己无法在 chat 赛道竞争。" 教训是：押注正确的非主流方向，复利速度远快于守住当前市场地位。

关于 post-transformer 架构：Kaiser 看到了一些早期的真实结果——引入了循环结构的小型模型（TRM、HRM）在 Sudoku 和 ARC 等任务上表现优异。这些想法能否扩展到 LLM 量级的应用，仍是一个开放性问题。他将当下时刻描述为一个有足够多实验室在押注激进想法的生态——这是健康的信号。

关于开源与闭源的差距：蒸馏缩小了这一差距，但大型模型的蒸馏并不完美。政府和企业对主权模型的需求将维持一个强健的开源生态。双方都不会完全赢得这场博弈。
