**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — "Stripe's AI Chief: How AI Agents Will Buy, Sell, and Pay"**

**The Takeaway:** Token theft — not payment fraud — is the existential risk quietly draining AI company margins, and more than one in six signups at AI companies are already this kind of abuse.

Emily Sense, head of data and AI at Stripe, joined Matt Turck to map the economic infrastructure being built for agentic commerce. The conversation is a rare look at what the AI economy looks like from the payments layer: who's buying, who's selling, how trust gets established, and what breaks when agents start spending at machine speed.

Stripe sees agentic commerce as a spectrum from L1 (human decides, AI executes checkout) to L5 (fully autonomous agent-to-agent transactions). Most consumer interactions today sit at L2-L3 — AI helps you find the product, maybe auto-fills checkout, but you're still choosing the thing. True agent-to-agent commerce is coming, but isn't the current reality.

The infrastructure Stripe has been quietly shipping: the Agentic Commerce Protocol (co-built with OpenAI, now adopted by Wix, Shopify, BigCommerce, Best Buy, Coach, and dozens more), the shared payment token (a new payment primitive where agents never see underlying credentials), and Link Wallet for agents (Stripe's consumer wallet, now extendable to agents with programmable spending controls and budget caps).

On token theft, Sense was direct: "Fraudsters have figured out that in AI, you actually don't really need to steal money or credentials. You can just steal tokens." Three attack patterns are prevalent: multi-account abuse to harvest free trial credits, free trial cards spun up with no intent to convert, and usage-based billing dine-and-dash. The dine-and-dash for tokens. Unlike SaaS fraud, token theft hits margins hard because every stolen inference has a real marginal cost. Stripe's Radar fraud detection has moved from transaction-level to full customer lifecycle to address this.

Macroeconomically, Stripe's data is remarkable: new business formations have doubled since a year ago, Atlas startups from the 2026 cohort are tracking to 5x the revenue of the prior year's class at the same point, and 5 million Americans are now solopreneurs — people whose entire income comes from running a one-person company, many clearing over $100k. Agents running entire small businesses end-to-end is Sense's 12-month prediction: "It's not Emily permissioning an agent to buy on her behalf. It's Emily has an agent who's tasked with running a business, and that includes buying some things and selling some things and making some profits."

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — "Stripe's AI Chief: How AI Agents Will Buy, Sell, and Pay"**

**核心洞察：** Token 盗窃——而非支付欺诈——正在悄然侵蚀 AI 公司的利润率，目前 AI 公司超过六分之一的新注册账号都属于此类滥用。

Stripe 数据与 AI 负责人 Emily Sense 与 Matt Turck 深度探讨了 AI agent 商业生态的基础设施建设——从支付层视角审视 AI 经济：谁在买、谁在卖、信任如何建立，以及当 agent 以机器速度消费时，什么会出问题。

Stripe 将 agent 商业定义为一个从 L1 到 L5 的自主性谱系：L1 是人类决策、AI 代为结账；L5 是完全自主的 agent 对 agent 交易。当前消费者大多处于 L2-L3 阶段——AI 帮你找产品、可能自动填写结账信息，但最终选择权仍在人手中。真正的 agent 间自主交易正在到来，但尚未成为现实。

Stripe 正在低调落地的基础设施包括：与 OpenAI 联合构建的 Agentic Commerce Protocol（已被 Wix、Shopify、BigCommerce、Best Buy、Coach 等数十家平台采用）、共享支付 token（一种 agent 永远看不到底层凭证的新支付原语），以及 Link 钱包的 agent 版（Stripe 消费者钱包的延伸，支持可编程消费控制和预算上限设定）。

在 token 盗窃问题上，Sense 直言不讳："欺诈者已经发现，在 AI 领域，你根本不需要偷钱或凭证，只需要偷 token。"目前最常见的三种攻击模式：多账号滥用以薅免费试用积分、开通免费试用后从不打算付费转化、以及基于用量计费模式下的"吃完就跑"。与 SaaS 欺诈不同，token 盗窃会直接打击利润率，因为每一次被盗的推理调用都有真实的边际成本。Stripe 的 Radar 欺诈检测已从单笔交易级别扩展到完整客户生命周期。

在宏观经济层面，Stripe 的数据令人印象深刻：新企业注册数量较一年前翻倍，2026 年 Atlas 加速器队列的初创公司收入进度是上届同期的 5 倍，美国目前有 500 万人靠经营独立公司为生——其中数十万人年收入超过百万美元。Sense 对未来 12 个月的预测是：agent 将端到端地运营小型企业。"不是 Emily 授权 agent 代她购物，而是 Emily 有一个被委托经营业务的 agent，这其中包括买一些东西、卖一些东西、赚一些利润。"
