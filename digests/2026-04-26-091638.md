**No Priors — "Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott"**

**The Takeaway:** The enterprise companies most vulnerable to AI disruption are single-function departmental tools — not deep platforms — because their switching costs are low and their moats are shallow.

Bill McDermott runs a company processing 85 billion workflows and 7 trillion transactions. He built his instincts selling deli sandwiches at 16, and that experience crystallized a maxim he hasn't let go of: "The customer and the customer alone determines whether you win or lose."

On the "SaaSPocalypse" — the thesis that LLMs will eat enterprise software — McDermott is skeptical but not dismissive. His calculation: for a simple application on ServiceNow, replicating it with a language model would be 10x more expensive once you account for rebuild costs, GPU factory overhead, and token consumption. And there's a deeper issue: "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake." Enterprise customers demand accountability, not a probabilistic model.

His framework for the AI moment: "AI thinks, workflow acts." Language models generate recommendations instantly. Closing the case — routing it through HR, Finance, Legal, Compliance, across multiple databases — requires a workflow platform. Both are necessary; conflating them is where most enterprises go wrong.

On talent and headcount: McDermott predicts net new hiring will dramatically slow as agents absorb scaling work. He estimates 2.2 billion agents entering the workforce in the next few years. But human judgment — relationship management, trust-building, critical decisions — remains irreplaceable. The bar for human contribution has simply risen: "If an agent can do it as good or better, that's an easy economic decision."

His read on global AI adoption: most companies are still in experiment mode. Only 11% of the Brazilian companies he met with recently have moved past experiments into deployment. Financial services is moving fastest; public sector is still trying to get departments talking to each other.

**No Priors — "AI 时代的全球组织规模化：与 ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott 对话"**

**核心洞见：** 在 AI 冲击面前最脆弱的企业软件，不是深度平台，而是单一职能的部门级工具——因为它们的迁移成本低、护城河浅。

Bill McDermott 领导着一家每天处理 850 亿条 workflow、7 万亿笔交易的企业。他的直觉源于 16 岁开熟食店的经历，那段经历让他刻下了一条至今未曾放弃的信条："客户，也只有客户，决定你的输赢。"

关于"SaaSPocalypse"（即 LLM 将吞噬企业软件的观点），McDermott 的态度是审慎的怀疑。他的计算是：对于 ServiceNow 上的一个简单应用，若想用语言模型复制，一旦算上重建成本、GPU 工厂开销和 token 消耗，代价将高出 10 倍。更深层的问题是："做生意的人能接受人犯错，但绝不会原谅软件犯错。"企业客户要的是可问责性，而不是概率性正确。

他对 AI 时代的框架描述：" AI 负责思考，workflow 负责行动。"语言模型瞬间给出建议，但真正解决问题——将案件流转到 HR、财务、法务、合规，穿越多个数据库——是 workflow 平台的工作。两者都不可或缺，把它们混为一谈是大多数企业的误区所在。

在人才与编制方面，McDermott 预计随着 agent 承担越来越多的规模化工作，净新增招聘将大幅放缓。他估计未来几年将有 22 亿个 agent 进入"劳动力"市场。但人类判断——关系管理、建立信任、关键决策——仍然不可替代。人类贡献的门槛只是提高了："如果 agent 能做得一样好甚至更好，这就是一道轻松的经济学算术题。"

他对全球 AI 采纳的判断：大多数企业仍处于实验阶段。他最近接触的巴西企业中，只有 11% 真正走出实验、进入部署。金融服务行业走得最快；公共部门还在努力让不同部门之间互通信息。
