**Aaron Levie** (Box CEO)

Box CEO Aaron Levie zeroed in on what he sees as the defining bottleneck for enterprise AI agents: context. Moving from agentic coding (where the codebase itself is the context) to general knowledge-work agents exposes a much deeper problem. Enterprise knowledge is fragmented across legacy systems, siloed behind ill-fitting access controls, and often not digitized at all — living instead in people's heads as tribal knowledge. **The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — "OpenAI's Yann Dubois: Why AI Progress Suddenly Feels Real"**

AI progress has felt like a step function lately — but Yann Dubois, who co-leads the Post-Training Frontiers team at OpenAI and helped build GPT-5.5, o3, and GPT-5 thinking, argues it's actually been continuous all along. The step function illusion comes from a reliability threshold: once models cross it, you can genuinely trust them to do real work. Dubois believes that threshold was crossed around December 2025.

The deeper insight is about where reinforcement learning has been redirected. For most of 2025, RL was honed on "verifiable rewards" — math competitions, coding challenges, anything with a clear right/wrong answer. That was necessary to get the technique working. But in late 2025 and early 2026, OpenAI began applying those same RL tools to messy, real-world utility: tasks where "correct" is hard to define, but productivity is easy to feel. The shift from competition benchmarks to genuine user usefulness is exactly why 5.5 feels so different in practice.

On efficiency, Dubois offers a clean analogy: an expert doesn't waste time exploring ten possible directions — they've internalized which paths are likely to be correct. GPT-5.5 is trained to reason that way. The efficiency gains aren't about doing less work, they're about doing the right work faster. Meanwhile, the Pro (extended thinking) model extends this curve outward for tasks where you genuinely want to maximize correctness and don't care about latency — think a mathematician who sets a query running overnight.

His most refreshing point: "We need to reach this level of reliability to really make any of these AI tools very useful, and I think we just crossed that probably December last year, at least at OpenAI. Now we can trust these models to do a lot of the work that we are doing."

Dubois' background is worth noting — he came from NLP research on low-resource languages at Grab (Khmer, Thai, Bahasa), did his PhD at Stanford, and co-authored Stanford Alpaca, which sparked much of the modern post-training research community.

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — "OpenAI's Yann Dubois: Why AI Progress Suddenly Feels Real"**

AI 进展最近感觉像是一个阶跃函数，但 OpenAI Post-Training Frontiers 团队的联合负责人 Yann Dubois（参与构建了 GPT-5.5、o3 和 GPT-5 thinking）认为，进展其实一直是连续的。所谓阶跃函数的错觉，来自于可靠性门槛：一旦模型跨越这个门槛，你就真的可以信任它们完成真实工作。Dubois 认为这个门槛大约在 2025 年 12 月被跨越。

更深层的洞见在于强化学习的方向转变。2025 年的大部分时间里，RL 一直在"可验证奖励"任务上打磨 -- 数学竞赛、编程挑战，任何有明确对错答案的事情。这是让技术跑通的必要阶段。但在 2025 年底到 2026 年初，OpenAI 开始将这些 RL 工具应用于混乱的真实世界场景：那些"正确"难以定义，但生产力提升有目共睹的任务。从竞赛 benchmark 转向真正的用户价值，正是 GPT-5.5 在实际使用中感觉如此不同的原因。

在效率方面，Dubois 给出了一个简洁的类比：专家不会浪费时间探索十个可能的方向，他们已经内化了哪些路径更可能正确。GPT-5.5 就是被训练成这样推理的。效率提升不是做更少的工作，而是更快地做正确的工作。与此同时，Pro（扩展思考）模型则将这条曲线向外延伸，专为那些真正需要最大化正确率、不在乎延迟的任务而生 -- 比如一位数学家设置一个过夜运行的查询任务。

他最直白的那句话值得记住："我们需要达到这种可靠性水平，才能让这些 AI 工具真正有用。我认为我们大约在去年 12 月跨越了那个门槛，至少在 OpenAI 内部是这样。现在我们可以信任这些模型承担我们正在做的大量工作了。"

Dubois 的背景值得一提 -- 他曾在 Grab 从事低资源语言 NLP 研究（高棉语、泰语、印尼语），后在斯坦福完成博士学位，并共同撰写了 Stanford Alpaca，由此点燃了现代 post-training 研究社区的火花。
