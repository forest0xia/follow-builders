**Claude Blog**

Anthropic's Claude team launched three new capabilities for Claude Managed Agents. *Dreaming* is a scheduled process that reviews past agent sessions and memory stores, extracts recurring patterns, and curates what's useful — so agents improve over time, between sessions, not just within them. Developers can choose how much control they retain: dreaming can update memory automatically, or surface changes for review before they land. *Outcomes* lets developers define a rubric for what success looks like; a separate grader evaluates the agent's output in its own context window (so it can't be influenced by the agent's own reasoning), then the agent takes another pass if the bar isn't met. In testing, outcomes improved task success by up to 10 percentage points, with the largest gains on the hardest problems — and boosted file generation quality by 8.4% for .docx and 10.1% for .pptx. Multiagent orchestration and webhooks are also now available to all developers, letting a lead agent break complex work into parallel subagent tasks with full traceability in the Claude Console. Early adopters include Harvey (legal drafting), Netflix (build log analysis across hundreds of services), and Spiral by Every (multiagent writing with quality enforcement).
https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents

Anthropic 的 Claude 团队为 Claude Managed Agents 推出了三项新能力。*Dreaming*（做梦）是一个定期运行的流程，它会回顾历史 agent 会话和记忆库，提取规律性模式，并对有价值的内容进行精炼，让 agent 在会话间隙也能持续自我改进。开发者可以自主决定介入程度：dreaming 既可以自动更新记忆，也可以先提交变更供人工审核。*Outcomes*（结果导向）让开发者写下衡量成功的标准，由独立的评分器在自己的上下文窗口中评估 agent 输出（避免被 agent 自身的推理过程影响），如果未达标，agent 会重新作答。测试显示，outcomes 功能使任务成功率最多提升了 10 个百分点，在最难的任务上提升最为显著；文件生成质量也有改善，.docx 提升 8.4%，.pptx 提升 10.1%。多 agent 编排和 webhook 也已向所有开发者开放，允许主 agent 将复杂工作拆解为并行子 agent 任务，并在 Claude Console 中全程可追溯。早期用户包括 Harvey（法律长文起草）、Netflix（跨数百个服务的构建日志分析）和 Every 旗下的 Spiral（带质量约束的多 agent 写作）。

**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 87: Gemini Co-Lead on World Models, RL's Next Domains & Continual Learning**

**The Takeaway:** The next frontier of AI isn't a new capability vertical — it's the meta-capability to learn and adapt, and Oriol Vinyals believes the mechanism that gets us there will look less like weight updates and more like a well-organized file system.

Oriol Vinyals is co-lead of Gemini at Google alongside Noam Shazir and Jeff Dean, and one of the researchers most directly responsible for deep learning breakthroughs over the last decade. Recorded a day after Google IO, the conversation covers world models, the future of reinforcement learning beyond code and math, and why continual learning may be the next paradigm shift on the order of reasoning models.

On world models: Vinyals draws a line between two definitions. The popular version — video generation you can steer with language, as seen in Omni — is genuinely impressive. But the deeper research ambition is extracting knowledge from video and images the same way we extracted knowledge from text. "That pure transfer is, I think, one of the core quests of machine learning for the last decade plus." His honest take: the "GPT moment" for visual data hasn't arrived yet.

On RL's next domains: What excites him most isn't a new vertical to RL on — it's what he calls "meta capabilities." Things like: can a model learn from novel instructions it's never seen? Can it adapt its behavior as it plays a new game? He tests models by giving them the instruction manual for an unfamiliar game and watching whether they can both understand the rules and improve as they play. Current models struggle when games get sufficiently out-of-distribution. "The ability to learn and adapt rather than the ability to be a professional player or IMO gold medalist is what I think fascinates me most when I look at new releases."

On memory and continual learning: Vinyals is bullish on file-system-style external memory — agents writing structured knowledge to files between sessions rather than integrating learning back into weights. The practical reason is scale: serving one model to millions of users makes per-user weight updates infeasible. The non-parametric approach (a persistent, readable, writable knowledge base) is already powerful and he expects it to keep improving. He draws an analogy to how reasoning models emerged: "I think getting to that would be paradigm-shifting, similar to how we saw reasoning a year and a half ago."

On scaffolding and the bitter lesson: He predicts the elaborate multi-agent scaffolding frameworks being hand-built today will eventually be generated on the fly *by the models themselves* — the scaffold becomes something the model writes for each task, optimized to that specific problem.

**关键洞察：** AI 的下一个前沿不是某项新的垂直能力，而是"元能力"——学习和适应的能力。Oriol Vinyals 认为，实现这一目标的机制，与其说是更新模型权重，不如说更像是一套组织良好的文件系统。

Oriol Vinyals 是 Google Gemini 的联席主导人，与 Noam Shazir 和 Jeff Dean 共同领导，同时也是过去十年深度学习核心突破的主要缔造者之一。这次对话录制于 Google IO 结束次日，涵盖了世界模型、强化学习在编码和数学之外的发展方向，以及为何"持续学习"可能是下一个类比推理模型的范式转变。

关于世界模型：Vinyals 区分了两种定义。流行的那种，是用语言指令驱动的视频生成（如 Omni 所展示的），令人印象深刻。但更深层的研究目标，是像从文本中提取知识那样，从视频和图像中提取知识。"那种纯粹的迁移，是我认为过去十多年机器学习核心追求之一。" 他坦言：视觉数据的"GPT 时刻"还没有到来。

关于 RL 的下一个领域：最令他兴奋的不是某个新的 RL 垂直方向，而是他所说的"元能力"——例如，模型能否从未见过的新指令中学习？能否在玩一个新游戏的过程中不断进步？他的测试方式是给模型一份陌生游戏的说明书，看它能否既理解规则又在对局中持续改进。当游戏足够脱离分布时，当前模型表现明显吃力。"学习和适应的能力，远比成为围棋高手或数学奥林匹克金牌选手的能力更让我着迷。"

关于记忆与持续学习：他看好文件系统风格的外部记忆，即 agent 在会话之间将结构化知识写入文件，而不是将学习内容重新整合进权重。原因很实际：以一个模型服务于数百万用户，为每个用户单独维护不同权重根本行不通。非参数式方案（一个持久化、可读写的知识库）已经很强大，而且他预期会越来越好。他类比了推理模型的崛起："我认为实现这一点，将是类似于一年半前推理模型出现那样的范式转变。"

关于"苦涩的教训"与脚手架：他预测，如今开发者手工搭建的复杂多 agent 框架，终将由模型自己按需生成——脚手架变成模型为每个具体任务量身生成的东西，而不再是开发者预先构建的结构。
