**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 86: Yann LeCun on Leaving Meta, Breaking The LLM Paradigm, & Why Hinton is Wrong**

**The Takeaway:** Yann LeCun believes the entire LLM paradigm is a dead end for building truly intelligent machines — and he's staking his post-Meta career on a completely different architecture.

LeCun is one of the founders of modern AI, sharing the Turing Award with Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio. After over a decade building Meta's FAIR into one of the world's top research labs, he left to found AMI Labs (Advanced Machine Intelligence) — headquartered in Paris, with a New York office deliberately placed away from Silicon Valley.

His core argument: large language models cannot predict the consequences of their own actions and cannot plan. They work well for language, math, and code — domains "where language itself is the substrate of reasoning" — but fail completely at understanding the physical world. "LLMs are intrinsically unsafe," he says bluntly. "I don't think they can be made reliable and safe."

The alternative he's building is called JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) — a non-generative approach that learns representations by predicting in abstract "thought space" rather than generating pixels or tokens. The insight came from observing that every successful self-supervised learning technique for images and video (DINO, iJEPA, V-JEPA) was non-generative, consistently outperforming generative alternatives.

His favorite analogy: a 17-year-old can learn to drive in 20 hours, yet millions of hours of driving data haven't produced Level 5 autonomy. Imitation learning — today's dominant approach in robotics — can never match the generalization a genuine world model would provide.

On Hinton and Bengio: their views split from his in 2023 when GPT-4 arrived. "They changed their mind. I didn't." He's skeptical of Anthropic's safety lobbying, suggesting commercial motivations may be entangled with the doomsday narrative.

His timeline: the paradigm shift will become "completely obvious" by early 2027. AMI Labs expects to demonstrate general hierarchical world models within 12-18 months, targeting industrial process control, robotics, and healthcare.

"The best way to get breakthrough research is: you hire the best people, and you get the f*** out of the way."

**要点：** Yann LeCun 认为，整个 LLM 范式对于构建真正智能的机器来说是死路一条——他正在用自己离开 Meta 后的职业生涯，押注于一套完全不同的架构。

LeCun 是现代 AI 的奠基人之一，与 Geoffrey Hinton 和 Yoshua Bengio 共同获得图灵奖。在花费十余年时间将 Meta AI 研究院（FAIR）打造成世界顶级研究机构之后，他离开并创办了 AMI Labs（Advanced Machine Intelligence），总部设在巴黎，纽约办公室则刻意远离硅谷。

他的核心论断：大型语言模型无法预测自身行为的后果，也无法进行规划。它们在语言、数学和代码领域表现出色——这些都是"语言本身就是推理基底"的领域——但在理解物理世界方面完全失败。"LLM 本质上是不安全的，"他直言不讳，"我认为它们无法被做得可靠和安全。"

他正在构建的替代方案叫做 JEPA（Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture，联合嵌入预测架构）——一种非生成式方法，通过在抽象的"思维空间"中预测来学习表征，而非生成像素或 token。这一洞见源于观察到：所有在图像和视频上成功的自监督学习技术（DINO、iJEPA、V-JEPA）都是非生成式的，且持续优于生成式方法。

他最喜欢的类比：一个 17 岁的年轻人能在 20 小时内学会开车，而数百万小时的驾驶数据至今没有产出 Level 5 自动驾驶。模仿学习——如今机器人领域的主流方法——永远无法实现真正的 world model 所具备的泛化能力。

关于 Hinton 和 Bengio 的分歧：他们的观点在 2023 年 GPT-4 面世时与他产生了分叉。"是他们改变了主意，不是我。" 他对 Anthropic 的安全游说持怀疑态度，暗示商业动机可能与末日叙事交织在一起。

他的时间线：这场范式转变将在 2027 年初变得"显而易见"。AMI Labs 预计在 12-18 个月内展示通用分层 world model，初步应用于工业过程控制、机器人和医疗领域。
