**Training Data — "Anthropic's Boris Cherny: Coding's Printing Press Moment"**

**The Takeaway:** Boris Cherny, creator of Claude Code, believes coding is already "solved" for him — and within years, it will be as universal as reading and writing.

Boris built Claude Code inside Anthropic Labs, a small internal incubator, in late 2024. The product shipped six months before it found product-market fit. That was intentional: "We were building for the next model." The exponential growth didn't begin until Opus 4 launched in May 2025, then inflected with each subsequent release — 4.5, 4.6, 4.7 and beyond.

Today, Boris writes zero code by hand. He merges dozens to 150 pull requests per day, runs 5-10 Claude Code sessions with hundreds of active agents, and lets thousands of agents work autonomously overnight — all from his phone. His core workflow tool is slash loop, which uses cron to schedule recurring agents: babysitting PRs, fixing flaky CI tests, clustering Twitter feedback every 30 minutes. Routines, a server-side version of loops that persists even when your laptop is closed, just launched.

On the future of teams: Boris sees cross-disciplinary generalists displacing narrow specialists. At Anthropic, everyone — product managers, designers, data scientists, finance staff — now writes code. He expects the rest of the industry to follow the same path, noting that Anthropic's real competitive edge isn't model access (which is the same for everyone), it's organizational structure and process.

On what he calls his favorite question — the "SaaSpocalypse": traditional moats like switching costs and process power will weaken as AI makes it trivial to migrate and automate workflows. But network effects and scale economies hold. The bigger shift: AI lets tiny startups compete head-to-head with large incumbents that face organizational inertia in adopting the same technology.

His most vivid analogy is the printing press. Before it, only 10% of Europeans were literate. In the 50 years after its invention, more literature was published in Europe than in the prior thousand years. Literacy eventually reached 70% globally. Software is at the same inflection point — and the transition will be far faster.

"The best person to write accounting software is not an engineer, it's a really good accountant. Because they know the domain really well and coding is the easy part."

**Training Data — "Anthropic的Boris Cherny：编程的活字印刷术时刻"**

**核心洞见：** Claude Code的创造者Boris Cherny认为，编程对他而言已经"被解决了"——而在未来几年内，编程将像读写一样成为普及技能。

Boris于2024年底在Anthropic Labs——一个小型内部孵化器——搭建了Claude Code。产品上线时距找到产品市场契合点还有六个月。这是刻意为之的："我们是在为下一个模型而构建。"指数级增长直到2025年5月Opus 4发布才开始，此后随着每一次模型迭代持续加速——4.5、4.6、4.7……

今天，Boris不再手写任何代码。他每天合并几十到150个pull request，同时运行着5到10个Claude Code会话、数百个活跃agent，并让数千个agent在夜间自主工作——这一切都通过手机完成。他的核心工作流工具是slash loop，利用cron调度循环运行的agent：监管PR、修复不稳定的CI测试、每三十分钟对Twitter反馈进行聚类分析。Routines——loop的服务端版本，关闭笔记本电脑后依然持续运行——也刚刚上线。

关于团队的未来：Boris认为跨学科的通才将逐步取代专才。在Anthropic，每个人——产品经理、设计师、数据科学家、财务人员——现在都在写代码。他预计整个行业将走上同样的路，并指出Anthropic真正的竞争优势不在于模型访问权限（这对所有人都一样），而在于组织架构和流程。

关于他最喜欢的问题——"SaaS末日论"：随着AI让迁移和工作流自动化变得轻而易举，切换成本和流程优势等传统护城河将会弱化。但网络效应和规模经济依然有效。更大的变化在于：AI让小型创业公司能够与面临组织惰性的大型巨头正面竞争。

他最生动的类比是活字印刷术。印刷机发明之前，仅有10%的欧洲人识字。印刷机诞生后的五十年里，欧洲出版的文献量超过了此前一千年的总和。全球识字率最终攀升至70%。软件正处于同样的拐点——而这次的转变将快得多。

"写财务软件最合适的人不是工程师，而是一位优秀的会计师。因为他们深谙业务领域，而编程才是容易的部分。"
