**Training Data: LIVE: Jensen Huang on Building the Dynamo of the Intelligence Age**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2UpQbeAZuqA

**The Takeaway:** Jensen Huang argues we're witnessing computing's most fundamental transformation since 1964 — from retrieval to generation — and positions NVIDIA as the builder of the new "Dynamo" for the intelligence age.

For decades, computers were retrieval machines: you stored data and fetched it back. The internet, data centers, recommendation systems — all retrieval. What changed is that AI now generates output fresh every single time based on context and prompt, making every computation unique. This shift, Huang argues, is as structurally significant as the invention of the power grid three hundred years ago and the internet thirty-five years ago.

The business case is concrete: AI went from "interesting" to genuinely valuable when it became agentic — capable of doing work, not just answering questions. "We pay for people who do work," Huang says. Now that AI does work, we pay it by the hour. One agent talking to another agent, then another, creates a multiplication effect that could eventually scale to hundreds of billions of agents running continuously on the internet.

Huang lays out a five-layer investment model for the AI economy: energy (the bottom layer and perhaps the biggest infrastructure opportunity in a generation), chips and networking (NVIDIA's layer), data center infrastructure, model layer (OpenAI, Anthropic, and "the $80 trillion physical world we're not talking about"), and applications (where $100 billion of VC flowed last year — the single largest year in history). Total AI investment this year: $1 trillion out of a projected $20 trillion annual ecosystem.

On jobs, Huang makes the radiology case: the prediction that computer vision would eliminate radiologists was right about the task, but wrong about the purpose. Radiology demand went up because productivity improved and more patients got treated. "You may or may not lose a job to an AI. But you will absolutely lose a job to someone who uses AI."

His most important framing: AI closes the technology divide. For forty years, the percentage of people who could program computers shrank (C++ literacy: roughly 2%). Now anyone who speaks a human language can direct a computer.

**核心观点：** Jensen Huang 认为，我们正在见证自 1964 年以来计算领域最根本性的转变——从检索到生成——并将英伟达定位为智能时代全新"发电机"的建造者。

几十年来，计算机本质上是检索机器：你存储数据，然后取回。互联网、数据中心、推荐系统——都是检索。而现在的变化在于，AI 每次都根据上下文和提示全新生成输出，让每一次计算都独一无二。Huang 认为，这一转变在结构意义上堪比三百年前电网的发明和三十五年前互联网的诞生。

商业逻辑很具体：当 AI 从"能回答问题"进化为 agentic（能够完成工作）时，它才真正有了价值。"我们为做事的人付钱，"Huang 说。现在 AI 能做事了，我们就按小时付费。一个 agent 与另一个 agent 对话，再与更多 agent 协作，这种倍增效应最终可能演化为数千亿个 agent 在互联网上持续运转。

Huang 为 AI 经济描绘了五层投资模型：能源（最底层，也可能是几代人中最大的基础设施机遇）、芯片与网络（英伟达所在层）、数据中心基础设施、模型层（OpenAI、Anthropic，以及"那个我们还没有充分讨论的价值 80 万亿美元的物理世界"）、以及应用层（去年 1000 亿美元风险投资流入，历史单年之最）。今年 AI 总投资约 1 万亿美元，而预期将达到每年 20 万亿美元的生态规模。

关于就业，Huang 以放射科为例：计算机视觉将取代放射科医生的预言在"任务"层面是对的，但在"目的"层面是错的。放射科需求反而上升了，因为生产力提升让医院能够诊治更多患者。"你可能会，也可能不会因 AI 失去工作。但你一定会因为懂得用 AI 的人而失去工作。"

他最重要的论断：AI 正在消除技术鸿沟。四十年来，能够编程的人在总人口中的比例不断下降（C++ 普及率约 2%）。而现在，任何会说人类语言的人都能指挥计算机。
