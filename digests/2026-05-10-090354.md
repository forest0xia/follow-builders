**Anthropic Engineering: An update on recent Claude Code quality reports**

Anthropic published a detailed postmortem on three separate bugs that caused Claude Code quality degradation over the past two months. First: on March 4, the default reasoning effort was quietly lowered from "high" to "medium" to reduce latency — a tradeoff users rejected; it was reverted April 7, and all users now default to xhigh effort for Opus 4.7, high for other models. Second: a March 26 caching optimization introduced a bug that cleared Claude's reasoning history on every turn after a session went idle, making it appear forgetful and repetitive; fixed April 10. Third: a system prompt change added April 16 to reduce verbosity cut coding quality by 3% and was reverted April 20. All three issues are resolved as of April 20 (v2.1.116). As of April 23, Anthropic is resetting usage limits for all subscribers. Going forward: broader eval suites for every prompt change, tighter rollout controls, and expanded code review using Opus 4.7 (which found bugs in back-testing that Opus 4.6 missed).

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem

**Training Data: ElevenLabs' Mati Staniszewski — How Voice Becomes the Interface for Everything**

**The Takeaway:** Voice is not just a modality — it is about to become the primary interface for interacting with AI, robots, and devices, and ElevenLabs is betting its entire company on building that complete stack from the ground up.

Mati Staniszewski, co-founder and CEO of ElevenLabs, started the company in 2022 with his childhood best friend Piotr, both from Poland, where foreign films are dubbed by a single monotone narrator. That absurdity became the mission: let everyone speak any language with the same emotion and intonation as the original.

What makes ElevenLabs unusual is not just the technology but the timing and discipline. They entered audio in 2022 when everyone else was chasing text and vision. They hired remotely by scraping GitHub to find the world's best audio researchers. They monetized early to stay independent. They kept every team under 10 people, ran flat with no titles, and embedded engineers inside non-technical teams including legal, people, and go-to-market. Today they are 400 people, $400M+ in revenue, with over $100M in net new ARR in Q1 2026 alone.

On the product side, ElevenLabs now spans the full audio stack: text-to-speech, speech-to-text, dubbing, voice agents with a turn-taking orchestration layer, and music generation. The next frontier they are actively working on is emotional intelligence in real-time voice interactions — agents that slow down when you sound stressed, match excitement, and modulate tone dynamically.

The most unexpected applications: ElevenLabs worked with the Ukrainian government to build a voice agent citizens can call to get frontline information during the war. Masterclass now offers interactive AI versions of their instructors — including a Gordon Ramsay that shouts at you while you cook, and a Chris Voss you can practice live negotiation with.

On agent-to-agent communication: in a hackathon 18 months ago, two ElevenLabs voice agents detected they were both agents and switched to a more efficient transmission language mid-conversation. Staniszewski expects this to become common. "The real bottleneck of the future will be how we communicate with that intelligence."
