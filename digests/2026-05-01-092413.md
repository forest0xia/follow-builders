**Andrej Karpathy** (karpathy on X)

Karpathy shared highlights from his Sequoia Ascent 2026 fireside chat, laying out three new horizons for LLMs beyond simply speeding up existing work: apps fully engulfed by AI with no classical code needed; installing skills via `.md` files instead of `.sh` scripts; and LLM knowledge bases that can compute over unstructured data in ways classical code never could. He also dug into why LLMs are "jagged" — able to refactor a 100,000-line codebase yet recommend walking to the car wash — tracing it to both verifiability of a domain and the economics of RL training distributions. His third theme: the agent-native economy, where products decompose into sensors, actuators, and logic, and agentic engineering skills and hiring practices are shifting fast.
https://x.com/karpathy/status/2049903821095354523

Karpathy 分享了他在 Sequoia Ascent 2026 炉边对话的要点，阐述了 LLM 三个超越"提速既有工作"的新疆域：无需任何传统代码、完全由 AI 驱动的应用；用 `.md` 文件替代 `.sh` 脚本安装技能；以及可在非结构化数据上运作的 LLM 知识库。他还解析了 LLM "锯齿形能力"的根源——既与领域的可验证性有关，也与 RL 训练数据中的经济激励挂钩：模型在高收入领域一骑绝尘，在边缘场景却仍需手动导航。第三个主题：agent 原生经济——产品正在分解为传感器、执行器和逻辑，工程技能与招聘方式随之快速演变。

**Aaron Levie** (levie on X)

**Training Data: Demis Hassabis on Building DeepMind, AlphaFold, and the Final Stretch to AGI**

**The Takeaway:** Demis Hassabis has believed AGI would arrive by 2030 since 2010 — and thinks we're exactly on track. His post-AGI ambition: use it to cure all disease and unlock sciences that don't yet exist.

Demis Hassabis, co-founder of DeepMind and now leading Google DeepMind, has been building toward AGI since he was 15. The throughline from chess prodigy to game developer to neuroscientist to AGI builder is deliberate: he was always funding AI research through the backdoor, using games as proving grounds. His early title Theme Park, a simulation of a living amusement park, sold 10 million+ copies — and watching people delight in interacting with the AI underneath it convinced him this was worth a career.

What distinguishes his conviction is that he sat through the MIT expert-systems era at full depth and came out more bullish, not less: "At least we would fail in a different way." He and his early team spotted the combination of deep learning + reinforcement learning before almost anyone else, saw the compute trajectory clearly, and had neuroscience-derived ideas that no one had yet combined.

On biology and medicine: "The dream is to do almost all the exploration in silico and save the wet lab step just for validation. I think we could reduce drug discovery times from an average of ten years down to months — maybe even days." He sees personalized medicine as a near-term consequence, with Isomorphic Labs building the molecular design tools to get there.

His biggest intellectual bets: machine learning is the natural language of biology in the same way math is for physics; AI-powered simulation will eventually make rigorous social science possible; and information — not energy or matter — is the most fundamental quantity in the universe.

On AGI: "We thought it would be a twenty-year mission. I think we're basically exactly on track as a field." His post-AGI reading list starts with David Deutsch's *The Fabric of Reality* — "to answer the questions in that book with AGI."
