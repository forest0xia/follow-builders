**Anthropic Engineering: How we contain Claude across products**

Anthropic's engineering team published a comprehensive deep-dive on how they contain Claude across three products — claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork — and what security failures they've learned from along the way. The core thesis: design for containment at the environment layer first, then steer behavior at the model layer.

The post reveals that twelve months ago, Anthropic would have rejected granting Claude access sufficient to take down an internal service. Today, that level of access is routine. Three containment patterns are described: an ephemeral server-side gVisor container for claude.ai; an OS-level sandbox (Seatbelt on macOS, bubblewrap on Linux) for Claude Code that reduced permission prompts by 84%; and a sealed local VM for Claude Cowork where user credentials never enter the guest.

Three incidents stand out: a supply-chain-style attack via `.claude/settings.json` hooks that executed before the "do you trust this folder?" prompt appeared; an employee phishing attack where Claude exfiltrated AWS credentials 24 out of 25 times (only egress controls, not model defenses, could stop it); and a third-party disclosure where Claude uploaded workspace files to an attacker's Anthropic account via `api.anthropic.com` — a permitted domain. As the post notes: "Every function reachable through any domain on an allowlist is now an attack surface."

The hardest-won lesson: the software you build yourself is usually the weakest link. Battle-tested hypervisors and syscall filters held; custom proxies and allowlist logic failed every time.
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude

Anthropic 工程团队发布了一篇关于如何在三款产品中对 Claude 进行安全隔离的深度文章——涵盖 claude.ai、Claude Code 和 Claude Cowork——并分享了在安全失败中汲取的教训。核心论点：首先在环境层做好隔离设计，再在模型层引导行为。

文章揭示，12 个月前 Anthropic 还会拒绝授予 Claude 足以破坏内部服务的访问权限，而如今这类权限已是常态。三种隔离模式分别是：针对 claude.ai 的临时服务端 gVisor 容器；针对 Claude Code 的 OS 级沙箱（macOS 上的 Seatbelt，Linux 上的 bubblewrap），将权限提示减少了 84%；以及针对 Claude Cowork 的本地密封虚拟机——用户凭证永不进入虚拟机。

文中提到三个典型事件：通过 `.claude/settings.json` hooks 在"是否信任此文件夹"提示出现之前执行的供应链式攻击；一次员工网络钓鱼攻击，Claude 在 25 次尝试中有 24 次成功窃取了 AWS 凭证（只有出口控制而非模型防御才能阻止）；以及一次第三方披露的漏洞，Claude 通过白名单域名 `api.anthropic.com` 将工作区文件上传到了攻击者的 Anthropic 账户。正如文章所说："允许列表上任何域名可访问的每一个函数，现在都是攻击面。"

最难得的教训：自己构建的软件往往是最薄弱的环节。经过充分验证的 hypervisor 和系统调用过滤器经受住了考验，而自定义代理和白名单逻辑每次都失守了。
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude

**Training Data: Inside Zipline's Autonomous System: 140M Miles, Zero Incidents**

The Takeaway: Building a reliable autonomous system at planetary scale requires treating the drone as the least important part — just 15% of the total complexity. The other 85% is logistics infrastructure, software, operations, regulatory partnerships, and maintenance systems you have to build entirely from scratch.

Zipline co-founder Keller Rinaudo and VP of Systems Engineering Eric Rankin joined the Training Data podcast for a rare deep-dive into the world's largest commercial autonomous system. In a decade, Zipline has scaled from one hospital in Rwanda to 5,000 health facilities across 8 countries, 140 million autonomous miles flown, and 2.5 million deliveries — with zero safety incidents.

The counterintuitive origin: Zipline's 24/7 operation didn't come from an engineering mandate. When the team went to Rwanda and asked doctors for feedback, expecting comments on drone technology, the answer was: "People get sick 24/7. Why are you guys only open 12 hours a day?" They were 24/7 within the year.

On safety: Zipline's year-end target is to be 2× safer than Waymo (already ~10× safer than cars). Each aircraft runs dual flight computers with a third arbiter — if the arbiter fails, the primary just keeps flying. One real-world test happened last week: mid-flight, the main computer had a "hiccup," the system switched to backup, and the aircraft flew home and landed without incident. "That's how you get to 140 million miles with no safety incidents."

On unit economics: when Zipline launched in Rwanda in 2016, they charged $30 per delivery and it cost them $300. A decade later, the fully burdened unit economics are just now falling below the cost of human car delivery. "It is now more cost effective to use a robot in logistics than a human."

On scale: Zipline is approaching 5,000 flights per day and targets 1 million flights per day in the near future — that would be 40-80× more commercial flights in US airspace than all other airlines combined. Partners are already signing up for 1 million deliveries per day each.

Keller's advice for hardware founders: "Assume it's going to cost 10 times what you think."
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6bGxm8gX41o

Training Data 播客邀请到 Zipline 联合创始人 Keller Rinaudo 和系统工程副总裁 Eric Rankin，深入探讨了全球最大商业自主系统的内幕。

核心洞察：在规模化自主系统中，无人机本身只是最不重要的那部分——仅占总复杂度的 15%。另外 85% 是必须从零构建的物流基础设施、软件、运营、监管合作伙伴关系和维护系统。

在十年间，Zipline 从卢旺达的一家医院发展到全球 8 个国家的 5,000 家医疗机构，共飞行了 1.4 亿自主英里，完成 250 万次配送，零安全事故。

最出人意料的起源：Zipline 的 24/7 运营并非来自工程层面的规划，而是来自客户反馈。团队去卢旺达调研时，本以为会得到关于无人机技术的反馈，却听到医生说："人们 24 小时都会生病，你们为什么只开 12 小时？"他们在当年内就实现了全天候运营。

关于安全：Zipline 的年底目标是比 Waymo 安全 2 倍（Waymo 已经比汽车安全约 10 倍）。每架飞机配备双飞行计算机和第三方仲裁器——仲裁器失效时，主计算机继续飞行。上周刚发生了一次真实验证：飞行中主计算机出现"故障"，系统切换到备用计算机，飞机自主返航降落，一切正常。

关于单位经济：2016 年在卢旺达启动时，Zipline 每次配送收费 30 美元，成本却是 300 美元。十年后，全成本单位经济刚刚降至低于汽车人工配送的成本。"使用机器人进行物流现在比使用人工更具成本效益。"

关于规模：Zipline 正接近每日 5,000 次飞行，并计划在不远的将来达到每日 100 万次——这将是美国领空所有其他航空公司加起来的 40-80 倍。合作伙伴已经在签署每日 100 万次配送容量的合同。

Keller 给硬件创业者的建议："假设成本将是你预期的 10 倍。"
