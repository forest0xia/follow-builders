**Claude Blog: "New in Claude Managed Agents: dreaming, outcomes, and multiagent orchestration"**

Anthropic launched three major updates to Claude Managed Agents. First, **dreaming**: a scheduled process that reviews past agent sessions, extracts patterns, and curates memory so agents self-improve over time — either automatically or with human review before changes land. It's especially powerful in multiagent settings where shared learnings can surface across many agents. Second, **outcomes**: you write a rubric for what success looks like, and a separate grader model evaluates the agent's output against that rubric, prompting a retry if the bar isn't met. In testing, outcomes improved task success rates by up to 10 points, with file generation gains of +8.4% (docx) and +10.1% (pptx). Third, **multiagent orchestration**: a lead agent can now delegate to specialist subagents running in parallel on a shared filesystem, each with its own model, prompt, and tools. Every step is traceable in the Claude Console. Real-world deployments cited: Harvey (legal drafting, 6x completion rate improvement with dreaming), Netflix (log analysis across hundreds of builds), and Spiral by Every (parallel writing with quality gates enforced by outcomes).
https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents

**Claude 博客：《Claude Managed Agents 新功能：dreaming、outcomes 与多 agent 编排》**

Anthropic 为 Claude Managed Agents 发布了三项重大更新。第一，**dreaming（回顾学习）**：一个定期运行的流程，会自动回顾历史 agent 会话、提取规律并整理记忆，让 agent 随时间自我进化——支持全自动更新或人工确认后生效，在多 agent 场景中尤为强大，可跨 agent 提炼共同学习成果。第二，**outcomes（目标评估）**：你只需写好成功标准，独立的评分 agent 会对输出进行评估，不达标则触发重试。测试数据显示，outcomes 可将任务成功率提升最多 10 个百分点，docx 文件生成质量提升 8.4%，pptx 提升 10.1%。第三，**多 agent 编排**：主 agent 可将任务拆解并委派给多个专门子 agent 并行执行，各子 agent 拥有独立的 model、prompt 和工具，共享文件系统，每一步均可在 Claude Console 中追溯。实际落地案例包括：Harvey（法律长文起草，借助 dreaming 任务完成率提升约 6 倍）、Netflix（跨数百个构建的日志分析）、Spiral by Every（并行写作 + outcomes 质量把关）。

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — "OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries"**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning isn't a finishing touch on top of language models — it's the engine that taught them to think, and it's what let AI disprove a decades-old math conjecture by having the audacity to assume everyone else was wrong.

Dan Roberts leads the foundations of reinforcement learning team at OpenAI, arriving from a theoretical physics background (MIT PhD, IAS postdoc) that shapes how he thinks about AI systems. His core claim: the "RL is the cherry on the cake" framing from a few years ago is dead. RL is now the cake. Pre-training builds the prior; RL turns that prior into something that can reason, explore, and self-correct.

The week's headline was AI cracking Erdős problems in combinatorics — and Roberts offered a revealing contrast between the two leading approaches. OpenAI's model worked in informal language (English math proofs, no formal verification layer), while DeepMind used Lean, a formal proof language where correctness is machine-checkable. The OpenAI result was distinctive not because the model was smarter, but because it was *contrarian*: "One of the things that ChatGPT was able to do was assume it was false. When you go against the grain and do something contrarian like that, you really have to have strong conviction in what you're doing in order to persevere down a really long calculation path." The model held a heterodox position and followed it for hours through algebraic number theory terrain most human specialists would never cross-wire together.

Roberts pushes back on Rich Sutton's "bitter lesson" (scale is all you need): good ideas are still essential, and the move from pre-training-only to pre-training + RL at scale is itself proof — it took insight, not just more compute. He also rejects the idea that phenomena "emerge" discontinuously at scale. His physics training drives a different instinct: if something looks discontinuous, you didn't understand what you were scaling. The job is to find a simplified toy model where the behavior is smooth, understand it there, and then scale with clarity.

On test-time compute: chain-of-thought isn't magic, it's just the model reusing its weights across more forward passes. The RL training process is what makes those passes productive — it teaches the model to use extended thinking time well.

Roberts closes with genuine excitement: he entered physics expecting to one day know the answers to its deepest questions, and eventually had to accept he might not live to see them. With AI as a research partner, he now believes many of those questions will get answered within reach.

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — 《OpenAI 的 Dan Roberts：为什么 AI 现在能做出科学发现》**

**核心洞见：** 强化学习不是语言模型的点睛之笔，而是教会它们"思考"的引擎——正是它让 AI 敢于反常识地假设"所有人都错了"，从而推翻了一个数十年悬而未决的数学猜想。

Dan Roberts 是 OpenAI 强化学习基础研究团队的负责人，理论物理学背景（MIT 博士、IAS 博士后）深刻影响了他对 AI 系统的思考方式。他的核心论断是：几年前流行的"RL 是蛋糕上的樱桃"比喻已经过时——RL 现在就是蛋糕本身。预训练建立先验知识，RL 让这种先验具备推理、探索和自我修正的能力。

近期最受关注的新闻是 AI 攻克 Erdős 组合数学问题。Roberts 对两种主流方案做了深刻对比：OpenAI 的模型用非形式化语言工作（英文数学证明，没有形式化验证层），DeepMind 使用 Lean（一种可机器验证正确性的形式证明语言）。OpenAI 的结果之所以引人注目，不在于模型更聪明，而在于它**敢于反向思考**："ChatGPT 能做到的其中一件事，就是假设这个命题是假的。逆势而为意味着你必须对自己的方向有极强的信念，才能在极长的推导链路中坚持下去。"这个模型坚持了一个异端立场，在代数数论领域跋涉数小时，走的是大多数人类专家绝不会尝试交叉的路径。

Roberts 也反驳了 Rich Sutton 的"苦涩教训"（规模是一切）：好的想法依然至关重要，从纯预训练到预训练叠加 RL 的跨越本身就是证明——这需要洞见，而不只是更多算力。他还否定了"能力在规模扩展时会不连续涌现"的观点，物理学训练让他有不同直觉：如果某个现象看起来是不连续的，说明你还没理解你在 scale 的是什么。正确做法是找到一个足够简单、能让行为变得平滑的玩具模型，在那里理解清楚，再带着清醒去扩展。

关于 test-time compute：思维链（chain-of-thought）并不神秘，本质上是模型在更多前向传播中复用自身权重。RL 训练过程的价值在于让这些额外的"思考时间"真正有效——它教会模型如何善用延长的推理时间。

Roberts 最后分享了一段令人动容的感慨：他当年进入物理学，以为终有一天能看到那些最深层问题的答案，后来才意识到也许这辈子都等不到。而有了 AI 作为科研伙伴，他现在相信，许多这样的问题终将在他有生之年得到解答。
