**Training Data — "How Autonomous Labs Will Transform Scientific Research: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly"**
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

**The Takeaway:** AI reasoning models paired with robotic labs have already beaten state-of-the-art human scientists at experimental biology — and Jason Kelly believes this is just the opening act of a total restructuring of how science gets done.

Jason Kelly is the founder and CEO of Ginkgo Bioworks, a company he started in 2008 with the mission of making biology programmable. The premise: DNA is code, cells are programmable like computers — but unlike computers that move information, cells move atoms, making them capable of producing physical things. For fifteen years, Ginkgo has worked on two parallel problems: making it cheaper and faster to test biological designs in the lab, and improving the quality of those designs themselves.

**The Takeaway:** AI reasoning models paired with robotic labs have already beaten state-of-the-art human scientists at experimental biology — and Jason Kelly believes this is just the opening act of a total restructuring of how science gets done.

In partnership with OpenAI, Ginkgo ran a landmark experiment. They gave a reasoning model access to a robotic laboratory and tasked it with optimizing "cell-free protein synthesis" — a process where you pop a cell open, extract its machinery, and use it to produce proteins in a test tube. The benchmark was set by a Stanford paper. After four rounds of autonomous experimentation (each round involving 384-well plates and thousands of data points), the AI beat the benchmark. After six rounds, it beat it by **40%**.

What made this work wasn't superior intelligence — it was the ability to run experiments 24 hours a day and systematically share data across parallel lines of inquiry. Kelly envisions a future where 100 AI scientists simultaneously pursue 100 different hypotheses, sharing raw experimental data *daily* rather than waiting for annual published papers. Today, less than 5% of research budgets go to reagents (the actual inputs); the rest is overhead — labs, people, administration. Autonomous labs flip that ratio, getting dramatically more data per dollar.

Kelly's conclusion is blunt: every previous tech revolution — the internet, social media — was irrelevant to biopharma. This one isn't. AI is about to fundamentally change how experimental science is done, and biopharma R&D leaders who aren't paying attention will be disrupted.
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

**Training Data — "How Autonomous Labs Will Transform Scientific Research: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly"**
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

**核心洞见：** AI 推理模型搭配机器人实验室，已经在实验生物学领域击败了人类顶尖科学家——而 Ginkgo Bioworks 创始人 Jason Kelly 认为，这只是科学研究方式彻底重构的序幕。

Jason Kelly 是 Ginkgo Bioworks 的创始人兼 CEO。他于 2008 年创立这家公司，使命是让生物学变得可编程。核心逻辑是：DNA 是代码，细胞是可编程的计算机——但不同于只处理信息的计算机，细胞操控的是原子，能够生产物质产品。十五年来，Ginkgo 同时在两个方向发力：降低生物设计在实验室中的测试成本与时间，以及提升设计本身的质量。

与 OpenAI 合作，Ginkgo 进行了一项标志性实验。他们让一个推理模型操控机器人实验室，目标是优化"无细胞蛋白质合成"——一种将细胞打开、提取其分子机器、在试管中生产蛋白质的技术。基准线由斯坦福大学的论文设定。经过四轮自主实验（每轮涉及 384 孔板和数千个数据点），AI 打破了基准；六轮之后，超越幅度达到 **40%**。

推动这一成果的并非更高的智能——而是 AI 能够 24 小时不间断运行实验，并在多条并行研究线之间系统共享数据。Kelly 设想的未来是：100 个 AI 科学家同时追踪 100 个不同假设，每天共享原始实验数据，而不是等待一年一度的论文发表。目前，研究预算中不到 5% 用于试剂（真正的投入品），其余全是开销——实验室、人员、行政。自主实验室将颠覆这一比例，用更少的钱获取更多数据。

Kelly 的结论毫不含糊：过去每一次技术革命——互联网、社交媒体——对生物医药行业几乎没有影响。但这一次不同。AI 将从根本上改变实验科学的运作方式，那些还没意识到这一点的生物医药 R&D 负责人，将面临颠覆。
