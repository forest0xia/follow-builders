**Claude Blog — "Building intelligent apps for Apple platforms with Claude in the Foundation Models framework"**

Claude is now available through Apple's Foundation Models framework via a new Swift package. Apple developers can use this to tap into Apple's on-device models for fast, local tasks — then seamlessly hand off to Claude for multi-step reasoning, code generation, web search, and code execution, all within the same view. Because Apple's framework returns typed Swift values through @Generable annotations, developers arrive at the Claude API call with clean, structured inputs instead of raw user text. The integration is available on iOS 27, iPadOS 27, macOS 27, visionOS 27, and watchOS 27. Practical examples: a journaling app can surface daily prompts on-device, then ask Claude to find threads across months of entries; a study app can define a concept locally, then hand off to Claude when the student asks "why does this matter for everything else we've covered?"

## PODCASTS

**Training Data — "Simulating Humans at Scale: Simile's Joon Sung Park"**

**The Takeaway:** Simulating human behavior at population scale is now tractable — and the companies that build this right could make social sciences as empirically rigorous as physics.

Joon Sung Park, founder and CEO of Simile, is building what he calls a "GPU of intelligence": not one rational superintelligence, but a massive parallelized simulation of diverse human personalities, values, and behaviors. His path started at Stanford in 2023 with "Smallville" — a generative agent experiment where 25 AI agents lived in a simulated town, woke up, went to work, formed relationships, and spontaneously threw Valentine's Day parties with no explicit instruction.

Park's core insight is that today's frontier models (OpenAI, Anthropic) are optimized for rationality and correctness — "CPUs of intelligence" that excel at problems with objective answers. But real human simulation requires something different. "Turns out, people are irrational. We have a lot of subjective values, preferences, and tastes. So you actually start to see divergence in model size going up and the performance in its ability to predict and simulate human behavior."

His enterprise customers — including CVS — use Simile to run concept testing at scales traditional polling can't reach: testing 1,000 product ideas across 1,000 subpopulations simultaneously. The company has validated that its simulations achieve 85% accuracy at predicting how real people replicate their own responses, with a north star metric of Total Variation Distance below 0.15 for quantitative studies.

The long-term vision is far grander: simulating earnings calls, market dynamics, the conditions that precede bank fraud, even what signals foretell a democracy's collapse. Park draws an explicit parallel to Thomas Schelling's Nobel-winning agent-based models on segregation: "Simulation can be the Hubble Telescope for human society."

**Training Data — "大规模模拟人类：Simile 的 Joon Sung Park"**

**核心观点：** 在人口规模上模拟人类行为已经成为可能，做好这件事的公司，或许能让社会科学像物理学一样具备严格的实证基础。

Simile 创始人兼 CEO Joon Sung Park 正在构建他所称的"intelligence 的 GPU"：不是一个理性的超级智能，而是对多元人类个性、价值观和行为的大规模并行模拟。起点是 2023 年他在斯坦福主导的"Smallville"实验——25 个 AI agent 生活在模拟小镇里，自主醒来、上班、建立社交关系，并在没有任何明确指令的情况下自发组织了情人节派对。

Park 的核心洞见是：当今前沿模型（OpenAI、Anthropic）是为理性和正确性而优化的"intelligence 的 CPU"，擅长解决有客观答案的问题。但真实的人类模拟需要截然不同的东西。"事实证明，人类是非理性的。我们有大量主观的价值观、偏好和品味。因此你会看到：模型规模越大，它预测和模拟人类行为的能力反而开始出现偏差。"

包括 CVS 在内的企业客户使用 Simile 以传统调研无法企及的规模进行概念测试，比如同时在 1000 个细分人群中测试 1000 个产品创意。公司已验证其模拟能以 85% 的准确率预测真实用户如何复现自己的行为，定量研究的核心评估指标是总变异距离（TVD）低于 0.15。

长远愿景更为宏大：模拟财报电话会、市场动态、银行欺诈爆发前的条件，乃至民主制度崩溃的前兆信号。Park 明确类比了 Thomas Schelling 那项关于种族隔离的诺贝尔奖级 agent-based 模型研究："模拟可以成为人类社会的哈勃望远镜。"
