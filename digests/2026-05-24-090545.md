**Aaron Levie** (CEO, Box)

Box CEO Aaron Levie pushed back sharply on the "AI kills jobs" narrative. His argument: automating individual tasks within a job doesn't eliminate the role — it expands the definition of that role. As AI handles each task, workers move to the next layer of complexity, and the job becomes valuable in new ways. Small businesses that previously couldn't afford a marketing agency can now hire one person who, with AI agents, delivers agency-level output. The mistake is confusing task completion with job elimination.

Box CEO Aaron Levie 对「AI 消灭就业」的说法提出了强力反驳。他的论点是：自动化工作中的某项任务并不会消灭这个职位，而是会扩大该职位的定义边界。随着 AI 接管一个个任务，员工会转向更高层次的工作，职位反而以新的方式变得更有价值。以前负担不起营销代理机构的小企业，现在只需雇一个营销人员，借助 AI agent 就能达到代理机构级别的产出。把完成某项任务等同于消灭整个岗位，是一个常见的认知误区。

**No Priors — "The Story Behind Cerebras' $63 Billion IPO with Founder and CEO Andrew Feldman"**

**The Takeaway:** Speed doesn't just make things faster — it creates entirely new categories of business, and Cerebras spent a decade building for that moment before the world caught up.

Andrew Feldman co-founded Cerebras in the mid-2010s with a radical hardware bet: build a chip the size of a dinner plate (46,000 sq mm, "wafer scale") instead of stacking postage-stamp-sized chips. The industry called it impossible — even the legendary Gene Amdahl had failed at wafer-scale before. From 2017 to 2019, Cerebras spent $8 million a month going to board meetings every six weeks to report the same thing: "we still can't build it." Then in summer 2019, it worked.

But nobody cared. Being 15–20x faster than GPUs is a trivia point when AI isn't useful enough to run at daily scale. Cerebras sold a dozen units in gen one. Then 300. They ran the table in supercomputing and found a crucial bridge in UAE-based G42, who placed a billion-dollar order. That funding enabled supply chain maturation and real cluster-scale testing.

Then 2025 happened. AI became genuinely daily-use, and inference speed became existential. In December 2025, Cerebras signed a $20+ billion deal with OpenAI in under five weeks — term sheet the night before Thanksgiving, master agreement by December 24. A deployment agreement with AWS followed. The company went public at roughly $63 billion.

Feldman's analogy: "Netflix used to deliver DVDs in envelopes. When the Internet got fast, they became a movie studio." Fast AI won't just accelerate existing workflows — it will make today's business model assumptions obsolete and generate categories that don't exist yet.

His advice for founders in the waiting-for-the-market phase: put explicit hypotheses on paper for what has to be true for this to work. When those come back negative, it's time to stop. The slippery slope is testing "one more thing" indefinitely — get seasoned operators around you who will hold you to what you said earlier.

**No Priors — 「Cerebras 630 亿美元 IPO 背后的故事：创始人兼 CEO Andrew Feldman」**

**核心要点：** 速度不只是让事情变得更快——它能催生全新的商业类别。Cerebras 为这一时刻构建了十年，直到世界终于赶上来。

Andrew Feldman 在 2010 年代中期联合创立了 Cerebras，押注了一个激进的硬件方向：建造一颗盘子大小（46,000 平方毫米，"晶圆级"）的芯片，而不是堆叠邮票大小的芯片。行业内的人都说这不可能——连传奇人物 Gene Amdahl 此前都在晶圆级计算上失败过。从 2017 年到 2019 年，Cerebras 每月烧掉 800 万美元，每六周开一次董事会，汇报同样的结论："我们还是造不出来。"然后在 2019 年夏天，它成功了。

但没有人在意。当 AI 还不够实用时，比 GPU 快 15 到 20 倍只是个无关紧要的数据点。Cerebras 第一代只卖出了十几台，第二代大约卖出 300 台。他们在超算领域横扫市场，并找到了一个关键的过渡客户——阿联酋的 G42，对方下了一笔 10 亿美元的订单。这笔资金推动了供应链成熟，也实现了真正集群规模的测试。

然后 2025 年到来了。AI 变得真正适合日常使用，推理速度突然成为生死存亡的关键。2025 年 12 月，Cerebras 在不到五周内与 OpenAI 签订了一份逾 200 亿美元的协议——感恩节前夕敲定意向书，12 月 24 日签署主协议。随后与 AWS 的数据中心部署协议也随之达成。公司上市时市值约 630 亿美元。

Feldman 的比喻一针见血："Netflix 以前用信封邮寄 DVD。当互联网变快后，他们变成了电影制片公司。"快速 AI 不只是会加速现有工作流——它会让今天的商业模式假设变得过时，并催生出目前根本不存在的全新商业类别。

他给那些处于"等待市场成熟"阶段的创始人的建议：把"这件事要成功需要哪些条件成立"明确写下来。当这些条件逐一被验证为否时，就该停下来了。滑坡效应的危险在于一再说"再测一件事"——找一些经验丰富的创业老手陪在你身边，让他们记住你当初说过的话，并在关键时刻拉你回来。
