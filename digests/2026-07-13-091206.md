**No Priors — "How Nuclear Will Unlock Energy Abundance with Valar Atomics Founder Isaiah Taylor"**

**The Takeaway:** Nuclear energy's biggest barrier isn't physics or safety — it's that the industry forgot how to build things. Valar Atomics just proved that a startup with a hardware-first mindset and relentless iteration can fix fifty years of stagnation in under three years.

Isaiah Taylor founded Valar Atomics despite having no traditional nuclear credentials. What he had instead was a clear-eyed diagnosis: the nuclear industry had become a "modeling and simulation industry" — full of paper reactors, exotic supply chains, and absurdly inflated costs. A vendor quoted $5M and two and a half years to build a reactor protection system. Valar's team built their own in six weeks for $400k. When no suitable concrete existed to shield the reactor without rebar (which becomes nuclear waste when irradiated), 23- and 21-year-old engineers flew across the country collecting rock samples, dissolved them in acid in the conference room on Friday nights, ran spectroscopy analysis, and invented a new grade of concrete.

Less than three years after founding, Valar's Ward 250 — a TRISO-fueled, graphite-moderated, helium-cooled reactor — became the first advanced reactor ever powered by a private startup, and the first new nuclear device to power an AI chip (an NVIDIA Blackwell connected directly to the reactor). Taylor calls their core metric "tick rate": the time between turning on successive reactors. First atom split took two years and four months from founding. The second reactor took seven more months. The goal is to compress tick rate to minutes.

The design philosophy is deliberately contrarian: "We don't want to make Lamborghinis. We want to make a very simple, very cheap, very safe reactor that we can make literally tens of thousands of." Unlike traditional light-water reactors that require active cooling even after shutdown — the failure mode behind both Three Mile Island and Fukushima — Valar's design is inherently safe through physics alone. Passive cooling via natural circulation, no moving parts, no human input. They proved it by shutting off all safety systems simultaneously and watching the plant stabilize on its own over two days.

Taylor's long-horizon argument: as AI and robotics convert human labor into energy consumption, cheap energy becomes the cost of all things. Make energy ten times cheaper, and you make everything ten times cheaper. "If you can figure out how to make energy cheaper, you will have demand. Energy being a commodity, that's true forever."

**No Priors — "核能如何解锁能源丰盛"（嘉宾：Valar Atomics 创始人 Isaiah Taylor）**

**核心洞见：** 核能最大的障碍不是物理学，也不是安全性——而是整个行业忘记了如何动手造东西。Valar Atomics 用不到三年的时间，证明了一家以硬件迭代为核心的初创公司，完全可以打破五十年的停滞。

Isaiah Taylor 在没有传统核能背景的情况下创办了 Valar Atomics。他的起点是一个清醒的诊断：核能行业已经变成了一个"建模与仿真行业"——充斥着纸面上的反应堆、稀缺的供应链和离谱的价格。某供应商报价 500 万美元、交货需要两年半，只为提供一个反应堆保护系统。Valar 的团队用六周时间、40 万美元自己造了一个。当他们找不到合适的混凝土来屏蔽辐射（传统钢筋会被中子激活变成核废料）时，两名分别 23 岁和 21 岁的工程师飞遍全国收集岩石样本，周五晚上在会议室用酸液溶解、做光谱分析，最终发明了一种全新等级的混凝土。

Valar 成立不到三年，其 Ward 250 反应堆——使用 TRISO 燃料、石墨慢化、氦气冷却——成为史上第一座由私人初创公司建造并产出电力的先进反应堆，也是第一座直接为 AI 芯片（NVIDIA Blackwell）供电的核反应堆。Taylor 用"tick rate"衡量公司进度：从公司注册到首次分裂原子用了两年零四个月，第二座反应堆又花了七个月。目标是把这个间隔压缩到分钟级。

Valar 的设计哲学刻意反其道而行："我们不是要造兰博基尼，我们要造的是一个极简、极便宜、极安全的反应堆，能造出成千上万台。"与传统轻水反应堆（关闭后仍需持续主动冷却，三里岛和福岛事故的根本原因就在于此）不同，Valar 的设计依靠物理原理本身实现本质安全：被动自然循环冷却，无运动部件，无需人工干预。他们已通过实验验证——同时关闭所有安全系统，反应堆在两天内自然稳定下来。

Taylor 的长期逻辑是：随着 AI 和机器人技术将人类劳动转化为能源消耗，廉价能源最终将决定一切物品的成本。能源便宜十倍，万物便宜十倍。"如果你能造出更便宜的能源，需求自然会来。这对能源这种商品来说是永远成立的。"
