**No Priors: How Nuclear Will Unlock Energy Abundance with Valar Atomics Founder Isaiah Taylor**

**The Takeaway:** A nuclear startup just made power from its own reactor — the first time a private company has done so since fission was discovered — and the founder believes this is the Ford Model T moment for nuclear energy.

Isaiah Taylor, founder and CEO of Valar Atomics, built the first advanced nuclear reactor to make power as a startup, inside a facility in Utah. The company is less than three years old. That alone is remarkable. But the philosophy behind it is what makes this conversation worth your time.

Taylor's core conviction: nuclear is not a design problem, it's a hardware execution problem. Most nuclear companies — what he calls "modeling and simulation companies" — produce paper reactors with beautiful specifications that never get built. Valar does the opposite: iterate fast, build simple, and actually turn things on. Their internal metric is "tick rate," borrowed from video games — how quickly can you go from one criticality event to the next? Their first reactor went critical two years and four months after founding. The second took seven months. The goal is to compress tick rate to minutes.

The comparison to SpaceX is apt and intentional: "Exponentials are really, really hard for humans to understand. If you're looking at the nuclear space today, you don't expect people to have this problem solved in 2031. In fact, I would say even 2035, a lot of these companies are still not gonna get there because they have the wrong mindset."

What they built: a TRISO-fueled, graphite-moderated, helium-cooled reactor where the physics prevents meltdown — no active cooling required post-shutdown. They demonstrated this by turning off every safety system after full-power operation and watching the reactor passively cool itself over two days. They also powered an NVIDIA Blackwell chip directly from the reactor's output, hosting a live website from it (nuclearwebsite.com — only accessible while the reactor runs).

The most revealing detail: their reactor protection system was quoted at $5M with a 2.5-year delivery timeline. A team of five engineers — including a 23-year-old and a 21-year-old — built a working version in six weeks for $400k. That's the Valar playbook: find the bottleneck, verticalize it, go fast.

**The Takeaway（要点）：** 一家核能初创公司刚刚用自建反应堆成功发电——这是自核裂变被发现以来，私营企业首次做到——而创始人相信，这是核能的"福特 T 型车时刻"。

Valar Atomics 创始人兼 CEO Isaiah Taylor 在犹他州的设施中，建造了首个由初创公司发电的先进核反应堆。这家公司成立不到三年。仅这一点便已非凡。但支撑这背后的理念，更值得你花时间细细了解。

Taylor 的核心信念：核能不是设计问题，而是硬件执行问题。大多数核能公司——他称之为"建模与仿真公司"——生产的是从未被建造出来的纸面反应堆，规格精美却束之高阁。Valar 恰恰相反：快速迭代、保持简单、实际运行。他们的内部指标是从电子游戏借来的"tick rate"——从一次临界事件到下一次临界事件需要多久？他们的第一台反应堆在公司成立两年四个月后达到临界，第二台只用了七个月。目标是将 tick rate 压缩至以分钟计。

与 SpaceX 的类比既恰当又有意为之："指数增长对人类来说真的很难理解。如果你今天看核能领域，你不会期待这个问题在 2031 年得到解决。事实上，我认为即使到 2035 年，很多公司仍然无法做到，因为他们的思维方式是错的。"

他们建造的是：TRISO 燃料、石墨减速、氦气冷却的反应堆，其物理特性天然防止堆芯熔毁——停堆后无需主动冷却。他们通过一次测试验证了这一点：满功率运行后关闭所有安全系统，观察反应堆在两天内被动冷却至安全状态。他们还用反应堆直接为一块 NVIDIA Blackwell 芯片供电，用它托管了一个实时网站（nuclearwebsite.com——仅在反应堆运行期间可访问）。

最具说明性的细节：他们的反应堆保护系统被外部供应商报价 500 万美元、交期两年半。由五名工程师组成的团队——包括一名 23 岁和一名 21 岁的成员——用六周时间以 40 万美元完成了一个可用版本。这就是 Valar 的打法：找到瓶颈，垂直自研，全速推进。
