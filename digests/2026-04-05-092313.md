**No Priors — "AI for Atoms: How Periodic Labs is Revolutionizing Materials Engineering with Co-Founder Liam Fedus"**

**The Takeaway:** The next frontier of AI isn't software — it's matter. Liam Fedus, co-creator of ChatGPT and former VP of Post-Training at OpenAI, left one of the most coveted seats in AI to build Periodic Labs, which applies foundation models to materials science. His bet: the same paradigm shift that transformed software engineering can now begin to transform how humanity discovers and engineers physical materials.

Fedus's path — physics undergrad, dark matter research, Google Brain during the transformer era, OpenAI for GPT-4's productionization — gives him a rare vantage point. He describes Periodic's architecture as a language model acting as an orchestration layer, directing specialized neural networks purpose-built for atomic systems with symmetry awareness. The LLM handles questions, literature ingestion, and experiment direction; the specialized nets handle the physics at low latency.

The data problem in materials science is genuinely hard. Unlike the internet for language models, materials data is sparse and inconsistent — reported property values in published literature can span "many orders of magnitude," making naive training useless. Periodic's answer is a closed-loop system: experimental data, physics simulations, and literature are combined in an active loop that drives the next round of experiments. "It's not just a pool of data," Fedus says, "it's a very active loop."

Generalization is more constrained than in language: a model trained on quantum mechanical effects doesn't readily transfer to fluid dynamics. But within governed domains, sample efficiency is high — the system carries a strong prior from the general-purpose models it builds on, dramatically reducing the data needed to reach useful performance in new chemical spaces.

On the business model, Fedus is starting with Periodic as its own customer zero — learning how to transform materials science internally — before expanding to enterprises bottlenecked by materials engineering and process engineering. A biotech-style discovery model is on the table as breakthroughs emerge. The long view: "Software engineering now looks wildly different than even six months ago. I think we see similar opportunities in the physical world."

**No Priors — 「AI for Atoms：Periodic Labs 如何用 AI 革新材料工程」（嘉宾：联合创始人 Liam Fedus）**

**核心观点：** AI 的下一个前沿不是软件，而是物质本身。Liam Fedus 是 ChatGPT 的共同创造者、前 OpenAI 后训练副总裁，他离开 AI 领域最令人羡慕的职位之一，去创建了 Periodic Labs——将 foundation model 应用于材料科学。他的赌注是：改变了软件工程的同一范式转变，现在可以开始改变人类发现和设计物理材料的方式。

Fedus 的经历——物理学本科、暗物质研究、transformer 时代的 Google Brain、参与 GPT-4 产品化的 OpenAI——赋予了他独特的视角。他将 Periodic 的架构描述为：以语言模型作为编排层，指挥专门为具有对称性感知的原子系统而设计的特殊神经网络。LLM 负责处理问题、摄取文献和指导实验；专用网络则以低延迟处理物理计算。

材料科学中的数据问题确实很难。与语言模型的互联网数据不同，材料数据稀疏且不一致——已发表文献中报道的属性值可能跨越「许多个数量级」，使得简单的训练毫无用处。Periodic 的解决方案是一个闭环系统：实验数据、物理仿真和文献资料在一个主动循环中结合，驱动下一轮实验。Fedus 说：「这不只是一个数据池，而是一个非常主动的循环。」

材料科学中的泛化能力比语言领域更受限：针对量子力学效应训练的模型并不能轻松迁移到流体动力学。但在受控领域内，样本效率很高——系统从其所依托的通用模型中获得了强大的先验知识，大幅减少了在新化学空间中达到实用性能所需的数据量。

在商业模式上，Fedus 将 Periodic 自身作为第一个客户，在内部学习如何变革材料科学——然后再扩展到受材料工程和工艺工程制约的企业。随着突破的出现，类似生物技术的发现模式也在考虑之中。长远展望：「软件工程在短短六个月内就发生了翻天覆地的变化。我认为在物理世界中，我们会看到类似的机遇。」
