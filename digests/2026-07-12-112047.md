**No Priors — Travel Through the Lens of AI with Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel**

**The Takeaway:** There is no moat — scale and regulatory mastery buy time, but the only durable advantage is relentless reinvention, and AI is both the biggest threat to incumbents and the most powerful tool for doing exactly that.

Glenn Fogel has spent 27 years at Booking Holdings, joining when it was Priceline and worth a few hundred million dollars; the company now exceeds $130 billion in market cap and processes $186 billion in annual travel volume. His perspective on AI is anything but abstract.

Fogel is genuinely bullish on agentic travel — and already building it. Booking's Penny (Priceline's agentic AI system) has doubled in monthly adoption for several consecutive months, delivering measurably higher conversion, faster paths to booking, and lower cancellations. He recently used it himself to plan a complex family European trip: two cabin classes, separate return cities for different family members, miles-vs.-cash optimization across airlines. "It was wonderful," he said — and that experience is the product direction he's chasing at scale.

But he's clear-eyed about what he doesn't yet know: the per-transaction token cost, the right model for each step, the long-term lifetime-value impact of AI-assisted bookings. "Which model should we be using? For which purpose? And when?" These are live, unsolved questions at a company doing a billion room nights a year.

His most counterintuitive point on competition: he doesn't believe in moats. Not for Booking, not for anyone. "Today, we have a competitive advantage on areas. Absolutely. But those can go away tomorrow." The only winning strategy is to keep building — particularly on the partner side, where the relationship complexity and regulatory compliance burden are things new AI entrants consistently underestimate.

On the jobs question, Fogel draws on lived experience: he watched machine translation eliminate hundreds of multilingual roles at booking.com over a decade. His concern now isn't whether new jobs will emerge — history says they will — it's the timing mismatch between displacement and creation. His company's response: invest heavily in AI upskilling across all 25,000 employees, because "even if we end up that we can't replace or retrain someone else, at least they are better skilled for a job somewhere else."

"There is no such thing as a moat. There is no such thing as somewhere you're gonna be protected against innovation."

**No Priors — 深度对话 Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel：AI 重塑旅行的现实与想象**

**核心洞察：** 护城河并不存在。规模优势和监管合规能力只能争取时间，真正持久的竞争力只有一种——不断自我颠覆。而 AI 既是对巨头最大的威胁，也是实现这种颠覆最有力的工具。

Glenn Fogel 在 Booking Holdings 待了 27 年，加入时公司还叫 Priceline，市值只有几亿美元；如今公司市值超过 1300 亿美元，每年处理 1860 亿美元的旅行交易量。他对 AI 的看法，远不是纸上谈兵。

Fogel 对 agentic 旅行充满信心，而且已经在做了。Booking 旗下 Priceline 的 agentic AI 系统 Penny，连续数月月活翻倍，带来了可量化的转化率提升、更短的预订路径和更低的取消率。他最近亲自用它规划了一次复杂的家庭欧洲旅行：两个舱位等级、不同家庭成员不同的回程城市、跨航空公司里程与现金的最优化组合。"太棒了，"他说——而这段体验，正是他要在规模上追求的产品方向。

不过他也坦诚地列出了还没答案的问题：每笔交易的 token 成本是多少，每个环节该用哪个模型，AI 辅助预订的长期用户生命周期价值如何。"应该用哪个模型？用于哪个目的？什么时候用？" 这些都是一家每年处理十亿间夜的公司当下仍在摸索的实际问题。

他最反常识的观点是关于竞争壁垒的：他不相信护城河的存在。不是 Booking 的，也不是任何人的。"今天，我们在某些领域确实有竞争优势。但这些优势明天就可能消失。"唯一的赢法是持续建设——尤其是在合作伙伴侧，那里的关系复杂度和监管合规门槛，是新兴 AI 竞争者始终低估的东西。

关于就业问题，Fogel 有亲身经历：他亲眼看到机器翻译在十年间淘汰了 booking.com 数百个多语言岗位。他现在担心的不是新岗位能否出现——历史告诉他会的——而是岗位消失与岗位创造之间的时间错位。公司的应对之策：对全部 25000 名员工进行大规模 AI 技能升级，因为"就算我们最终无法帮某些人转岗，至少让他们在其他地方更有竞争力。"

"根本不存在什么护城河。根本不存在你能在创新浪潮中躲避的地方。"
