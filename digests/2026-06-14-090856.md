**No Priors — Biohub: The Future of Biology is Open-Source with Co-Founders Mark Zuckerberg, Priscilla Chan, and Head of Science Alex Rives**

The Takeaway: Mark Zuckerberg and Priscilla Chan are betting that giving AI-powered biology tools to the entire scientific community — free, open-source — will cure more diseases than any single company ever could.

Ten years ago, Zuckerberg and Chan walked into meetings with Nobel Prize-winning scientists announcing they wanted to "cure, prevent, and manage all disease by the end of the century." The scientists laughed. Now those same timelines feel conservative.

Their philanthropic organization, CZ Biohub, has evolved from a general tool-development effort into something more precise: a "frontier AI + frontier biology" lab, with a $500M commitment to a Virtual Biology Initiative. The strategy is deliberately hierarchical — model proteins first, then cells, then full biological systems — because "you can't just go straight to cells without understanding the protein modeling."

Alex Rives, the Head of Science who joined from Meta Fair, announced a major release: the new ESMfold, a protein language model trained on 1.1 billion protein sequences. It achieves state-of-the-art performance on protein-protein interaction benchmarks and can now design single-chain antibodies digitally — then validate them in a 96-well plate in a single experimental cycle. "We didn't design a model for antibodies. We didn't design a model to be able to bind one particular target. We just designed a model that could understand proteins, and you kind of get protein design as an emergent property."

Perhaps the most underreported angle: they're applying mechanistic interpretability — a technique usually used to understand language models — to protein models, to discover biology the model has learned that humans don't yet know. "You open up the black box and you can actually understand the biology that the model is representing."

The reason it's a nonprofit: not purity, but strategy. Priscilla Chan argued that the neutral, open structure attracts more scientists, enables more contributions, and is the only way to address the long tail of rare diseases that commercial incentives would otherwise ignore.

Five-year north star: world-class hierarchical world models of biology, and tools that empower researchers everywhere to work faster. "The theory isn't that we're gonna cure the diseases. We're not. It's that we wanna help accelerate the pace of progress for the whole scientific field."

**No Priors — Biohub：生物学的未来是开源的，联合创始人 Mark Zuckerberg、Priscilla Chan 与科学负责人 Alex Rives 出席**

核心观点：Mark Zuckerberg 和 Priscilla Chan 正在押注 —— 将 AI 驱动的生物学工具免费开源地提供给整个科学界，将比任何单一公司都能治愈更多疾病。

十年前，Zuckerberg 和 Chan 走进诺贝尔奖得主的会议室，宣布要"在本世纪末治愈、预防和管理所有疾病"。科学家们笑了。如今，那些时间线反而显得保守。

他们的慈善机构 CZ Biohub 已从一个通用工具开发机构，进化为更精准的形态："前沿 AI + 前沿生物学"实验室，并承诺向"虚拟生物学计划"投入 5 亿美元。其策略是刻意分层的 —— 先建模蛋白质，再到细胞，再到完整生物系统 —— 因为"没有理解蛋白质建模，就无法直接跳到细胞"。

从 Meta Fair 加入的科学负责人 Alex Rives 宣布了一个重要发布：新版 ESMfold —— 一个在 11 亿条蛋白质序列上训练的蛋白质语言模型。它在蛋白质-蛋白质相互作用基准测试中达到最先进水平，现在可以纯数字化设计单链抗体，并在一次实验周期（96 孔板）内完成验证。"我们没有专门设计用于抗体的模型，也没有设计用于结合特定靶标的模型。我们只是设计了一个能理解蛋白质的模型，蛋白质设计就作为涌现能力自然出现了。"

最容易被忽视的角度：他们将机械可解释性 —— 通常用于理解语言模型的技术 —— 应用于蛋白质模型，以发现模型已经学到而人类尚不知晓的生物学知识。"打开黑盒，你就能真正理解模型所表征的生物学。"

选择非营利模式的原因：不是出于纯粹性，而是出于战略。Priscilla Chan 认为，中立开放的结构能吸引更多科学家、激发更多贡献，也是解决罕见病长尾问题的唯一方式 —— 这些疾病否则会被商业逻辑忽视。

五年北极星：面向全球研究人员的世界级分层生物学世界模型，以及帮助他们更快开展研究的工具。"我们的目标不是治愈疾病。不是我们。而是帮助加速整个科学领域的进步。"
