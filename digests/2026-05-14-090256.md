## OFFICIAL BLOGS

**Claude Blog — New in Claude Managed Agents: dreaming, outcomes, and multiagent orchestration**

Anthropic's Claude Platform is shipping three significant upgrades to its Managed Agents offering. First: **dreaming** — a scheduled background process that reviews past agent sessions, extracts recurring patterns, and automatically refines an agent's memory store between sessions. Harvey is already running this in legal workflows; their task completion rates went up roughly 6x in internal tests. Second: **outcomes** — you write a rubric describing what success looks like, and a separate grader evaluates the agent's output in its own context window, isolated from the agent's own reasoning. When output falls short, the grader pinpoints the gap and the agent self-corrects. In benchmarks, outcomes improved task success by up to 10 percentage points, with +8.4% on docx file generation and +10.1% on pptx. Third: **multiagent orchestration** — a lead agent breaks complex work into pieces and delegates each to a specialist with its own model, prompt, and tools running in parallel. Netflix's platform team uses this to process build logs from hundreds of apps simultaneously; Spiral by Every runs a lead agent on Haiku that delegates drafting work to Opus subagents, scoring each draft against an editorial rubric before returning it to the user.

"Agents do their best work when they know what 'good' looks like."
https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents

Anthropic 的 Claude Platform 对其 Managed Agents 平台发布了三项重要升级。第一项：**dreaming（梦境）** — 一个定期在后台运行的流程，它回顾过去的 agent 会话，提取反复出现的模式，并在会话间自动优化 agent 的记忆库。Harvey 已经将其用于法律工作流程，内部测试中任务完成率提升了约 6 倍。第二项：**outcomes（结果评估）** — 你编写一个描述"成功是什么样子"的评分标准，一个独立的评分器在自己的上下文窗口中（与 agent 的推理隔离）对 agent 的输出进行评估。当输出不达标时，评分器会指出差距，agent 进行自我修正。基准测试中，outcomes 将任务成功率提升了最多 10 个百分点，docx 文件生成提升 +8.4%，pptx 提升 +10.1%。第三项：**多 agent 编排** — 一个主导 agent 将复杂工作拆解成若干部分，分配给各自拥有独立模型、提示词和工具的专责 agent 并行执行。Netflix 平台团队用它同时处理来自数百个应用的构建日志；Spiral by Every 让运行在 Haiku 上的主导 agent 将写作任务委派给 Opus 子 agent，每份草稿在返回用户之前都会根据编辑规范评分。

"当 agent 知道'好'的标准是什么时，它们才能发挥最大水平。"

## PODCASTS

**Training Data — Suno's Mikey Shulman: Everyone Can Make Music Now**

**The Takeaway:** Music is the first creative medium where the act of making something — not listening to it — is itself the entertainment.

**要点：** 音乐是第一个"创作本身就是娱乐"的创意媒介，而不仅仅是聆听。

Mikey Shulman, founder and CEO of Suno, spent his career as a Harvard physics PhD researcher in quantum computing, then as an early employee at financial data company Kensho — before building the largest AI music company in the world. The transition is less strange than it sounds: Shulman's real insight was that the biggest opportunities live at the intersection of two things that don't usually go together.

Suno 的创始人兼 CEO Mikey Shulman，曾在哈佛攻读量子计算方向的物理学博士，随后加入金融数据公司 Kensho 担任早期员工，最终却创建了全球最大的 AI 音乐公司。这个转变其实没有听起来那么跳跃：Shulman 的核心洞见是，最大的机会往往藏在两个通常不会交汇的领域的交叉点上。

Before Suno, music was almost entirely a consumption medium. Out of 8 billion people, a tiny fraction made music. What Suno discovered is that on any given day, 90% of their users create something — not to share it elsewhere, but because making something is inherently fulfilling. "The creation is actually the entertaining bit. And that is the big step change."

在 Suno 出现之前，音乐几乎完全是一种消费媒介。80 亿人当中，只有极少数人在创作音乐。Suno 发现，在任意一天，90% 的用户都会创作一些东西——不是为了分享，而是因为创作本身就令人满足。"创作才是真正有趣的部分，这才是真正的范式转变。"

One of Suno's most counterintuitive engineering decisions: they threw away all musical knowledge when training their models. No 12-tone scale. No list of 200 instruments. Everything is modeled as raw sound — a continuous float-32 number sampled 48,000 times per second. The reasoning is sharp: "If you tell the model there are 12 tones, it will only ever produce those 12 tones. You will be forever limited." By treating music as pure audio waveform, Suno gets emergent behavior that no rule-based system could produce: trap with a sitar, country with 808s, entirely new microtonal structures that have no name.

Suno 最反直觉的工程决策之一：他们在训练模型时完全抛弃了所有音乐知识。没有十二音体系，没有 200 种乐器列表。一切都被建模为原始声音——以每秒 48,000 次采样的连续 float-32 数值。其背后的逻辑很清晰："如果你告诉模型只有 12 个音，它就永远只会产生这 12 个音，你将永远受限。"通过将音乐视为纯粹的音频波形，Suno 获得了任何基于规则的系统都无法产生的涌现能力：融合印度西塔琴的 trap，加入 808 鼓机的乡村音乐，以及无名的全新微分音结构。

Unlike LLMs, where throwing more compute at a problem tends to help, Shulman says music models are actually relatively small and scale isn't the primary driver of quality. The real edge comes from preference data. "The scale of preference data that we have — we wouldn't even be able to develop the techniques we are using." More users creating music → more preference signals → models that better align to what humans actually find compelling. That virtuous cycle, Shulman suggests, is harder to replicate than raw compute.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq3BIGz4vXQ

与 LLM 领域"堆算力就能提升效果"的逻辑不同，Shulman 表示音乐模型实际上相对较小，算力规模并不是质量的主要驱动因素。真正的竞争壁垒来自偏好数据。"凭借我们所拥有的偏好数据规模，我们才得以开发出现在使用的这些技术。"更多用户创作音乐 → 更多偏好信号 → 模型更能贴合人类真正喜欢的内容。Shulman 认为，这个正向循环比单纯堆砌算力更难被复制。
