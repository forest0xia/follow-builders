### Claude Blog

**New in Claude Managed Agents: dreaming, outcomes, and multiagent orchestration**

Anthropic launched three significant additions to Claude Managed Agents this week. The headline is *dreaming* — a scheduled background process that reviews past agent sessions, extracts patterns, and refines memory so agents improve over time without human babysitting. Dreaming is particularly powerful in multiagent setups where a single agent can't see cross-session patterns on its own.

*Outcomes* lets developers write a rubric for success, and a separate grader evaluates the agent's output independently — so the grader isn't anchored to the agent's own reasoning. Agents self-correct until they clear the bar. In internal benchmarks, outcomes improved task success rates by up to 10 points, with the biggest gains on the hardest problems. File generation also improved: +8.4% on .docx and +10.1% on .pptx.

*Multiagent orchestration* addresses the scale problem: when a task is too large for one agent, a lead agent breaks it into pieces and fans out to specialist subagents. All steps are traceable in the Claude Console.

Real teams are already using these features. Harvey (legal AI) saw completion rates go up ~6x with dreaming. Netflix's platform team uses multiagent orchestration to analyze logs from hundreds of builds in parallel. Spiral (a writing agent) uses outcomes to enforce Every's editorial standards, with subagents scoring drafts against a rubric before returning them to users.
https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents

**Claude Blog 最新发布：Claude Managed Agents 新增 dreaming、outcomes 与 multiagent 编排**

Anthropic 本周为 Claude Managed Agents 推出了三项重要新功能。重头戏是 *dreaming* — 一个定期在后台运行的进程，回顾过去的 agent 会话、提取规律并优化记忆，使 agent 能够随着时间推移自我改进，无需人工干预。在多 agent 场景下，dreaming 尤为强大，因为单个 agent 无法感知跨会话的规律。

### Training Data — "Suno's Mikey Shulman: Everyone Can Make Music Now"

**The Takeaway:** AI hasn't just made music creation faster — it has fundamentally changed who gets to be a creator, shifting music from a passive consumption medium to an active creative experience for everyone.

Mikey Shulman, founder and CEO of Suno, took an improbable path to building what may be the world's largest AI music company: a Harvard physics PhD focused on quantum computing, who originally thought making high-quality AI music was "a couple of orders of magnitude away" in terms of compute. He was wrong — in the best possible way.

What makes Suno's approach counterintuitive is what they deliberately *didn't* build. Rather than feeding the model musical knowledge — the 12 western tones, instrument categories, genre rules — they stripped all of that away and modeled raw audio from scratch. The constraint? A continuous float-32 waveform sampled 48,000 times per second. The result: a model unconstrained by human musical conventions, capable of producing trap with a sitar, country with 808s, or entirely new microtonal sounds that have no name yet.

"The more musical knowledge we give the model, the more constrained it will be in a bad way. We threw away everything we know about music, and let's try to do this from scratch."

Shulman's most striking data point: on any given day, 90% of Suno users are creating something — not just listening. Before Suno, making music was reserved for a tiny fraction of humanity. That shift — from consumer to creator — is the real product innovation, not the audio quality. He's explicit about this: they intentionally chose autoregression over diffusion (producing less crisp audio but full songs with narrative arcs) because telling a story matters more than sounding perfect.

On the business side: Suno is at a $300M revenue run rate, has had users sign to record deals and chart with AI-made songs, and recently settled with Warner Music in what Shulman frames not as a legal truce but as a foundation for building entirely new fan-artist products that "couldn't have existed until approximately right now." His vision for the next 12 months includes interactive concerts where audiences co-create music live with artists on stage.

The consumer AI founder question: Shulman thinks the reason so few AI companies go after consumers (vs. enterprise) is simply that it's harder and the ROI is less obvious. His counterintuitive bet is that the real unlock of AI isn't automating existing tasks — it's enabling things that were never possible before.

**Training Data — "Suno 的 Mikey Shulman：现在每个人都可以创作音乐了"**

**核心观点：** AI 不仅让音乐创作变得更快，更从根本上改变了"谁能成为创作者" — 将音乐从被动消费的媒介，变成了所有人都能参与的主动创作体验。

Suno 的创始人兼 CEO Mikey Shulman，走了一条出人意料的路来到今天：哈佛量子计算物理学博士，曾认为制作高质量 AI 音乐在算力方面"还差几个数量级" — 而他错了，以最好的方式错了。

Suno 方法论中最反直觉的一点，是他们刻意*不*做什么。他们没有向模型灌输音乐知识 — 西方音乐的 12 个音调、乐器分类、曲风规则 — 而是把这些全部抛弃，从零开始直接建模原始音频。约束条件？以每秒 4.8 万次采样的连续 float-32 波形。结果：模型不受人类音乐惯例的束缚，能够生成融合西塔琴的 trap、带 808 鼓机的乡村音乐，以及完全没有名字的全新微分音音乐。

"给模型的音乐知识越多，它就会以一种不好的方式被束缚得越紧。我们抛掉了所有关于音乐的既有认知，从零开始尝试。"

Shulman 分享了一个令人印象深刻的数据：在任何一天，90% 的 Suno 用户都在*创作*，而不只是在听。在 Suno 出现之前，音乐创作是极少数人的专利。这种从消费者到创作者的转变，才是真正的产品创新，而不是音质本身。他对此毫不含糊：他们刻意选择了自回归而非扩散模型（音频质感稍差，但能生成有叙事弧度的完整歌曲），因为讲故事比听起来完美更重要。

在商业层面：Suno 年化收入已达 3 亿美元，已有用户凭借 AI 生成的歌曲签约唱片公司并登上排行榜，近期与华纳音乐达成和解 — Shulman 将此定义为构建全新艺人–粉丝产品的基础，而非法律上的休战。他对未来 12 个月的愿景包括：观众能在舞台上与艺人实时共同创作音乐的互动演唱会。

关于消费者 AI 创始人稀缺的问题：Shulman 认为原因很简单 — 做消费者产品更难、回报也没那么显而易见。他最反直觉的判断是：AI 真正的突破不是自动化现有任务，而是实现从前根本不可能做到的事情。
