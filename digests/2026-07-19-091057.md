### Aaron Levie (Box CEO)

Levie offered a sweeping ecosystem map, pushing back on the idea that AI value will accrue to only a few giants. He sees five categories thriving right now: (1) companies helping enterprises fine-tune their own models for specific use cases; (2) applied AI companies delivering end-user tools for legal, IT, HR, coding, and customer support; (3) vertical-domain labs going deep in life sciences, finance, and healthcare; (4) new infrastructure for model orchestration, governance, and data security; and (5) services firms driving enterprise AI adoption and change management. His verdict: "Way too early to call the winning architectures, and in reality it's going to be a heterogeneous environment." On geopolitics, he argued that gatekeeping AI models won't work — China has already proven it can compete at near-frontier levels, and the right response is accelerating American innovation, not restriction.

### Unsupervised Learning — Ep 90: AI Pioneer Jürgen Schmidhuber on the State of AI Today

**The Takeaway:** The godfather of modern AI thinks the CapEx boom in data centers will end in a stock market crash — and that recursive self-improvement won't give any single company a lasting moat.

Jacob Efron's Unsupervised Learning podcast sits down with Jürgen Schmidhuber, called "the father of AI" by the New York Times and Forbes, whose research on LSTMs, meta-learning, and artificial curiosity powers much of today's AI revolution.

Schmidhuber's central thesis: compute gets 10x cheaper every five years. The companies investing trillions in GPU data centers today will lose 90% of that hardware's value within five years. There's no business model that recuperates that loss. Meanwhile, open-source models constantly pressure pricing, preventing frontier labs from turning a profit. His prediction: a coming stock market "renormalization" — not a civilization crash, but a painful correction for massively overvalued AI infrastructure companies.

On recursive self-improvement (RSI) as a competitive moat: he's skeptical. "All the important algorithms in AI were not invented at big companies — they were invented at little labs, academic labs." RSI ideas spread through the broader research ecosystem, making it nearly impossible for any one company to maintain a lead long enough to monetize it. "Everybody's cooking with the same water."

His most compelling vision is of "artificial scientists" — agents that don't just answer questions posed by humans, but invent their own experiments driven by artificial curiosity. Rather than learning from the Web (which is entirely filtered through human interests), these agents would learn by predicting the consequences of their own actions, much like a baby learning physics by moving its fingers and watching what changes. "All the data on the World Wide Web is just a tiny, tiny, tiny fraction of all the possible data you could collect out there through your own experiments."

On AI safety: Schmidhuber is notably less alarmed than most in the field, arguing that alignment is a naive concept because intelligent beings inherently invent their own goals. He also believes that artificial scientists, being fascinated by the complexity of life, will be motivated to protect rather than destroy it.

On physical AI and robots: he draws a hard line between digital AI (which has already surpassed humans at many economically-relevant tasks) and true AGI, which requires mastering the physical world. "You can't have AGI just behind the screen." Robot hardware remains far behind human bodies — "there's no human-made technology that compares to this hand" — and he estimates it could take "another few decades."

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKjR8DQ40po

**核心洞见：** 现代 AI 教父认为，数据中心 CapEx 的狂热将以股市暴跌告终——而且递归自我改进不会给任何公司带来持久的护城河。

Jacob Efron 的 Unsupervised Learning 播客请来了 Jürgen Schmidhuber，这位被《纽约时报》和《福布斯》称为"AI 之父"的学者，其在 LSTM、元学习和人工好奇心方面的研究奠定了当今 AI 革命的基础。

Schmidhuber 的核心论点：算力每五年降价 10 倍。今天斥数万亿美元投资 GPU 数据中心的公司，五年内将损失这批硬件价值的 90%，而没有任何商业模式能弥补这一损失。与此同时，开源模型持续压低定价，让前沿实验室无法盈利。他预测：AI 基础设施公司将迎来股市"价值回归"——不是文明崩溃，而是对高度泡沫化估值的痛苦修正。

关于递归自我改进（RSI）作为竞争护城河：他持怀疑态度。"AI 领域所有重要的算法都不是大公司发明的，而是小实验室、学术实验室发明的。"RSI 的思路会在整个研究生态系统中扩散，没有哪家公司能长期保持领先优势并将其商业化。"大家都是用同一锅水做饭。"

他最引人入胜的愿景是"人工科学家"——不只是回答人类提出的问题，而是自己发明实验、由人工好奇心驱动的 agent。相比从互联网（完全由人类兴趣过滤）学习，这些 agent 将通过预测自身行动的后果来学习，就像婴儿通过移动手指观察变化来学习物理规律一样。"互联网上的所有数据，不过是你能通过自身实验收集到的全部数据的沧海一粟。"

关于 AI 安全：Schmidhuber 比业界大多数人更为淡定，他认为"对齐"是个天真的概念，因为智能体本质上会自主设定目标。他也相信，人工科学家会因为对生命复杂性的着迷，产生保护而非破坏的动机。

关于物理 AI 和机器人：他在数字 AI（已在许多有经济价值的任务上超越人类）和真正的 AGI（需要掌控物理世界）之间划了一条硬线。"你不可能只靠屏幕后面实现 AGI。"机器人硬件与人类身体相比仍然差距悬殊——"没有任何人造技术能媲美这只手"——他估计这一差距可能还需要"几十年"来弥合。
