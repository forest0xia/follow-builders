**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 86: Yann LeCun on Leaving Meta, Breaking The LLM Paradigm, & Why Hinton is Wrong**

**The Takeaway:** Yann LeCun — the Turing Award winner and one of the architects of modern AI — believes that large language models, despite their usefulness, are fundamentally not a path to human-level intelligence. He left Meta to pursue a different architecture entirely, and he's willing to tell you exactly why everyone else is wrong.

LeCun has long been skeptical of the LLM paradigm, but this conversation gets into the specifics of what he thinks is missing. His argument centers on two capabilities he considers essential for any genuinely intelligent system: the ability to *predict the consequences of your own actions*, and the ability to *plan by optimization and search* — not by predicting one token or action at a time.

"LLMs do not have the ability to predict the consequences of their actions, and they do not have any planning abilities, because inference is by predicting the next token. It's not by search."

That distinction matters enormously. When you plan in the real world, you don't just output one action after another and hope for the best — you mentally simulate possible futures, weigh them, and pick the sequence most likely to achieve your goal. LLMs skip that loop entirely. Vision-language-action models (VLAs), which many robotics teams have bet on, are now being widely seen as a failure for the same reason.

What LeCun is building instead — through his new company Ami Labs — is grounded in world models and the JEPA architecture (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) he developed at Meta. The core idea: train a system to predict the *abstract* consequences of actions, not at pixel level, but at a meaningful representational level. Reality is high-dimensional, continuous, noisy, and messy. That's why understanding the physical world is vastly harder than processing language — and why he believes a fundamentally different architecture is needed.

He left Meta because the company had pivoted its entire AI organization toward chasing the LLM race, leaving exploratory research deprioritized. The JEPA work had found an audience — Mark Zuckerberg and CTO Andrew Bosworth believed in it — but the broader organization had moved on. LeCun concluded that Ami was the only way to push the work forward at the speed it deserved.

For AI practitioners and investors: LeCun isn't dismissing LLMs. He uses them. He helped build some of the first major open-source ones at Meta. But his bet is that the next decade of AI progress, especially for real-world robotics and physical systems, will come from a completely different architecture — and he's now running at it full speed.

**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 86：Yann LeCun 谈离开 Meta、打破 LLM 范式与为何 Hinton 是错的**

**核心洞见：** 图灵奖得主、现代 AI 奠基人之一 Yann LeCun 认为，尽管大型语言模型很有用，但它们在本质上并不是通往人类级别智能的路径。他离开了 Meta，转而追求一种截然不同的架构，并坦然告诉你为什么他认为其他人都走错了方向。

LeCun 对 LLM 范式的质疑由来已久，但这次对话深入到了具体细节。他的论点聚焦于他认为任何真正智能系统都不可或缺的两种能力：**预测自身行动后果的能力**，以及**通过优化和搜索进行规划的能力**——而不是逐个预测 token 或动作。

"LLM 不具备预测自身行动后果的能力，也不具备任何规划能力，因为推理过程就是预测下一个 token，而不是搜索。"

这一区别至关重要。在现实世界中规划时，你不会一个接一个地输出行动然后听天由命——你会在脑海中模拟可能的未来，权衡利弊，选择最可能实现目标的行动序列。LLM 完全跳过了这个循环。许多机器人团队押注的视觉-语言-动作模型（VLA）也因同样的原因，正被广泛认定为失败之路。

LeCun 通过他的新公司 Ami Labs 正在构建的替代方案，基于他在 Meta 开发的世界模型和 JEPA 架构（联合嵌入预测架构）。核心思路是：训练系统在**抽象层面**预测动作后果，而非像素级别，而是有意义的表征层面。现实世界是高维的、连续的、嘈杂的、混乱的。这正是理解物理世界远比处理语言困难的原因——也是他相信需要一种根本不同的架构的原因。

他离开 Meta 是因为公司已将整个 AI 组织的重心转向追赶 LLM 竞赛，探索性研究被降低了优先级。JEPA 的工作虽然获得了 Zuckerberg 和 CTO Andrew Bosworth 的认可，但整个组织已经转向了。LeCun 判断，Ami 是以应有速度推进这项工作的唯一方式。

对于 AI 从业者和投资者：LeCun 并不否认 LLM 的价值，他自己也在用，也参与构建了 Meta 最早的一批重要开源模型。但他的判断是，未来十年 AI 的重大进展——尤其是真实世界的机器人和物理系统——将来自一种完全不同的架构。而他现在正在全力冲刺。
