## PODCASTS

**No Priors — "AI for Atoms: How Periodic Labs is Revolutionizing Materials Engineering with Co-Founder Liam Fedus"**
https://www.youtube.com/@NoPriorsPodcast

**The Takeaway:** The next frontier of AI acceleration isn't software — it's atoms. Liam Fedus, co-creator of ChatGPT and former VP of Post Training at OpenAI, has left language models behind to build an AI foundation lab for the physical world at Periodic Labs.

Fedus's career arc is remarkable: physics undergrad doing dark matter research, then Google Brain in 2016–17 (he was present for the invention of the transformer, mixture of experts, and distributed training), then co-creating ChatGPT at OpenAI. He left in late 2022 convinced that the next major wave of AI impact required connecting AI systems to the physical world — not just language.

At Periodic Labs, the goal is to apply AI to materials engineering and chemistry in a closed-loop system. Unlike language models trained on the internet, Periodic's models need experimental data grounded in physical reality. Literature alone is unreliable: reported material properties in papers can span orders of magnitude, giving you a distribution rather than ground truth. Actual experiments are the only way to get reliable signal. The company uses an orchestrating language model layer that directs specialized neural nets (symmetry-aware architectures for atomic systems), reads literature, processes experimental data, and drives the next round of experiments — all in a tight loop.

On AGI, Fedus pushes back on treating intelligence as a scalar. These systems are "spiky" — world-class in one domain, easily degraded by adjacent perturbations. For software engineering, he thinks recursive self-improvement is happening now. For physical sciences, a similar closed loop is possible but slower — the outer loop is checking scaling properties and model convergence over hours on GPUs, not passing unit tests in milliseconds.

His biggest excitement beyond Periodic: robotics. A reliable, dexterous humanoid that can navigate an unstructured lab would be a massive accelerator for generating the high-volume, high-quality experimental data that Periodic needs. "If our physical world could keep up at some fraction to our digital world," he says, "I think life will just feel dramatically different."

**No Priors — "AI for Atoms: How Periodic Labs is Revolutionizing Materials Engineering with Co-Founder Liam Fedus"**
https://www.youtube.com/@NoPriorsPodcast

**核心洞见：** AI 加速的下一个前沿不是软件——而是原子。Liam Fedus，ChatGPT 的联合创造者、前 OpenAI 后训练 VP，已经离开语言模型领域，在 Periodic Labs 搭建面向物理世界的 AI 基础实验室。

Fedus 的职业轨迹令人瞩目：物理学本科从事暗物质研究，2016–17 年加入 Google Brain（他亲历了 transformer、mixture of experts 和分布式训练的诞生），随后在 OpenAI 联合创造了 ChatGPT。2022 年底，他离开 OpenAI，坚信 AI 的下一波重大影响必须将系统与物理世界相连——而不止于语言。

在 Periodic Labs，核心目标是在闭环系统中将 AI 应用于材料工程和化学领域。与基于互联网训练的语言模型不同，Periodic 的模型需要扎根于物理现实的实验数据。仅靠文献是不够的：论文中报告的材料属性可能相差数个数量级，只能给你一个分布而非真值。真实实验才是获取可靠信号的唯一途径。该公司使用一个语言模型作为编排层，指挥专用神经网络（专为原子系统设计的对称感知架构），读取文献、处理实验数据，并推动下一轮实验——一切在紧密闭环中运行。

在 AGI 问题上，Fedus 反对将智能视为标量。这些系统存在明显的"尖峰性"——在某个领域世界顶尖，却可能因相邻问题的微小扰动而大幅退化。在软件工程领域，他认为递归自我改进正在发生。在物理科学领域，类似的闭环也是可能的，但更慢——外层循环是在 GPU 上耗费数小时检验 scaling 属性和模型收敛，而不是毫秒级的单元测试通过。

他在 Periodic 之外最兴奋的领域：机器人。一个能在非结构化实验室中可靠行动、执行指令的灵巧人形机器人，将极大加速 Periodic 所需的高量、高质量实验数据生成。他说："如果物理世界能以哪怕数字世界一小部分的速度追上来，我认为生活会感觉截然不同。"
