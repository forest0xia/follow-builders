**Claude Blog: Redesigning Claude Code on desktop for parallel agents**

Anthropic shipped a major redesign of the Claude Code desktop app, built for how agentic coding actually works now: multiple tasks in flight across different repos, with the developer in the orchestrator seat rather than waiting on a single prompt.

**AI & I by Every: "The AI Sandwich: Where Humans Excel in an AI World"**

**The Takeaway:** Humans are not getting replaced by AI agents — they're becoming the bread in the sandwich. The beginning (framing the problem, ideation) and the end (polish, taste, finishing touches) must remain human. The middle can and should be automated.

**要点：** 人类不是在被 AI agent 取代，而是成为三明治中的面包。开始部分（定义问题、构思创意）和结尾部分（打磨、品味、最后润色）必须由人类完成。中间部分可以也应该被自动化。

Kieran Klaassen, GM of Quora and creator of Compound Engineering, laid out a clear framework for human-AI collaboration in conversation with Every CEO Dan Shipper. Compound Engineering began as four steps: plan, work, review, and compound (feeding learnings back into the system as stored knowledge for future runs). What Klaassen found is that the "work" phase is essentially solved — give a model a good plan and it executes reliably. Review is getting there too. The real question becomes: what's left for humans?

Quora GM、Compound Engineering 创始人 Kieran Klaassen 与 Every CEO Dan Shipper 就人机协作提出了一套清晰的框架。Compound Engineering 最初包含四个步骤：plan（规划）、work（执行）、review（审查）和 compound（将学习成果作为知识存入系统供未来使用）。但 Klaassen 发现，"work" 阶段基本上已经被解决了——给模型一个好的计划，它会可靠地执行。review 阶段也在迅速追赶。真正的问题变成了：人类还剩什么？

The answer: the front and the back. At the front, ideation and brainstorming require the deepest human presence — "the human should think hard, the LLM should support the human." At the back, after everything is built and tested, the polish phase is where humans bring their taste: clicking through the product, sensing what feels off, elevating the work beyond the merely functional. "If you ship something or do something, if you want it to be your own, you cannot fully automate everything. It's like art."

答案是：前端和后端。在前端，构思和头脑风暴需要最深度的人类参与——"人类应该努力思考，LLM 应该辅助人类"。在后端，当一切都构建和测试完毕后，打磨阶段是人类带入品味的时刻：点击体验产品，感知什么感觉不对，将作品从"能用"提升到"出色"。"如果你想让它成为你自己的，你就不能完全自动化一切。这就像艺术一样。"

Klaassen draws on his background in classical composition to make the point concrete: practicing a piece 100 times is the boring middle. But the performance — bringing something into the world for others to experience — is irreplaceable. And the moment of composing from nothing is equally special. The AI-assisted polish at the end mirrors a live performance; the ideation at the start mirrors the act of composition.

Klaassen 用他古典作曲的背景来具体阐释这一点：把一首曲子练习 100 遍是无聊的中间部分。但演奏——将作品带入世界让他人体验——是无可取代的。从零开始创作的那一刻同样特殊。AI 辅助下的最终打磨如同现场演出；开始时的构思如同作曲的行为。

The hosts also push back on the assumption that ideation will soon be fully automated too. Even simulating millions of users can only cover a fraction of the decisions a real person needs to make. Language models are fundamentally out-of-context — trained on a static slice of reality — which is why their outputs tend toward the generic unless humans tightly frame the problem at both ends. The prescription: lean into what is beautiful to you, whether that's code, design, copy, or architecture. That is where human value remains irreplaceable.

主持人还反驳了"构思阶段很快也会被完全自动化"的假设。即使模拟数百万用户，也只能覆盖真实决策者所需做出决策的一小部分。language model 在本质上是脱离当下语境的——基于现实的静态切片训练而来——这就是为什么它们的输出往往趋于通用，除非人类在两端紧密地设定问题框架。建议：深入挖掘对你来说什么是"美"——无论是代码、设计、文案还是架构。这正是人类价值无可替代之处。
