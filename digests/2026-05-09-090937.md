### AI & I by Every: "The Secrets of Claude's Platform From the Team Who Built It"

**The Takeaway:** Anthropic's platform team has found that the real blocker for AI agents isn't harness engineering or model quality — it's production infrastructure. They built Claude Managed Agents to solve exactly that wall.

Angela (Head of Product, Claude Platform) and Caitlin (Head of Engineering, Claude Platform) at Anthropic walked through the evolution of what it means to build an AI platform: from a simple completion endpoint, to stateful sessions, to a full agentic infrastructure layer. Their key insight is that as models get more capable, the right abstraction isn't a generic harness that hot-swaps models — it's a harness tightly paired with one model, tuned to extract maximum performance from it. "You probably do it at the layer of the agent — meaning the harness plus the model — rather than a really generic harness and hot-swapping everything underneath."

What surprised them most: developers assume harness engineering is the hard part. It isn't. "The infrastructure part especially is the wall that most people end up hitting." Everyone who takes a prototype to production runs into the same cluster of problems — persistent compute, transcript storage, secure sandboxing, keeping agents alive when connections drop. Claude Managed Agents was built after Anthropic's own internal teams went through these iterations enough times that they decided to solve it once, properly.

Multi-agent orchestration opens up a new design space that the team is excited about: advisor/executor splits, adversarial generator-critic pairs, swarm-style bug hunting, best-of-N sampling. Different architectures suit different tasks. Teams that treat these patterns as composable building blocks can hill-climb at the architectural level, not just at the prompt level. Real-world use cases already emerging internally: end-to-end software development platforms, automated legal review of marketing copy — thin layers on top of managed agents that non-technical teams can own and iterate on.

The longer-term vision: "We wanna experiment with directions where Claude actually gets so good at understanding itself. It figures out what model you should be using. It figures out how to spin up all the sub agents. You don't have to think so much about what kind of architectures are there because Claude is actually able to understand itself enough that it can write itself on the fly."

**The Takeaway：** Anthropic 平台团队发现，AI agent 的真正障碍不在于 harness 工程或模型质量——而在于生产环境基础设施。Claude Managed Agents 正是为击穿这堵墙而生。

Anthropic Claude 平台产品负责人 Angela 和工程负责人 Caitlin 梳理了构建 AI 平台的演进历程：从简单的 completion endpoint，到有状态会话，再到完整的 agent 基础设施层。她们的核心洞察是：随着模型能力增强，正确的抽象方式不是用通用 harness 热插拔不同模型，而是将 harness 与特定模型深度绑定，调优以榨取最佳性能。"你可能需要在 agent 这一层面来做——也就是 harness 加模型——而不是一个高度通用的 harness 在底层替换一切。"

让她们最意外的是：开发者普遍认为 harness 工程才是难点，其实不然。"基础设施部分才是大多数人最终撞上的那堵墙。"所有把原型推向生产环境的人都会遇到同一组问题——持久化计算、转录数据存储、安全沙箱、断连后保持 agent 存活。Claude Managed Agents 正是在 Anthropic 内部团队反复踩坑、受够了之后，决定一次性彻底解决这些问题的产物。

多 agent 编排打开了令团队兴奋的全新设计空间：顾问/执行者分离、对抗式生成器-评论者配对、蜂群式漏洞猎取、Best-of-N 采样。不同架构适合不同任务。把这些模式当作可组合积木的团队，可以在架构层面而非仅仅在 prompt 层面持续迭代。内部已出现的真实用例包括：端到端软件开发平台、营销文案的自动法务审核——都是在 managed agents 之上搭建的轻薄层，非技术团队也能独立拥有和迭代。

关于长远愿景："我们想探索这样一个方向：Claude 对自身的理解好到能够判断你该用哪个模型、如何启动子 agent。你不需要过多思考有哪些架构——因为 Claude 对自身的理解已经足够深入，可以即时写出适合的架构。"
