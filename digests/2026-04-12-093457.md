### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie offers a striking macro lens on AI infrastructure demand: Amazon has deployed more capital expenditure in the last three years than in its entire prior history. But his insight is what comes next — right now, AI adoption is dominated by chat tools that are relatively token-efficient. Coding agents, by contrast, use orders of magnitude more tokens, and they're only used by a small population today. As agentic AI spreads to all knowledge work, Levie argues the demand to process tokens could be "100s of times greater than we're realizing right now. Expect these charts to keep going vertical."
https://x.com/levie/status/2043192337111924930

Box CEO Aaron Levie 用一个宏观数字点破了 AI 基础设施需求的实质：亚马逊过去三年的资本开支，已经超过了它有史以来的总和。但他的洞察不止于此 —— 目前 AI 使用主要集中在消耗 token 相对较少的对话工具上，而编程 agent 的 token 消耗量则高出数个量级，目前用户群体还很小。随着 AI agent 向所有知识工作渗透，Levie 认为对 token 的处理需求"可能比我们现在意识到的大100倍以上。预计这些曲线将继续垂直上涨。"
https://x.com/levie/status/2043192337111924930

In a separate thread, Levie makes a counterintuitive case that AI will increase demand for lawyers, not reduce it. AI will cause more people to ask legal questions (requiring lawyers to verify and execute), create more exotic legal terms requiring expert review, and generate entirely new fields of AI-specific law around IP, privacy, and regulatory compliance across every industry. Historical precedent backs this up: despite the PC and internet making the legal profession far more efficient, US active attorneys grew from roughly 400,000 in 1975 to 1,375,000 in 2025. "When we make professions more efficient and automated, often demand for them goes up not down."
https://x.com/levie/status/2043051227387433144

在另一条推文中，Levie 提出了一个反直觉的观点：AI 不会减少对律师的需求，反而会增加。AI 会让更多人开始提出法律问题（从而需要律师帮助确认和执行）、催生更多复杂法律条款需要专业审阅，并在各行业产生大量关于 AI 本身的 IP、隐私和合规挑战。历史数据支持这一判断：尽管 PC 和互联网让法律行业效率大幅提升，美国执业律师数量仍从 1975 年的约 40 万增长到 2025 年的约 137.5 万。"当我们让某个行业变得更高效、更自动化时，对它的需求往往不降反升。"

### YC President & CEO Garry Tan

Garry Tan, who has been releasing open-source tools used by tens of thousands of agentic engineers, lays out his core design philosophy: "thin harness, fat skills." The idea is that the scaffolding orchestrating AI agents should be as minimal as possible, while skills — the actual capabilities and instructions — should be rich and persistent. His memorable formulation: "If your memory dies when your harness dies, you built the harness too thick. Memory is markdown. Skills are markdown. Brain is a git repo. The harness is a thin conductor — it reads the files, it doesn't own them."
https://x.com/garrytan/status/2043198783006355747

Garry Tan 基于他发布的、被数万名 agentic 工程师每天使用的开源工具，提出了一套核心设计理念："thin harness, fat skills"（瘦框架，厚技能）。意思是协调 AI agent 的骨架应尽量精简，而技能 —— 真正的能力和指令 —— 应该丰富而持久。他有一句令人印象深刻的话："如果框架挂了，你的记忆也跟着消失，那说明你的框架建得太厚了。记忆是 Markdown，技能是 Markdown，大脑是一个 git 仓库，框架只是一个瘦导体 —— 它读取文件，但不拥有它们。"
https://x.com/garrytan/status/2043198783006355747

### No Priors: "The Agentic Economy: How AI Agents Will Transform the Financial System with Circle Co-Founder and CEO Jeremy Allaire"

**The Takeaway:** AI agents are about to need their own financial infrastructure — and stablecoins, not traditional banking, are the only architecture that can actually support the scale, speed, and programmability the agentic economy demands.

Circle co-founder and CEO Jeremy Allaire has been thinking about programmable money since 2013, when he co-founded Circle on the thesis that the internet needed a protocol for dollars. That vision has now collided headfirst with the AI revolution.

Allaire's core argument: the agentic economy is being born right now. As AI agents take on more real economic work — consuming services from each other, collaborating globally, executing contracts autonomously — the existing financial system simply cannot support them. Traditional banking infrastructure was never designed to operate globally, instantly, at micro-transaction scale ($0.001 or less), or to be programmed directly by arbitrary software. "We need an infrastructure where the agents themselves can dynamically create and spin up different financial endpoints themselves."

His answer is stablecoin infrastructure built on a new blockchain called ARC, which Circle describes as an "economic operating system." The design choices are deliberately different from earlier crypto projects: validators are major regulated financial institutions (not anonymous miners), USDC is the native currency rather than a volatile gas token, and the system guarantees deterministic settlement finality in hundreds of milliseconds. Transaction costs on ARC have dropped to a millionth of a penny — which makes micro-transactions between agents economically viable for the first time.

One striking idea Allaire floats: using AI inference work itself as proof-of-work for a new kind of cryptocurrency — making the actual compute output useful rather than just burning energy as Bitcoin does.

On the ten-year horizon, Allaire gets philosophical: "We're going to have simultaneously all around the world a renegotiation of the social contract." He expects new hybrid human-AI corporate forms to emerge on-chain — governed by smart contracts, mixing human and agentic actors — and predicts these could become "the most productive corporate forms we've ever seen in economic history."
https://www.youtube.com/@NoPriorsPodcast

**核心洞察：** AI agent 即将需要属于自己的金融基础设施 —— 而能真正支撑 agentic 经济所需的规模、速度和可编程性的，只有 stablecoin，而非传统银行体系。

Circle 联合创始人兼 CEO Jeremy Allaire 从 2013 年就开始思考可编程货币。他当年联合创立 Circle，核心论点是互联网需要一个"美元协议"。这一愿景如今与 AI 革命正面碰撞。

Allaire 的核心论断：agentic 经济正在此刻诞生。随着 AI agent 承担越来越多真实经济工作 —— 相互消费服务、全球协作、自主执行合同 —— 现有金融体系根本无力支撑。传统银行基础设施从未被设计成可以跨境即时运行、支持微额交易（0.001美元甚至更低），或可被任意软件直接编程。"我们需要一种基础设施，让 agent 自己就能动态创建并启动不同的金融端点。"

他的答案是建立在新区块链 ARC 之上的 stablecoin 基础设施，Circle 将其定位为"经济操作系统"。设计选择与早期加密项目截然不同：验证节点是受监管的主要金融机构（而非匿名矿工），USDC 是原生货币而非波动性 gas 代币，系统可在数百毫秒内保证确定性结算。ARC 的交易成本已降至百万分之一美分 —— 这让 agent 之间的微额交易首次具备了经济可行性。

Allaire 还提出了一个令人眼前一亮的设想：将 AI 推理工作本身作为一种新型加密货币的工作量证明 —— 让实际的计算产出有用，而非像比特币那样纯粹消耗能源。

谈及十年愿景，Allaire 上升到了哲学层面："我们将同时在全世界范围内经历一场社会契约的重新谈判。"他预计，新型人机混合的链上企业形态将会涌现 —— 由智能合约治理，融合人类和 agent 参与者 —— 并预言这些可能成为"经济史上见过的最高效的企业形态"。
