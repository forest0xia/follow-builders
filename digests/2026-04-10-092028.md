**AI researcher and former Tesla AI Director Andrej Karpathy** posted the most viral thread of the day — a sweeping analysis of why two groups of people are so far apart in their perception of AI. **Unsupervised Learning — "Ep 84: OpenAI's Chief Scientist on Continual Learning Hype, RL Beyond Code, & Future Alignment Directions"**
https://www.youtube.com/@RedpointAI

**The Takeaway:** Continual learning isn't a neglected research frontier — it IS what scaling and RL have been pursuing all along — and the alignment optimism of OpenAI's chief scientist is sharply at odds with the doomsday narratives that dominate public discourse.

Ako Paioki, OpenAI's Chief Scientist, sits down for a wide-ranging conversation covering model timelines, agentic software, AI for science, and the long game on alignment. He's one of the people most responsible for where the field is today, and his answers are refreshingly specific.

On timelines: four months after publicly targeting "research intern-level" AI capabilities by September and a fully automated AI researcher by March 2028, Paioki says he's still on track. The explosive growth of coding tools (Codex in particular) is the clearest signal. Math benchmarks — especially IMO-level problems — served as a verifiable north star for reasoning models, and now that those milestones are being hit, attention is shifting toward real-world economic utility: models that can "materially change the economy."

On the continual learning debate: he's somewhat puzzled by the frenzy around startups pivoting to "continual learning" as if it's an overlooked problem. "The whole kind of excitement that we've had — even if you look at the titles of the GPT three paper — is that this class of models is actually capable of continual learning." In his view, RL scaling IS the path to continual learning, not an alternative to it.

On why long-horizon agents still struggle: it's mostly about connecting them to the right context, files, and infrastructure — not raw intelligence. The models need to see, to interact with environments, and eventually to inhabit physical bodies. "I think someday soon, we'll really come out robots."

On alignment: Paioki is more optimistic than ever that a concrete technical path exists — one focused on understanding how model generalization falls back on pretraining data. He's investing significantly in this line of research via OLM. "My general kind of belief that there is a research path here that actually gets us to an extremely happy world, has increased quite a lot." At the same time, his timeline to transformative models has shortened significantly — which is why urgency matters.

One of the more interesting structural insights: he's explicitly budgeting a large chunk of OpenAI's compute to "the most scalable methods" — even when that's not the most efficient short-term allocation — to ensure they don't fragment attention across too many experiments and lose sight of the main scaling curve.

**Unsupervised Learning — 第 84 集：OpenAI 首席科学家论持续学习的炒作、RL 在代码之外的应用以及 AI 对齐的未来方向**
https://www.youtube.com/@RedpointAI

**核心观点：** 持续学习并不是一个被忽视的研究前沿——它本来就是 scaling 和 RL 一直追求的目标——而 OpenAI 首席科学家对 AI 对齐的乐观态度，与主流公众话语中弥漫的末日论调形成了鲜明对比。

OpenAI 首席科学家 Ako Paioki 进行了一场涵盖模型时间线、agentic 软件、科学领域 AI 应用以及对齐长期策略的深度对谈。他是对当今 AI 领域格局贡献最大的人之一，他的回答出人意料地具体而坦率。

关于时间线：在四个月前公开宣布目标——今年9月实现"研究生实习生级"AI 能力、2028年3月实现完全自主 AI 研究员——之后，Paioki 表示他仍然按计划推进。编程工具（尤其是 Codex）的爆炸式增长是最清晰的信号。数学基准测试——尤其是 IMO 级别的题目——曾是推理模型的可验证"北极星"，如今这些里程碑正在被一一超越，关注点已经转向现实世界的经济价值：能够"实质性改变经济"的模型。

关于持续学习之争：他对创业公司纷纷转向"持续学习"，好像这是个被忽视问题的热潮，感到有些困惑。"我们一直以来的兴奋点——就算看看 GPT-3 论文的标题——正是这类模型具备持续学习的能力。"在他看来，RL scaling 本身就是通往持续学习的路径，而非其替代选项。

关于长 horizon agent 为何仍然挣扎：主要问题在于如何为 agent 接入正确的上下文、文件和基础设施——而不是原始智能的不足。模型需要"看见"、需要与环境交互，最终还需要拥有物理躯体。"我觉得很快，我们就真的会迎来机器人了。"

关于 AI 对齐：Paioki 比以往任何时候都更乐观——他相信存在一条具体可行的技术路径，核心是理解模型的泛化如何向预训练数据回落。他正通过 OLM 在这一研究方向上大力投入。"我对'存在一条研究路径能让我们走向一个极度美好世界'的信念，已经大幅增强。"与此同时，他对变革性模型出现的时间预期也大幅缩短——这正是紧迫感如此重要的原因。

一个结构性洞见值得关注：他明确将 OpenAI 大块算力预算分配给"最具可扩展性的方法"——即便这在短期内并非最高效的资源分配——以确保不会把注意力分散在太多实验上、从而迷失主要的 scaling 曲线。
