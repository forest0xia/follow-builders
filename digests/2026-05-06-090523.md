**Anthropic Engineering: Claude Code auto mode — a safer way to skip permissions**

Anthropic Engineering introduced "auto mode" for Claude Code — a new middle ground between manually approving every tool call and the risky `--dangerously-skip-permissions` flag. The core insight: users already accept 93% of permission prompts anyway, which leads to approval fatigue. Auto mode delegates approvals to a two-stage model-based classifier. Stage 1 is a fast single-token yes/no filter tuned to err on the side of blocking (8.5% false positive rate, 6.6% false negative rate on real traffic). Stage 2 fires only on flagged actions, adds chain-of-thought reasoning, and reduces FPR to 0.4% — while FNR rises to 17% on real overeager actions. A prompt-injection probe also screens all tool outputs before the agent sees them. The honest caveat from the team: a 17% false-negative rate on real overeager actions means it's not a drop-in replacement for careful human review on high-stakes infrastructure, but it is a substantial improvement over no guardrails at all.
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-auto-mode

**Anthropic Engineering：Claude Code auto mode — 一种更安全的跳过权限审批方式**

Anthropic Engineering 为 Claude Code 推出了"auto mode"——介于手动审批每个工具调用与危险的 `--dangerously-skip-permissions` 标志之间的新选择。核心洞察：用户本就接受了 93% 的权限提示，这导致了审批疲劳。Auto mode 将审批委托给一个两阶段的模型分类器。第一阶段是快速的单 token 是/否过滤器，倾向于拦截（真实流量 FPR 8.5%，FNR 6.6%）；第二阶段仅对被标记的行为触发，加入思维链推理，将 FPR 降低到 0.4%——但真实过度主动行为的 FNR 上升至 17%。此外，一个 prompt 注入探针会在 agent 看到之前扫描所有工具输出。团队坦诚的警告：17% 的漏报率意味着对于高风险基础设施，它不能完全替代人工审查，但相比完全没有防护措施，已是实质性的改进。

**Training Data — Waymo's Dmitri Dolgov: 20 Million Rides and the Road to Full Autonomy**

The Takeaway: Twenty-one years of building the same thing is not stubbornness — it's the only way to solve problems whose long tail never gets shorter no matter what technology breakthrough arrives.

Waymo co-CEO Dmitri Dolgov traces a path from Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology to the DARPA urban challenge in 2005 — where he had what he describes as a "light switch moment" — to leading one of the most consequential technology companies operating today. Waymo has now given over 20 million fully autonomous rides, with 10 million of those happening in just the last seven months.

The most counterintuitive insight from Dolgov: end-to-end AI models and structured intermediate representations are not a dichotomy. Waymo uses an end-to-end Waymo Foundation Model (multimodal, world-action, language-aligned), but augments it with structured materialized intermediate representations that enable closed-loop evaluation, richer reinforcement learning rewards, and runtime validation. "There's a massive difference between using end-to-end versus purely relying on it."

On the AV hype cycle: Dolgov sees a recurring pattern where technology breakthroughs (convolutional nets, transformers, LLMs) compress the early part of the learning curve and attract capital, but don't change the "long tail" of getting to full autonomy. The teams that survived understood the mission deeply enough not to look for silver bullets.

The safety numbers are stark: Waymo's driver is more than 13 times safer than a human driver for serious-injury-causing collisions. At their current scale of 4 million fully autonomous miles per week, that translates to preventing a serious injury every eight days. One emergent capability Dolgov describes: the LiDAR detecting the movement of a pedestrian's feet under an opaque bus, predicting they'd step into the intersection, and stopping before they appeared.

The sixth-generation Waymo Driver hardware now powers the new Waymo vehicle (OHAI), designed around the rider experience — living-room-sized interior, auto-opening sliding doors. Expansion to London and Tokyo is planned for this year.

关于自动驾驶炒作周期：Dolgov 看到一个反复出现的规律——技术突破（卷积网络、Transformer、LLM）压缩了学习曲线的前段并吸引资本涌入，但并未改变通往完全自动驾驶的"长尾"。坚持下来的团队对使命有足够深刻的理解，不去寻找速效药。
