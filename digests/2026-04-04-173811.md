**Aaron Levie** (CEO at Box)

Aaron Levie pushed back on the idea that AI agents will simply absorb human work. His argument: human cognition has hard limits on how much context one person can hold, which is exactly why orgs develop management layers. Agents today are "only as effective as the context they're provided, the tools they have access to, the human's ability to keep them on track or review their work." Until agents can perfectly self-escalate and operate without review, the mental work of managing them falls on real people. This, he argues, is why confident predictions about job elimination will prove wrong — at least for now.

**Latent Space — Marc Andreessen introspects on The Death of the Browser, Pi + OpenClaw, and Why "This Time Is Different"**

**The Takeaway:** AI's current breakthroughs aren't sudden — they're an eighty-year overnight success, and for the first time, the technology is actually working in ways that matter beyond creative novelty.

Marc Andreessen, co-founder of a16z and one of Silicon Valley's most storied investors, sat down with the Latent Space team to make the case that AI skepticism is running out of defensible ground. His framing of the moment is precise: "eighty year overnight success" — ChatGPT, reasoning models, and agents feel sudden, but they're really the delayed payoff on decades of foundational research that most people ignored. Neural networks, first theorized in 1943, were considered controversial for sixty of those years. The researchers who built the foundations often retired — or died — without ever seeing their ideas work.

Andreessen is direct about what changed: it wasn't just LLMs. It was a sequence of four real breakthroughs — language models, reasoning (with o1 and r1), agents (with OpenClaw), and now early signs of recursive self-improvement. Each one answered a different version of the skeptic's argument. Before reasoning, you could still credibly say AI was "just pattern completion." After the coding breakthrough, Linus Torvalds himself acknowledged AI had surpassed him — "that's never happened before," Andreessen notes.

On the AI winter question: he doesn't dismiss it entirely, but he calls out the asymmetry. The scaling laws are self-fulfilling predictions that motivate the investment and engineering required to sustain them. And right now, every dollar going into GPU infrastructure is turning into immediate revenue — not a speculative future bet. He draws the contrast to the dot-com collapse, which was really a telecom overbuild: companies like Global Crossing raised debt to build fiber for internet traffic that was doubling every quarter in 1995 — until it wasn't. The difference today is that the buyers are Microsoft, Amazon, Google, and Meta, not highly-levered startups. They have cash, they have revenue, and they have room to absorb the cycles.

His most interesting observation: we're running on the "sandbag version" of AI. Supply constraints mean the models we actually use are inferior to what they would be if compute were ten times cheaper. When those constraints eventually ease, even today's architectures will produce dramatically better results.

"If I were 18, this is a hundred percent what I would be spending all of my time on. This is such an incredible conceptual breakthrough."

**Latent Space — Marc Andreessen 深度访谈：浏览器之死、Pi + OpenClaw，以及"这次真的不一样"**

**核心观点：** AI 当前的突破并非横空出世，而是一场历时八十年的"一夜成功"——这一次，技术真正开始在重要的场景中起作用，不再只是创意玩具。

a16z 联合创始人、硅谷最具影响力的投资人之一 Marc Andreessen 与 Latent Space 团队深度对话，他的判断是：AI 怀疑论已经越来越难以站稳脚跟。他对当下时刻的定义非常精准："八十年的一夜成功"——ChatGPT、推理模型、agent 的出现看似突然，但其实是几十年来大多数人忽视的基础研究的迟到回报。神经网络的理论雏形早在 1943 年就已提出，但在此后六十年里一直饱受争议。许多奠基者一生都在研究这个方向，有人退休了，有人离世了，却从未亲眼看到它真正运作起来。

Andreessen 对转折点的分析很清晰：不是单靠 LLM，而是四次真实突破的接力——语言模型、推理能力（o1 和 r1）、agent（以 OpenClaw 为代表），以及现在初现端倪的递归自我改进。每一次突破都在回答怀疑者的一个具体质疑。在推理能力出现之前，你还可以说 AI "只是模式匹配"；在编程突破之后，连 Linus Torvalds 都承认 AI 已经超越了他——Andreessen 说，"这种事以前从未发生过。"

关于 AI 寒冬的问题：他没有完全否认其可能性，但他指出了一个关键的不对称性。Scaling law 是一种自我实现的预言，它会吸引维持曲线所需的投资和工程投入。而当下，每一美元的 GPU 基础设施投入都在立即转化为收入，而不是押注于未来。他将此与互联网泡沫破裂作对比——那次崩溃本质上是电信基础设施的过度建设：Global Crossing 这样的公司举债大量铺设光纤，押注互联网流量将持续每季度翻倍，直到这个假设不再成立。而今天的买家是微软、亚马逊、谷歌和 Meta，不是高杠杆的创业公司，它们有现金流、有营收、有足够的纵深来穿越周期。

他最有意思的一个观察是：我们现在用的，其实是"缩水版"的 AI。供给限制意味着当前部署的模型，远不如在算力十倍更便宜的假设情境下所能达到的水平。一旦这些瓶颈解除，即便沿用今天的架构，模型表现也将大幅提升。
