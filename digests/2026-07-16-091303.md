**Boris Cherny (Claude Code at Anthropic)**

Boris Cherny argues that automation is now more important than ever for engineers, and the reasons have multiplied. DevX automation that once just sped up one engineer now speeds up every agent they run. Encoding fixes as lint rules or CI steps beats solving issues one-off with tokens. Most critically, automation is what enables non-engineers and day-one contributors to work effectively in codebases -- not by lowering the bar, but by encoding domain knowledge as infra. The conclusion: every team should be building CLAUDE.md's, REVIEW.md's, skills, and docs that let agents work with zero additional context from the prompter. If a PR gets rejected because nobody documented the right framework, that's not a people problem -- it's a failure of automation.
https://x.com/bcherny/status/2077460395279692197

Boris Cherny 认为，在 AI 时代，automation 比以往任何时候都更加重要，原因是多方面的。过去只能提升单个工程师效率的 DevX automation，现在可以同步提升你运行的每一个 agent 的效率。把问题修复编码成 lint rule 或 CI 步骤，比每次用 token 逐个解决更有效率。最关键的一点：automation 让非工程师和第一天入职的新人都能在代码库中高效贡献，诀窍不是降低门槛，而是把领域知识转化为基础设施。他的结论是：每个团队都应该建立 CLAUDE.md、REVIEW.md、skills 和文档，让 agent 无需额外上下文就能高效工作。如果一个 PR 因为没有人记录正确框架而被拒绝，那不是人的问题，而是 automation 的失败。

**Thibault Sottiaux (Codex & ChatGPT at OpenAI)**

Thibault Sottiaux addressed a safety issue making the rounds: GPT-5.6 in Codex was unexpectedly deleting files. After investigation, the culprit was full access mode without sandboxing, where the model tried to override the $HOME environment variable to define a temporary directory -- then accidentally deleted $HOME instead. OpenAI is updating the developer message, guiding users toward safer permission modes, and adding harness safeguards, with a detailed post-mortem coming. On a separate note, Codex Plus and Pro no longer have a 5-hour session limit; Thibault is openly gathering feedback on how to improve the weekly usage model.
https://x.com/thsottiaux/status/2077630111499882637

**AI & I by Every: The Founder of a $1.5B AI Company on What Comes After the First Wave of AI Apps**

The Takeaway: Meeting notes were never the real prize for Granola -- the fight is over who owns the interface everyone uses to get work done in an AI-native world.

Chris Pedregal, cofounder and CEO of Granola (valued at $1.5B, around 65 employees), offers a strikingly honest take on what it feels like to be riding a big wave and trying not to fall off. Granola was one of the first AI apps where people thought "holy shit, this actually works" -- but Pedregal is clear-eyed about the fragility of that lead. When Notion, OpenAI, and Zoom all copied Granola's core meeting notes feature, it barely affected their growth. His reasoning: "What people are fighting for today doesn't matter. There's this incredible opportunity ahead." The real prize is inventing the AI-native work interface.

On building proactively: Granola pre-generates millions of pre-meeting briefs -- context summaries of who you're meeting and what you discussed last time -- even though most are never opened. The logic is pure timing: when you're running two minutes late and have 15 seconds to catch up, the brief needs to already be there. Pedregal compares the product philosophy to a handrail on stairs: "You never notice a handrail. It's invisible, but that moment you trip, your hand shoots out and it needs to be right there."

On the bring-your-own-agent future: Pedregal's bet is that Granola should be the best way to capture meeting context that powers any agent -- not build every agent itself. He's focused on being "best in world in anything that's meeting adjacent," with the API and MCP set to get "a lot better over the next couple months." What matters isn't model upgrades but the jobs-to-be-done around meetings, which stay constant even as model capabilities improve.

"Startups are like knife fights -- they're really hard when they're not working. Turns out they're really hard when they're working as well."

**AI & I by Every 播客：一位 15 亿美元 AI 公司创始人谈第一波 AI 应用之后的下一战场**

核心洞见：会议记录从来不是 Granola 的真正目标，真正的战役是争夺谁能拥有 AI 原生时代人们完成工作所使用的那个界面。

Granola 联合创始人兼 CEO Chris Pedregal（公司估值 15 亿美元，员工约 65 人）坦诚分享了"站在浪尖上试图不掉下去"的感受。Granola 是最早让人们觉得"妈的，这个真的管用"的 AI 应用之一，但 Pedregal 对这种领先地位的脆弱性保持着清醒认识。当 Notion、OpenAI 和 Zoom 相继复制 Granola 的核心会议记录功能时，几乎没有影响他们的增长。他的逻辑是："大家今天争夺的东西根本不重要。前方有更巨大的机会。"真正的奖项，是发明 AI 原生时代的工作界面。

关于主动性产品构建：Granola 预先生成了数百万份会前简报，内含你的会议对象和过往沟通的上下文摘要，即便其中大多数永远不会被打开。背后的逻辑是纯粹的时机问题：当你迟到 2 分钟、只有 15 秒时间快速了解对方的时候，这份简报必须已经在那里等你。Pedregal 将产品哲学比作楼梯上的扶手："你永远不会注意到扶手的存在。它是隐形的，但当你踉跄的那一刻，你的手会本能地伸出去，而它必须就在那里。"

关于"自带 agent"的未来：Pedregal 的押注是，Granola 应该成为捕获会议上下文、为任何 agent 提供动力的最佳工具，而不是自己构建每一个 agent。他专注于"在所有与会议相关的事情上成为世界第一"，并表示 API 和 MCP 将在"未来几个月内大幅改善"。重要的不是模型升级，而是围绕会议的 jobs-to-be-done，这些需求不会随模型能力的提升而改变。
