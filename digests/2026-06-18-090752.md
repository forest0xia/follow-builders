**Aaron Levie** (Box CEO)

Box CEO Aaron Levie laid out a four-part playbook for the Applied AI layer, pushing back on skeptics who dismissed it as "just a thin wrapper on the LLM." His framework: (1) build bespoke features that bridge intelligence to specific workflows, (2) act as a model router balancing frontier capability against cheaper models based on deep task-specific evals, (3) deploy field engineers and change management expertise — because enterprises can't self-implement, (4) build domain-specific GTM so you can speak the customer's language. His closing note: even if model intelligence eventually solves everything, "enterprises need help changing *today*" — and that demand alone sustains the applied layer.
https://x.com/levie/status/2067455756795039957

Box CEO Aaron Levie 提出了针对 Applied AI 层的四步竞争手册，驳斥了"只是薄薄一层 LLM wrapper"的质疑声音。他的框架：（1）构建将智能与特定工作流连接起来的定制功能；（2）充当模型路由器，基于深度任务评测在前沿能力与低成本模型之间动态切换；（3）派驻现场工程师并提供变革管理专业能力——因为企业自行实施往往力不从心；（4）建立垂直行业 GTM 体系，用客户听得懂的语言沟通。他的结语发人深省：即便模型智能"从长远来看"能解决一切，"企业今天就需要帮助来推进变革"——这一需求本身就足以支撑 Applied AI 层的价值。

**Zara Zhang** (Builder, Harvard '17)

Zara Zhang offered two sharp takes on building with AI. On writing: don't use AI for writing until you've developed your own taste and voice — the danger isn't AI itself, it's using it before you can recognize slop when you see it. "Read a lot to figure out what good looks like. Write a lot to know what your voice sounds like. Only then, use AI to help you write, but make sure it actually sounds like you." On vibe-coded apps: "Building the thing takes a day. Finding out if you'll actually use it takes a week." Most dead projects worked fine — people just never opened them. The real design challenge is building for the lazy, forgetful human, not the idealized user who completes every step.

**AI & I by Every — "GitHub's COO Explains Why AI Hasn't Replaced Developers"**

The takeaway: GitHub COO Kyle Daigle believes AI agents won't replace developers — they'll multiply them, and the next frontier is building AI that understands *you* deeply enough to complete your thoughts without being told.

Kyle Daigle has been at GitHub for 13 years, most recently as COO and now also Chief Marketing Officer for Developers at Microsoft. He sits at the center of the agent economy: GitHub saw 17 million agent-created pull requests in March alone, and is on pace for 14 billion total commits this year if growth stays linear (it won't). The story isn't mass unemployment — it's mass expansion of who counts as a developer. GitHub's legal teams, finance teams, and "knowledge workers" are now using Copilot to build little apps for themselves.

The open source maintainer crisis is real: PRs are flooding in faster than humans can review them. GitHub's answer is agentic code review that catches novel vulnerabilities, agentic merge that handles the final manual steps once a PR is ready, and maintainer controls to gate who gets to contribute what.

On the $200-to-$2,000 subscription blowup problem: Daigle sees intelligent model routing as the fix — automatically directing simple tasks to cheaper models while reserving frontier capability for genuinely hard problems. "There's a lot of times where a lot of the reasons why tokens are expensive is because we're all going and choosing our model of the day or week or hour."

His long-term bet is on personalization and memory: "Using an agent that you feel like is completing a thought for you will give you that great experience, especially if you did not have to personally codify that thought to your agent." He's already living this — his personal AI setup reads all his emails, Slack messages, and communications daily, then surfaces patterns: not what he said, but how he says it. "Humans are way more willing to take critical feedback from robots than other humans. It's less threatening."

**AI & I by Every — "GitHub's COO Explains Why AI Hasn't Replaced Developers"**

核心观点：GitHub COO Kyle Daigle 认为 AI agent 不会取代开发者——而是会倍增开发者的能力。下一个前沿，是构建真正了解"你"的 AI，让它无需你明说就能补全你的想法。

Kyle Daigle 在 GitHub 工作了 13 年，现任 COO，同时兼任 Microsoft 开发者首席营销官。他处于 agent 经济的核心位置：仅 3 月份，GitHub 就收到了 1700 万个由 agent 创建的 PR；如果增速保持线性，今年全年提交量有望达到 140 亿次（实际增速会更高）。这不是大规模失业的故事，而是"谁算开发者"定义的大规模扩展——GitHub 的法律团队、财务团队，以及各类"知识工作者"，如今都在用 Copilot 为自己构建小工具。

开源维护者危机是真实存在的：PR 涌入的速度已超过人工审核的极限。GitHub 的应对之策是：具备 agentic 能力的代码审查（能发现更多新型漏洞）、agentic 合并（处理 PR 最后的手动步骤），以及赋予维护者更多控制权来过滤贡献者。

针对"200 美元订阅膨胀成 2000 美元"的问题，Daigle 认为解法是智能模型路由——简单任务自动分配给低成本模型，真正困难的问题才调用前沿能力。"很多时候 token 贵，是因为我们每天、每周、每小时都在手动选模型。"

他的长期赌注押在个性化与记忆上："使用一个能补全你想法的 agent——尤其是你无需亲自向它描述这个想法——才能带来真正卓越的体验。"他已经活在这个未来里：他的个人 AI 设置每天读取他所有的邮件、Slack 消息和通讯记录，然后总结规律——不是复述他说了什么，而是他怎么说的。"人们比起接受来自其他人的批评，更愿意接受来自机器人的批评。没那么有压迫感。"
