**AI & I by Every — "How Anthropic Uses Claude Fable 5 With Mike Krieger"**

The Takeaway: The most capable AI models don't just accelerate existing workflows — they force a complete rethink of how work itself is organized.

Mike Krieger, head of Anthropic Labs and co-founder of Instagram, spent months with Fable before it launched publicly. His account is less a review and more a field report from someone who had to unlearn years of engineering intuition.

When Fable first became available internally, Krieger felt like "a total newbie again" — not because the model was hard to use, but because his mental model for decomposing tasks was obsolete. The shift he describes is from turn-by-turn prompting to genuine delegation: set up the right context, then trust the model to work through an overnight task, adapt when a remote service goes down, document what it couldn't complete, and pick back up when things recover.

"I will wish Claude a good night, set it up on a pretty complex task, and wake up to... it's usually done by two in the morning."

Krieger's most revealing insight is about verification. Screenshots alone aren't enough — he now feeds video captures directly to Claude, which scrubs through recordings and catches animation jank that static screenshots miss. The tight feedback loop has evolved from "watch the code compile" to "review the full screenshot gallery of every UI state."

On the future of software engineering: Krieger doesn't think it's "over," but he's clear the craft has fundamentally changed. The role that remains distinctly human is product ownership — understanding intent, holding context, and being accountable for what ships. The pure implementation layer has been handed off.

He's particularly excited about dynamic workflows — structured multi-step programs that let Fable execute complex tasks over hours or days. His example: porting an entire Python codebase to TypeScript over a weekend, with the model autonomously creating a spec, translating module by module, running adversarial tests, and flagging what it couldn't port.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XWpTgCvgYaE

**重点摘要：** 最强大的 AI 模型不仅仅是加速了现有工作流，而是迫使人们从根本上重新思考工作本身的组织方式。

Mike Krieger，Anthropic Labs 负责人兼 Instagram 联合创始人，在 Fable 公开发布前已使用数月。他的分享与其说是评测，不如说是一份来自一线的田野报告——一个不得不推翻多年工程直觉的人的真实记录。

Fable 在内部首次可用时，Krieger 说自己感觉"完全像个新手"——不是因为模型难用，而是因为他对任务拆解方式的认知已经过时了。他描述的转变，是从逐轮 prompting 变为真正意义上的"委托"：设好上下文，然后信任模型完成整晚的复杂任务——当远程服务宕机时自主应对，记录未完成事项，服务恢复后继续推进。

"我会跟 Claude 说晚安，交代一个比较复杂的任务，然后第二天醒来……通常凌晨两点就已经做完了。"

Krieger 最有价值的洞察在于验证环节。截图已经不够用——他现在直接把视频录屏喂给 Claude，让它逐帧扫描并发现动画卡顿，而这些问题在静态截图中根本看不出来。紧密的反馈循环已从"等代码编译"演变为"review 所有 UI 状态的截图全集"。

关于软件工程的未来：Krieger 不认为它"结束了"，但他明确表示，这个职业已从根本上改变。仍然属于人类独特优势的，是产品 ownership——理解意图、持有上下文、对最终交付物负责。而纯粹的实现层已经被移交出去了。

他对 dynamic workflow（动态工作流）尤为兴奋——这是一种结构化的多步程序，让 Fable 可以在数小时乃至数天内执行复杂任务。他的例子：在一个周末将整个 Python 代码库移植到 TypeScript，模型自主创建规格说明、逐模块翻译、运行对抗性测试，并标注无法移植的部分。
