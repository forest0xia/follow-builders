**Anthropic Engineering: Claude Code auto mode: a safer way to skip permissions**

Anthropic launched "auto mode" for Claude Code — a middle ground between manually approving every action and the risky `--dangerously-skip-permissions` flag. The problem it solves: users were already approving 93% of permission prompts anyway, creating approval fatigue that undermined the purpose of the prompts entirely.

Auto mode delegates approval decisions to a two-stage transcript classifier running on Sonnet 4.6. Stage 1 is a fast, cheap single-token filter tuned to err toward blocking (8.5% false-positive rate, 6.6% false-negative rate on real traffic). Only what Stage 1 flags escalates to Stage 2, which applies chain-of-thought reasoning to reduce false positives (0.4% FPR, 17% false-negative rate on real overeager actions). A separate prompt-injection probe also screens tool outputs before they reach the agent's context.

The team is candid about the 17% miss rate on real overeager actions: "Whether 17% is acceptable depends on what you're comparing against. If you are running --dangerously-skip-permissions, this is a substantial improvement." Auto mode is explicitly not a replacement for careful human review on high-stakes infrastructure — but a meaningful safety upgrade for teams running autonomous coding sessions. When the classifier blocks an action, Claude recovers and tries a safer path rather than halting the session.

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-auto-mode

**Anthropic Engineering：Claude Code auto mode：一种更安全的跳过权限审批方式**

Anthropic 为 Claude Code 推出了 "auto mode"，定位于手动批准每个操作与危险的 `--dangerously-skip-permissions` 标志之间的中间地带。它解决的问题是：用户本来就在批准 93% 的权限提示，这种审批疲劳从根本上破坏了提示存在的意义。

Auto mode 将批准决策委托给运行在 Sonnet 4.6 上的两阶段 transcript 分类器。第一阶段是快速、廉价的单 token 过滤器，偏向于拦截（假阳性率 8.5%，对真实流量的假阴性率 6.6%）。只有第一阶段标记的内容才会升级到第二阶段，后者使用 chain-of-thought 推理来减少误报（假阳性率 0.4%，对真实 "过度热情" 行为的假阴性率 17%）。另有一个 prompt injection 探针在工具输出进入 agent 上下文之前进行筛查。

团队对真实过度操作场景 17% 的漏报率坦诚以告："17% 是否可接受，取决于你与什么相比。如果你一直在用 --dangerously-skip-permissions，这已经是重大改进。"Auto mode 明确不是高风险基础设施上人工仔细审查的替代品，但对于运行自主 coding session 的团队来说，这是一次有意义的安全升级。当分类器拦截某个操作时，Claude 会自行恢复并尝试更安全的路径，而不是中断整个 session。

**Training Data: Waymo's Dmitri Dolgov: 20 Million Rides and the Road to Full Autonomy**

**The Takeaway:** Twenty years of persistence through hype cycles, technical winters, and near-impossible safety bars — Waymo's secret was never losing sight of a mission so urgent it demands relentlessness.

Waymo co-CEO Dmitri Dolgov has been building autonomous vehicles since the DARPA challenge era of 2005, long before it was fashionable. He helped build the Google Self-Driving Car project from a team of 12 people pulling all-nighters in 2009, through the AV winter when most competitors failed or quit, to today's breakout scaling: 20 million fully autonomous rides delivered, with 10 million coming in just the last seven months. Earlier this year, Waymo launched four new cities in a single day — something that took eight years the first time around.

His technical vision centers on what he calls the Waymo Foundation Model — an end-to-end multimodal world action model powering three pillars: driver, simulator, and critic. He pushes back on the "end-to-end or nothing" framing as a false dichotomy: "It's end-to-end — and then, what else?" The "what else" is structured intermediate representations that enable closed-loop evaluation, richer reinforcement learning rewards, and real-time safety validation. The results: over 170 million fully autonomous miles logged, with Waymo 13 times safer than human drivers in serious injury collisions.

One jaw-dropping emergent capability: Waymo detected a pedestrian hidden behind a bus by bouncing LiDAR signals under the bus and catching the sparse return from the person's moving feet. The system not only detected the pedestrian but predicted their trajectory — and kept everyone safe.

On navigating hype cycles: "Whenever there's a big breakthrough — convolutional nets, transformers, large language models — it reshapes the early part of the curve, but it doesn't change the long tail." The mission kept Waymo going: someone dies in a road crash every 26 seconds worldwide. At current scale, that 13x safety advantage prevents a serious injury every 8 days.
