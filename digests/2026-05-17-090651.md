**No Priors** — Pax Silica: Inside the Trump Administration's Tech Strategy with US Under Secretary of State for Economic Affairs Jacob Helberg

**The Takeaway:** The US is building a private-sector-led coalition to secure the AI supply chain — and the key insight is that America's edge has never been government infrastructure; it has always been companies that make things people actually want.

Jacob Helberg, the US Under Secretary of State for Economic Affairs, is running what amounts to a geopolitical startup from inside the State Department. Pax Silica is a 14-country economic security coalition built on a deceptively simple premise: America cannot out-Belt-and-Road China by behaving like the Chinese government. State-owned enterprises, central planning, roads to nowhere — that is Belt and Road's playbook, and it has left dozens of countries trapped in what Helberg describes as debt that converts to Chinese equity. The American answer is the opposite: put private companies in the driver's seat.

The first concrete move is a 4,000-acre "economic security zone" in the Philippines — currently State Department diplomatic property, one-third the size of Manhattan — where a two-year negotiation is underway to establish a multi-decade investment framework. The targets are the AI supply chain's less glamorous inputs: precision reducers, rare earth magnets, server motors, actuators — the thousands of components that most people don't think about when they say "AI supply chain."

What is actually rare about rare earths, Helberg explains, is not the minerals — they exist in many places — but the refining capacity, which China has cornered and heavily subsidizes. His approach: 54 critical mineral MOUs signed at a State Department summit in February, plus demand-side price negotiations to make non-Chinese sources commercially viable. "I'm incredibly confident that we will resolve the pricing issue for the minerals market before the end of this administration."

The biggest surprise of his tenure has been the Trump administration's entrepreneurial speed: "When he likes something, he wants it yesterday." Helberg calls this "Trump time" — and notes it is genuinely unusual by government standards.

"Our superpower is really our private sector and our companies," he said. "The answer has been trying to work in lockstep with our private companies and our builders to build platforms that are commercially viable and that can ultimately live outside of the government as a private service."

**No Priors** — Pax Silica：与美国国务院副国务卿 Jacob Helberg 深探特朗普政府科技战略

**核心结论：** 美国正在打造一个由私营部门主导的联盟来保障 AI 供应链安全——关键洞察在于：美国的优势从来不是政府基础设施，而是能创造出人们真正想要的产品的企业。

美国国务院经济事务副国务卿 Jacob Helberg，正在国务院内部运营着一个堪比地缘政治创业公司的项目。Pax Silica 是一个 14 国经济安全联盟，建立在一个看似简单却深刻的前提之上：美国无法通过模仿中国政府的行为方式来赢得"一带一路"式的竞争。国有企业、中央计划、通向虚无的道路——这是"一带一路"的剧本，而它已让数十个国家深陷 Helberg 所描述的那种"债务转股权"的困境。美国的答案恰恰相反：让私营企业担纲主角。

第一个具体行动是在菲律宾建立一个 4000 英亩的"经济安全区"——目前是国务院的外交财产，面积相当于曼哈顿的三分之一——目前正在进行一场为期两年的谈判，以建立一个覆盖数十年的投资框架。目标是 AI 供应链中那些不那么光鲜的输入端：精密减速器、稀土磁铁、伺服电机、执行器——那些大多数人在谈及"AI 供应链"时根本不会想到的数千个零部件。

关于稀土真正稀缺的是什么，Helberg 解释说，不是矿石本身——它们在很多地方都存在——而是提炼加工能力，这一环节已被中国高度垄断并大力补贴。他的解决路径包括：2 月在国务院峰会上签署的 54 份关键矿产谅解备忘录，以及需求侧价格谈判，旨在使非中国来源的矿产具备商业可行性。"我对于在本届政府任期内解决矿产市场的定价问题充满信心。"

他任期内最大的意外，是特朗普政府的创业精神和执行速度："他要是喜欢什么事，他希望昨天就完成。"Helberg 称之为"特朗普时间"，并指出这在政府标准下实属罕见。

"我们真正的超级能力是私营部门和我们的企业，"他说，"答案是与我们的私营企业和建造者并肩工作，共同打造商业上可行、并最终能作为私营服务独立运营的平台。"
