**Nan Yu (thenanyu on X) — Head of Product, Linear**

Linear Head of Product Nan Yu shared a hard-won design lesson from building AI-assisted project update workflows: one-shotting with agents creates "slop" because users disengage their brains. Switching to multi-turn interactive mode -- where the agent prompts the user for emphasis, priorities, and missing context -- produced dramatically better outputs. "This simple change elicits much better project updates and ensures that the user has a chance to steer rather than simply being asked to say yes to everything. A very good example of introducing friction for a net benefit." The insight cuts against the dominant "automation-first" framing of AI agents and points toward deliberate human-in-the-loop design as a quality mechanism.

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — The Neocloud Boom: State of AI Compute 2026 | Stephen Balaban**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NttU4CbyVs

**The Takeaway:** GPU compute was never a commodity, we are still underbuilding AI infrastructure, and the people who said otherwise have been wrong every single time.

Lambda Labs cofounder and CTO Stephen Balaban has watched the same wrong narrative replay for years: that GPU cloud would commoditize and neoclouds would lose their moat. His pushback is sharp -- cloud computing was never a commodity. The giants running multitrillion-dollar cloud businesses (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Oracle) are proof of that. AI cloud is simply cloud designed for the age of AI, and it layers land entitlement, data center construction, high-performance computing, software, and cloud services into a single highly integrated product.

What's actually happening with GPU pricing is subtler than headline indices suggest. Balaban points out that Bloomberg-style H100 indices can mislead because they mix short-term on-demand rates with long-term contract rates. Long-term rates are "very consistent, if not increasing" -- the apparent price decline is a mix-shift in methodology, not real deflation.

The real bottleneck today is not chips -- it's land, power, and physical shell. Getting a utility megawatt commitment, entitling the land, and sourcing the mechanical, electrical, and plumbing equipment is what rate-limits the industry. Community backlash against data centers is real, but largely driven by a desire for voice in local development decisions and by misinformation -- modern GPU deployments use closed-loop liquid cooling with dry coolers, meaning near-zero water evaporation.

Lambda's origin story earns its own spotlight: facial recognition startup, to a baseball cap with a camera in it, to a $60K bet that became a near-billion-dollar cloud business.

On hot takes: agentic workflows for non-software tasks are overhyped (no feedback loop comparable to automated testing, no tight iteration signal). Agent-based software development is underrated, because most people simply haven't tried it yet at maximum effort. His closing line: "AI won't write software -- it will become the software."

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — The Neocloud Boom: State of AI Compute 2026 | Stephen Balaban**
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NttU4CbyVs

**核心观点：** GPU 算力从来都不是商品，我们在 AI 基础设施上的建设仍然不足，而且那些持相反观点的人每一次都被证明是错的。

Lambda Labs 联合创始人兼 CTO Stephen Balaban 多年来一直在亲历同一个错误叙事的重演：GPU 云将商品化，Neocloud 将失去护城河。他的反驳直接有力——云计算从来都不是商品。那些运营万亿市值云业务的巨头（亚马逊、微软、谷歌、Oracle）就是最好的证明。AI 云只是为 AI 时代设计的云，它将土地权利、数据中心建设、高性能计算、软件和云服务整合为一个高度一体化的产品。

GPU 定价的实际情况比头条指数显示的要微妙。Balaban 指出，彭博 H100 指数可能会产生误导，因为它混合了短期按需定价和长期合同定价。长期合同价格"非常稳定，甚至还在上涨"——表面上的价格下跌只是指数方法论中的结构混淆，而非真实的价格下降。

当前真正的瓶颈不是芯片，而是土地、电力和物理外壳。获得电力公司的兆瓦承诺、完成土地权利确认、采购机械电气管道设备，才是制约行业发展的关键。社区对数据中心的反弹是真实存在的，但主要源于对本地开发决策的参与诉求和信息失真——现代 GPU 部署采用闭环液冷干冷器方案，几乎零水蒸发。

Lambda 的创业故事本身就值得单独讲述：从人脸识别初创公司，到内置摄像头的棒球帽，再到以 6 万美元赌注起家、成长为接近十亿美元规模的云业务。

关于当下的热门观点：面向非软件任务的 agentic 工作流被高估了（没有自动化测试那样的反馈回路，缺乏紧密的迭代信号）；而基于 agent 的软件开发被低估了，因为大多数人根本还没有以最大力度、使用最新模型真正尝试过。他的结语是："AI 不会去写软件——AI 本身将成为软件。"
