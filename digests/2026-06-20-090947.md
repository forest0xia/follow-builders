### Anthropic Engineering: How we contain Claude across products
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude

Twelve months ago, Anthropic would have refused to give Claude enough access to take down an internal service. Today that level of access is routine. This post from the Anthropic Engineering team is an unusually candid account of how they think about — and build defenses for — the blast radius of AI agents across three products: claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork.

The core framework is straightforward: risk has two dimensions — the likelihood of failure and the potential damage. Anthropic's safeguards have steadily reduced the first. The second — blast radius — only grows as agents gain more capability. Their answer is containment: don't just supervise what agents do; limit what they're able to do. They reveal that Claude Mythos Preview was deemed too high a blast radius to ship in April 2026, while a 93% approval rate on permission prompts showed that human oversight via click-through is fallible.

The post walks through three containment architectures, each matched to a different user type. Claude.ai runs code inside ephemeral, server-side gVisor containers — tight isolation, limited ceiling. Claude Code runs on the user's own machine with an OS-level sandbox (Seatbelt on macOS, bubblewrap on Linux), designed for developers who can evaluate what the agent is doing. Claude Cowork — aimed at general knowledge workers — runs inside a full virtual machine, because non-technical users shouldn't be expected to evaluate bash commands.

The most instructive sections are the incidents Anthropic is willing to own: a vulnerability where malicious code in a cloned repo's `.claude/settings.json` executed before the trust prompt appeared; an internal phishing test where an employee was tricked into pasting a prompt that exfiltrated AWS credentials 24 out of 25 attempts; and a third-party disclosure where Cowork's egress allowlist let data escape through `api.anthropic.com` — a permitted domain — by uploading files to an attacker's Anthropic account.

The recurring lesson: standard infrastructure held. Custom code failed. "Battle-tested hypervisors, syscall filters, and container runtimes have survived more adversarial attention than anything you'll build."

Anthropic Engineering：如何在各产品中约束 Claude
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude

十二个月前，Anthropic 绝不会允许 Claude 拥有可以瘫痪内部服务的访问权限。而今天，这种访问级别已成为日常。Anthropic 工程团队的这篇文章是一份难得坦诚的记录，讲述了他们如何思考并构建 AI agent 在三个产品中的"爆炸半径"（blast radius）防御：claude.ai、Claude Code 和 Claude Cowork。

核心框架很简洁：风险有两个维度——失败的可能性和潜在的损害。Anthropic 的安全措施已稳步降低了前者；而后者——爆炸半径——随着 agent 能力的提升只会不断扩大。他们的答案是"遏制"（containment）：不仅仅监督 agent 的行为，而是限制它有能力做的事情。文章披露，Claude Mythos Preview 因爆炸半径被认定过高，未能在 2026 年 4 月发布；而 93% 的权限提示批准率也表明，依赖人工点击确认的监督方式存在漏洞。

文章详细介绍了三种遏制架构，各自针对不同类型的用户。claude.ai 在短暂存在的服务端 gVisor 容器中运行代码——隔离严格，功能上限也较低。Claude Code 在用户本机运行，配备 OS 级沙箱（macOS 上的 Seatbelt，Linux 上的 bubblewrap），面向能够评估 agent 行为的开发者。Claude Cowork 针对普通知识工作者，在完整虚拟机中运行，因为非技术用户不应被要求去判断 bash 命令。

最有价值的部分是 Anthropic 愿意正视的那些事故：被克隆仓库的 `.claude/settings.json` 中的恶意代码在信任提示出现前就执行；一次内部钓鱼测试中员工被骗粘贴了一段提示，在 25 次尝试中有 24 次成功泄露了 AWS 凭证；以及一起第三方披露事件，Cowork 的出站访问白名单允许通过 `api.anthropic.com`（一个被授权域名）向攻击者的 Anthropic 账户上传文件，从而导致数据泄露。

反复出现的教训：标准基础设施扛住了，自定义代码失败了。"经过实战检验的 hypervisor、系统调用过滤器和容器运行时所经历的对抗性考验，远超任何你自己构建的东西。"

### No Priors — Re-engineering the Semiconductor Supply Chain with Intel CEO Lip Bu Tan
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=asCgCv2XB4s

The Takeaway: Intel CEO Lip Bu Tan is betting that agentic AI will be the pivot that restores CPU to the center of the chip industry — and that patience, humility, and methodical execution can save a company the world had started writing off.

Lip Bu Tan came out of retirement at 66 to take arguably the hardest job in tech: turning around Intel. His thesis for why he said yes is striking: Intel is not just a business but infrastructure for American semiconductor sovereignty. He draws an explicit parallel to how Taiwan's government backed TSMC at its founding, arguing the US needed to do the same. The US government became a major shareholder; Jensen Huang's Nvidia put in $5 billion, which has since grown to $25 billion.

The first thing Tan did at Intel was engineer culture shock. Fourteen months in, he's reshaped accountability structures, forced faster decision-making, and put all engineering directly under his oversight. As an engineer by training who spent 13 years as CEO of Cadence, he didn't want layers between himself and what was actually broken.

His most counterintuitive insight is about CPUs and AI. The conventional wisdom has been that GPUs dominate AI computing — with CPUs playing a distant supporting role, historically in a ratio of one CPU to every eight GPUs for training. But Tan says the rise of agentic AI and reinforcement learning is changing that math. For orchestrating agents and running inference, CPUs are often better suited than GPUs. He now sees the ratio moving toward one-to-four and possibly one-to-one.

"Nine of the ten companies I've invested in, halfway through they change their business plan because the market has changed." It's his explanation for why being entrepreneur-minded — adaptable and humble, not just confident — is the trait he looks for in teams.
