**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 84: OpenAI's Chief Scientist on Continual Learning Hype, RL Beyond Code, & Future Alignment Directions**

**The Takeaway:** OpenAI's chief scientist believes we're entering a phase where the short-term quality of AI models directly determines the pace of AI research itself — and that makes alignment work more urgent, not less.

Ako Paioki, chief scientist of OpenAI, joins Redpoint investor Jacob Efron for a wide-ranging conversation covering model timelines, reinforcement learning beyond code, AI for science, and long-term alignment.

On model timelines: Paioki is confident the pieces are mostly in place. "We're not very far from models that can work autonomously for a couple days." He distinguishes a research intern from a fully automated researcher by the specificity of instructions required — and expects that bar to keep dropping. The explosive growth of coding tools is, to him, a clear signal that the trajectory is on track.

On RL beyond code: The hard part of applying RL to medicine, law, or finance isn't just sparse rewards — it's that longer-horizon tasks face the same challenge as harder-to-evaluate ones. Paioki sees these difficulties as "coinciding," and is encouraged by early signs of scaling RL into more general domains.

On AI for science: He recounted watching OpenAI's model solve a research mathematics problem — from his own PhD field — in about an hour, a feat he estimates would take him one to two weeks. "Seeing that happen for math... really increased my feeling of urgency." He's bullish on AI making meaningful contributions to science, though he's clear this is an evolution from game-playing AI, not a sudden discontinuity.

On alignment: Chain-of-thought monitoring is a key tool precisely because reasoning traces aren't directly supervised — they can reveal genuine model motivations. Hiding CoT in products isn't just competitive strategy; it protects the integrity of this interpretability signal. He's grown more optimistic that concrete technical paths to alignment exist, while also noting his timelines to very capable models have shortened considerably.

On society: He's worried about governance of AI-powered organizations "controlled by a very small number of people" — a concern he sees as requiring policymaker involvement, not just technical solutions.

**Unsupervised Learning — Ep 84：OpenAI 首席科学家谈持续学习炒作、代码之外的 RL 与未来对齐方向**

**核心结论：** OpenAI 首席科学家认为，我们正进入一个 AI 模型的短期质量直接决定 AI 研究进度的阶段——这让对齐工作变得更紧迫，而非更宽裕。

OpenAI 首席科学家 Ako Paioki 与 Redpoint 投资人 Jacob Efron 进行了一次深度对话，涵盖模型时间线、代码之外的强化学习、AI for science 以及长期对齐问题。

关于模型时间线：Paioki 认为大部分拼图已就位。"距离模型能够自主运行几天，我们已经不远了。"他区分了"研究实习生级别"和"完全自动化研究员"的差距在于所需指令的具体程度——而他预计这个门槛会持续降低。coding 工具的爆炸式增长对他而言是一个清晰的信号，说明发展轨迹是正确的。

关于 RL 延伸至更广泛领域：将 RL 应用于医疗、法律、金融的难点不仅是稀疏奖励，更在于长时序任务与难以评估的任务面临同样的挑战。Paioki 认为这两类困难"不谋而合"，并对将 RL 扩展到通用领域的早期迹象感到鼓舞。

关于 AI for science：他讲述了 OpenAI 的模型在约一小时内解决了一道来自他自己博士研究领域的数学难题——这个问题他估计自己需要一到两周。"看到这发生在数学领域……极大地加深了我的紧迫感。"他对 AI 在科学领域做出实质性贡献持乐观态度，但强调这是从游戏 AI 的演进，而非突然的跃变。

关于对齐：思维链（CoT）监控是核心工具，正因为推理过程没有被直接监督训练，它能揭示模型的真实动机。在产品中隐藏 CoT 不只是竞争策略，更是保护这一可解释性信号的完整性。他对对齐问题的具体技术路径越来越乐观，同时也指出强大模型的时间线已大幅缩短。

关于社会影响：他担忧"由极少数人控制"的 AI 驱动组织的治理问题——这是他认为需要政策制定者介入的议题，而非仅靠技术解决。
