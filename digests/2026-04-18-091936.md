**No Priors — "Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott"**

**The Takeaway:** AI language models and enterprise workflow platforms are not competitors — they're complements. The companies that master this distinction and position themselves as the "AI control tower" will define the next decade of enterprise software.

Bill McDermott, CEO of ServiceNow and one of enterprise software's most storied executives, lays out a remarkably clear framework for why established platforms aren't just surviving the AI moment — they're positioned to win it. His core argument: language models are brilliant advisers, but they don't close the case. A model can give you a three-step plan for a compensation dispute in milliseconds. Only a workflow platform, with access to HR data, finance, legal, and compliance, can actually resolve it.

The math is clarifying. McDermott claims that replicating even a simple ServiceNow application using a language model would cost 10x more when you factor in GPU costs, tokens, and the human capital redirected to rebuilding what the platform already does. And while people forgive humans for making mistakes, "they never will forgive software for making a mistake." Probabilistic AI output, however impressive, doesn't meet the determinism that enterprise customers demand.

McDermott's other big unlock: "AI thinks, workflow acts." Language models generate recommendations; platforms execute them across complex, multi-department workflows. This isn't a rhetorical distinction — it's the competitive moat. ServiceNow now runs 85 billion workflows, handles 7 trillion transactions, and is integrating with Moveworks (agentic enterprise front door), VESA (identity), and Armis (OT security) — all in 20 days.

On the future of work, he's direct: net new headcount additions will "dramatically reduce" as agents absorb operational volume. "There'll be 2.2 billion of these agents entering the workforce in the next couple of years." The human premium will go to those who manage relationships, make judgment calls, and build trust — things agents can't replicate.

His most quotable line on the pace of change: "It is fast, but it'll never move this slow again."

**No Priors** —— 《与 ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott 对谈：AI 时代的全球组织规模化》

**核心洞察：** AI 语言模型与企业工作流平台并非竞争关系，而是互补关系。能够掌握这一区别、并将自身定位为"AI 控制塔"的公司，将主导企业软件的下一个十年。

Bill McDermott，ServiceNow CEO，企业软件领域最具传奇色彩的高管之一，为我们梳理出一套清晰的框架，说明为何成熟平台不仅能在 AI 时代存活，还能赢得主动权。他的核心论点：语言模型是出色的顾问，但它无法"结案"。模型可以在毫秒内给出一份三步走的薪资争议处理方案，但只有工作流平台才能真正整合 HR、财务、法律、合规等部门数据，将问题实际解决。

数字很说明问题。McDermott 称，若要用语言模型复制哪怕一个简单的 ServiceNow 应用，综合考虑 GPU 成本、token 费用以及从平台迁移所需的人力成本，花费将高达 10 倍。此外，人们可以原谅人犯错，"但永远不会原谅软件犯错。" 再强大的概率性 AI 输出，也无法达到企业客户对确定性的要求。

McDermott 提出的另一个关键拆解："AI 负责思考，工作流负责执行。" 语言模型生成建议，平台在跨部门的复杂流程中落地执行。这不是修辞上的区别，而是真实的竞争壁垒。ServiceNow 目前运行着 850 亿个工作流，处理 7 万亿笔交易，并在 20 天内完成了 Moveworks（agent 企业入口）、VESA（身份管理）、Armis（OT 安全）的集成。

谈及工作的未来，他直截了当：随着 agent 承接大量运营工作量，净新增岗位数量将"大幅减少"。"未来几年将有 22 亿个 agent 进入劳动力市场。" 人类的溢价将集中在那些能够管理关系、做出判断、建立信任的人身上——而这些是 agent 无法替代的。

关于变化速度，他有一句最值得记住的话："现在很快，但这已经是它最慢的时候了。"
